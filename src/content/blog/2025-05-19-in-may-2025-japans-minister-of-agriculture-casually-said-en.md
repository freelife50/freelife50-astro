<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>In May 2025, Japan's Minister of Agriculture casually said,</p>
<p>"I've never bought rice."</p>
<p>While food prices were climbing and people were using hometown tax just to afford basic groceries, this one comment triggered a viral backlash.</p>
<p>What happened to <em>"Japan as No.1"</em>?<br>Now the country is trending for having a minister who doesn't even buy rice.</p>
<p>This post takes a sarcastic but honest look at what this viral moment says about Japan today — where the real hunger might be for empathy, not just rice.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🍚 Opening Rant: The Era Where Buying Rice Makes You Impressive</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/4cef5e30d0471c5d0547323e7266440d.png" alt="" class="wp-image-864"/></figure>

<p>I buy rice through Japan's hometown tax system. You know why? <strong>Because buying it outright is too expensive these days.</strong></p>

<p>Prices keep going up, but wages don't. Every month is a careful balancing act — and I'm spending real effort researching which municipalities offer the best deal on rice donations.</p>

<p>Then Japan's Agriculture Minister said this:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"I've never bought rice. My supporters give it to me. I have more than I could ever use."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>You've <em>got</em> to be kidding me.</p>

<p><strong>Can I get my supporters to give me some too?</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🔥 The Backlash: Online Japan Reaches a Boiling Point</h2>

<p>The moment the comment was reported, social media erupted.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>"Hey, can you introduce me to your supporters?"</li>
<li>"The head of agriculture doesn't know what rice costs?"</li>
<li>"You thought that was something to brag about?"</li>
</ul>

<p>The public was furious. Actually, "furious" isn't strong enough — <strong>people were simmering at the same temperature as a good pot of rice.</strong></p>

<p>And then it got worse. He added:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"My food storage is full of all kinds of things. Some of it even has little black stones mixed in."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Let me get this straight — he <em>receives</em> rice as gifts, and his response is to complain about the quality? Not a word of gratitude. <strong>Breathtaking.</strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🧠 Getting Serious for a Moment: The Lost 30 Years — Whose Fault Is It?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/6bc72dfbe2a5957be57d1def1756e4cd.png" alt="" class="wp-image-867"/></figure>

<p>You've heard of Japan's "Lost 30 Years" — the long economic stagnation after the bubble burst, during which Japan became the one major economy where both wages and prices stubbornly refused to rise.</p>

<p>I used to think it was some kind of external pressure — foreign conspiracy, corporate greed, voter apathy. But lately, I've started to wonder:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>What if it was simply a governing class that was so desperate to be liked — to avoid controversy at all costs — that they never made the hard calls? Endless "safe" decisions. No real reform. Just treading water for three decades.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>And now we're living with that tab.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong>Japan used to be called "No. 1."</strong><br>Today, it's trending because a cabinet minister doesn't buy his own groceries.</p>

<p>At some point, you just have to laugh. Or cry. Maybe both.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">📉 Summary: Japan, Is This Really Where We Are?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/013ad5cdd5691db73fcdcccf892595b1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-868"/></figure>

<p>This whole viral moment started with one casual sentence:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"I've never bought rice."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But underneath it was something much heavier — <strong>the feeling that the people in charge simply don't understand how hard ordinary life has become.</strong></p>

<p>The exhausted housewife sighing at the price of a bag of rice.<br>The young person cutting food costs just to make rent.<br>The family stretching every yen.<br>The retiree on a fixed pension.</p>

<p>When those people hear a comment like that, of course they're going to say "Are you serious?"</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>What I wish the minister had said was this:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"I've never bought rice myself. But I know how hard things are for everyone right now. Tonight I'll eat the rice my supporters gave me, and I'll think carefully about how to earn the trust you're placing in me."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>That would have been something.</p>
