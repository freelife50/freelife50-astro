<h2>🌐 English Section: When a Minister's Words Reveal a Nation's Wounds</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/4cef5e30d0471c5d0547323e7266440d.png" alt="" class="wp-image-864"/></figure>

<p>In May 2025, Japan’s Minister of Agriculture made a statement that shook the internet:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>“I’ve never bought rice. We have so much, it’s practically for sale in our pantry.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p>He said this during a political speech—while food prices were surging, and many families were cutting costs just to get by.</p>

<p>People didn’t laugh. They raged.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>🍚 Why It Went Viral</h3>

<p>In Japan, rice isn’t just a food. It’s a symbol of daily life, family meals, and national identity.</p>

<p>To hear a top official casually say he’s never bought it? That hit hard.</p>

<p>Especially now, when:</p>

<p>Online reactions exploded:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>“You’re in charge of food policy and you don’t buy food?”<br>“Can your ‘supporters’ send me rice too?”<br>“This country’s cooked.”</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>💸 Japan’s Long, Slow Decline</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/6bc72dfbe2a5957be57d1def1756e4cd.png" alt="" class="wp-image-867"/></figure>

<p>For over 30 years, Japan has been stuck.</p>

<p>Wages frozen. Growth stalled.<br>We used to hear <em>“Japan as No.1”</em> in the 1980s. Now?</p>

<p>It’s more like <em>“Japan as nostalgic.”</em></p>

<p>We once blamed outside forces, global conspiracies, even AI.<br>But maybe... it's simpler than that.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Maybe politicians just played it safe—trying to stay popular, avoiding real reforms, and kicking the can down the road.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>And now, the can’s hit the wall.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>🥢 The Real Taste of This Moment</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/013ad5cdd5691db73fcdcccf892595b1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-868"/></figure>

<p>This story isn’t just about one ridiculous comment.<br>It’s about disconnect. About leaders who don’t feel what everyday people feel.</p>

<p>When a minister brags about never buying rice,<br>while a single parent is comparing rice prices at the store…</p>

<p><strong>That’s the gap.</strong><br>That’s the fuel for frustration, memes, and yes—hope for change.</p>
