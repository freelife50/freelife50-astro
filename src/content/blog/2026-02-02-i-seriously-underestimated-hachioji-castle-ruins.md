<p><strong>Estimated reading time:</strong><br>About 8 minutes</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong>Who This Is For</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>People who think Hachioji Castle Ruins are just “a castle park for a casual stroll”</li>

<li>Those who choose low mountains assuming it’s an extension of Mt. Takao</li>

<li>Anyone in their 50s who has started thinking about safety, knees, and getting home before dark</li>

<li>People who trust real, on-the-ground experience over neat desk-top summaries</li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong>Conclusion</strong></p>

<p>Hachioji Castle Ruins are kind <strong>only to those who prepare</strong>.<br>(It’s far more serious mountain walking than most people expect.)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">【Before Anything Else】The Most Important Safety Information — Read This Part Alone If You Must</h2>

<p>This article is based on <strong>firsthand, time-stamped experience</strong> from my actual walk on <strong>February 1, 2026</strong>.</p>

<p>To be clear:<br>Hachioji Castle Ruins may look like a tourist spot,<br>but once you climb higher, <strong>it is undeniably a real mountain</strong>.<br>Carelessness here can be dangerous.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Distance, Time, and Pace (Based on Actual Feel)</h3>

<p><strong>Route:</strong><br>Parking lot → Castle ruins walk → Mountain trail → Fujimidai → Main keep &amp; Hachioji Shrine → Loop descent</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Distance: approx. <strong>5.5–6 km</strong></li>

<li>Total time: about <strong>2 hours 40 minutes</strong> (including breaks and scenery)</li>

<li>Felt pace: around <strong>2 km/h</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>It’s not that you <em>can’t</em> walk faster —<br>there are sections where it’s <strong>unsafe to do so</strong>.<br>That’s the honest truth.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"> Footing &amp; Gear (The Biggest Misconception)</h3>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2419.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3111"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2452.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3112"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2473.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3113"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Almost no paved paths</li>

<li>Loose rocks, narrow trails, steep drop-offs</li>

<li>Multiple sections with <strong>yellow safety ropes</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>👉 <strong>Proper hiking boots are strongly recommended.</strong><br>Sneakers put your ankles at real risk here.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Asiatic Black Bears (Sightings in 2025–2026)</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Sightings reported even near the facility entrance</li>

<li>Possibly younger individuals</li>

<li><strong>Bear bells are essential</strong> — start ringing them from the parking lot</li>

<li>For solo hikers, a small radio is also helpful</li>

<li>Avoid early morning and late afternoon</li>
</ul>

<p>You won’t necessarily walk back at the same pace you went up.<br>Getting locked in because you took your time is <strong>not funny</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Bus Access (Weekday &amp; Return Traps)</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Weekends/holidays:</strong> Direct buses from Takao Station</li>

<li><strong>Weekdays:</strong> No direct bus — walk 15–20 minutes from “Reien-mae”</li>

<li>Even on weekends, return buses are limited</li>
</ul>

<p>Miss the last one, and you’ll be walking a dark road past a cemetery.<br>This part deserves real caution.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Toilets, Water, Shops (Clear This Up)</h3>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2422.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3116"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2412.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3117"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2478.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3118"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>No water sources / no shops</strong> — bring everything</li>

<li>Toilets confirmed at <strong>three locations</strong>:<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Near the parking lot teahouse</li>

<li>At the guidance / information facility</li>

<li>Near Matsukimagari and Hachioji Shrine (upper mountain)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<p>That said, <strong>do not plan your hike assuming toilets on the ridgeline</strong>.<br>It’s safest to go before climbing.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">A Bit of History</h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/IMG_2416.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3141"/></figure>

<p>Built by <strong>Hojo Ujiteru</strong> of the Later Hojo clan.<br>Fell in 1590 during Toyotomi Hideyoshi’s Odawara campaign.</p>

<p>Around Goshuden ruins and the nearby waterfall,<br>there are stories of many lives lost.</p>

<p>I can’t verify every historical detail.<br>But this is not a place to treat as “Instagram scenery.”<br>Standing there, you feel the weight of it.</p>
