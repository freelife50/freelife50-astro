<h3>🚗 Chapter 2: What Is Chigasaki Satoyama Park?</h3>

<p>Located in inland Chigasaki, this prefectural park is a model of Japan’s traditional satoyama — where people once lived in harmony with the forest and fields.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>🕰️ Open: Always (facilities may vary)</li>

<li>💴 Admission: Free</li>

<li>🐶 Pet-friendly: Yes (on leash)</li>
</ul>

<p>📍 <strong>Access:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>By car: About 15 minutes from the Kan-etsu Expressway “Samukawa North IC”</li>

<li>By bus: From JR Chigasaki Station to “Serizawa Entrance” stop, then walk 5 minutes</li>
</ul>

<p>🅿️ <strong>Parking:</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>🌍 <strong>ENGLISH SUMMARY</strong><br>Easy to access, easy to love.<br>The park is welcoming to both people and pets — just bring your leash and a little curiosity.</p>

<h3>⛩️ Chapter 4: The Sacred Forest and “Koshikake Jinja”</h3>

<p>In the north corner of the park, nestled in a forest, lies Koshikake Shrine.<br>It’s named after a legend where <em>Yamato Takeru</em>, a legendary warrior, rested on a stone here — that stone is still there today, now considered sacred.</p>

<p>Nearby stands "Tani-no-ie", a traditional Japanese home, complete with a hearth and a sunlit wooden floor.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>🌍 <strong>ENGLISH SUMMARY</strong><br>It wasn’t a big shrine. No crowds, no tourists.<br>Just trees and stone and quiet — and I felt like I had stepped into a time before noise.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>🍖 Chapter 5: BBQ Under the Blue Sky</h3>

<p>The park offers a well-kept BBQ area, bookable at the main building.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>🍽️ Bring your own tools and food</li>

<li>🕙 Open from 10:00–16:00</li>

<li>👨‍👩‍👧 Family- and dog-friendly (grass nearby)</li>
</ul>

<p>It’s perfect for spending the whole day — nature walk in the morning, BBQ lunch after.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>🌍 <strong>ENGLISH SUMMARY</strong><br>A walk, a fire, and a full belly — what more could a day need?</p>

<h3>❓ Chapter 6: Frequently Asked Questions</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Can I bring my dog?</strong> → Yes, on a leash.</li>

<li><strong>Are there bathrooms?</strong> → Several, including accessible ones.</li>

<li><strong>How long does it take to explore?</strong> → 1–2 hours for a walk, half-day if you BBQ.</li>

<li><strong>Is there food in the park?</strong> → No shops. Bring snacks and water.</li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>🌍 <strong>ENGLISH SUMMARY</strong><br>Bring what you need, but don’t overthink it.<br>This park gives back more than it asks.</p>
