<p>This year marks 80 years since the end of World War II. I couldn't run — but maybe that's why I noticed what I would've missed otherwise. Here's a quiet reflection on peace, seen not at full speed, but step by step.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>The Morning I Couldn't Run</h3>

<p>Hello, I'm Hiroshi.</p>

<p>Today, I was supposed to run in the East Japan International Friendship Marathon. Yes… <em>supposed to</em>.</p>

<p>But that morning, my lower back decided to rebel — big time.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p><em>"Nope, you're not running today."</em></p></blockquote>

<p>So instead of running, I switched to cheering and strolling mode. While wondering, <em>"Why did I even come here?"</em> I decided to make the most of it by exploring the base on foot.</p>

<h3>Is This America... or Japan?</h3>

<p>The moment I entered the base, it felt completely foreign. American soldiers, hula dancing, English announcements everywhere. <em>"Wow, feels like I'm on a trip to the US!"</em> I thought.</p>

<p>But then... I spotted a <strong>Shinto shrine</strong>.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p><em>"Wait, why is there a shrine here?"</em></p></blockquote>

<p>At that moment, I remembered — this land was once Japan. Seeing that shrine standing quietly amid the American atmosphere sent a strange but meaningful shiver through me.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>80 Years After the War — What I Realized by Slowing Down</h3>

<p>As I walked through the base, I was wrapped in a strange feeling — <em>"Is this Japan or America?"</em> Children played under the blue sky, surrounded by a blend of American and Japanese culture.</p>

<p>I realized once again that for 80 years, Japan has maintained peace without war. Some call it "peace complacency," but I felt deeply grateful for this ordinary, peaceful life where people can smile freely.</p>

<h3>Can We Leave a Better Japan for the Future?</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/PXL_20250427_024631523-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-238" style="width:659px;height:auto"/></figure>

<p>But then, a worry crossed my mind.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p><em>"Can we really leave a better Japan for future generations?"</em></p></blockquote>

<p>In these unstable times, it's hard to see hope for the future. Still, I want to believe. Even small actions — like sharing these thoughts — might plant a seed of hope in someone's heart. That's why I'll keep sharing the value of peace and the blessings of everyday life.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>So... What Was I Even Doing There?</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/PXL_20250427_034946728.MP_-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-237" style="width:334px;height:auto"/></figure>

<p>While reflecting on all this, reality hit me — <em>back pain</em>. I found a free mobile chiropractic booth and got my back massaged. Before leaving, I booked a proper appointment for tomorrow.</p>

<p>And once again, I muttered to myself:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p><em>"So... what was I even doing there?"</em></p></blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3>Final Thoughts</h3>

<p>I couldn't run, but by slowing down, I saw things I would've missed at full speed. Health and peace — never take them for granted.</p>

<p>I'll keep being thankful and doing what I can, step by step, for the future.</p>

<p>...But first, I need to fix my back. 😄</p>
