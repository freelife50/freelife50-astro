<p><strong><em>Just 15 minutes from the parking lot to the summit. Fall colors, quiet trails, and a touch of history.</em></strong></p>

<p><strong>Visited:</strong> November 23, 2025 (Peak Autumn Colors)<br><strong>Reading Time:</strong> ~11–12 minutes<br><strong>Difficulty:</strong> ★☆☆☆☆ (Sneakers OK / Beginner &amp; Dog-Friendly)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong><span class="fz-24px">📌 Who This Hike Is Perfect For</span></strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>You want a mountain with “just enough challenge” — <em>not</em> a punishing climb<br></li>

<li>You want to enjoy autumn leaves at a slow, relaxed pace<br></li>

<li>You prefer a short-up, short-down, time-efficient hike<br></li>

<li>You’re hiking with a dog or family and want a safe trail<br></li>

<li>You need free parking + a restroom<br></li>

<li>You want a low mountain but still want beautiful views<br></li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong><span class="fz-24px">💡 Conclusion:</span></strong></p>

<p><strong><em><span class="fz-24px">For people in their 50s, this small mountain offers big comfort.</span></em></strong></p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251123_031239433.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2791"/></figure>

<p>Mt. Zukkō delivers that perfect balance of<br>“Don’t push too hard” × “Still want real nature.”</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Just 15 minutes from the parking lot to the summit<br></li>

<li>Well-maintained trails<br></li>

<li>Surprisingly beautiful autumn colors<br></li>

<li>Clear views of the Tanzawa mountain range<br></li>

<li>A small mountain with a surprisingly deep history<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Even after work… even on a low-energy morning…<br>This place feels like a <strong>reward</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⭐Why I Chose This Mountain That Day</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251123_034829286.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2792"/></figure>

<p>Once you hit your 50s, there are days when you don’t want a full-on hike.<br>You just want to <em>breathe some autumn air.</em></p>

<p>That day was exactly that.<br>I wanted the colors, the fresh air…<br>but not the exhaustion.</p>

<p>Mt. Zukkō turned out to be the perfect answer.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⭐ The Hike Was My Wife’s Idea</strong></h2>

<p>It was the morning of November 23rd.<br>I was frozen at my desk, thinking:</p>

<p><em>“I’ve got no blog ideas today…”</em></p>

<p>My wife glanced over and casually said:<br><strong>“Why don’t we try Mt. Zukkō?”</strong></p>

<p>I hadn’t heard the name before (it’s pronounced <em>Zukkō</em>).<br>But a mountain means good blog material, and our Shiba dog Momiji can join.</p>

<p>So off we went—<br>and it turned out to be the best idea of the week.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🚻 Restroom</strong></h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Exterior looks clean<br></li>

<li>Comfortable for a low mountain trail<br></li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🐕 Dog-Friendly Notes (Momiji’s Report)</strong></h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Narrow spots exist but no dangerous areas<br></li>

<li>The left route has more stairs<br></li>

<li>No ticks or leeches in November<br></li>

<li>Momiji walked happily the whole way<br></li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🚉 rain Access</strong></h3>

<p>From <strong>Odakyu Line – Shibusawa Station</strong>, you can walk to the trailhead.<br>Popular among locals for light hiking and daily walks.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⭐ Two Main Routes (We Picked the Left One)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251123_034317939.MP_.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2796"/></figure>

<p><strong>Left:</strong> Toward summit<br><strong>Right:</strong> Toward viewpoint</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🌿 Left Route (Our Route)</strong></h3>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>📝 Why the Elevation Has Two Numbers</strong></h3>

<p>You’ll see two heights:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>203 m</strong> = triangulation point (survey marker)<br></li>

<li><strong>303 m</strong> = highest natural point<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Survey markers are placed where easy—not always on the highest land.<br>Fuji is one of the rare cases where everything lines up neatly.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⭐A Mountain Full of History</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251123_034857239.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2801"/></figure>

<p>For such a small mountain, the stories are rich.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Name origin: round shape like a “head”<br></li>

<li>Old travel road: <em>Yagurasawa Ōkan</em><em><br></em></li>

<li>Princess legend connected to beacon fires<br></li>

<li>Kobo Daishi’s sacred spring “Suzuri-mizu”<br></li>

<li>Akiba Shrine dedicated to the Fire God<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Walking there made me think:<br><em>“People have walked this small mountain with meaning for centuries.”</em><em><br></em> My steps naturally became a little more careful.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>📍 Basic Information </strong></h2>

<p><strong>Mt. Zukkō (頭高山 / Zukkō-yama)</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Location: Hadano City, Kanagawa<br></li>

<li>Elevation: 203.4 m (survey point) / ~303 m (highest point)<br></li>

<li>Hiking Time: ~15 min from parking lot<br></li>

<li>Difficulty: Beginner<br></li>

<li>Best Seasons: Spring (double cherry blossoms), Autumn (fall colors)<br></li>

<li>Parking: Free — search “頭高山駐車場 / Zukkō-yama Parking”</li>
</ul>
