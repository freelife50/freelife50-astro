<!-- wp:paragraph -->

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I don’t have a cat, but I do live with a Shiba Inu named Momiji.<br>And while she lay beside me, quietly sleeping,<br>I found myself wondering—what does it really mean to protect someone?</p>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第1章：命を守るって、どういうことなんやろな</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>5月25日は「保護猫の日」らしい。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>うちは猫やないけど、柴犬のもみじと暮らしてる。<br>でも、“守る”ということを考えたとき、<br>猫や犬の種類なんて、関係ない気がした。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>子どもの頃、犬を飼ったことはなかった。<br>それやのに、記憶の奥に――<br>でっかい犬の背中にまたがってたような、自分の姿がある。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>たぶんあれは野良犬やったんやと思う。<br>僕が子どもやったから、実際よりも大きく見えたんやろな。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>あの背中のぬくもり、妙によう覚えてる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 1 – What does it mean to protect a life?</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>On Japan’s Cat Protection Day, I found myself reflecting on this question—not just about cats, but about all the lives we choose to care for.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I live with a Shiba Inu named Momiji, not a cat, but the kind of love we give doesn’t depend on species.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Somewhere deep in my childhood memories, I still feel the warmth of a big dog’s back.<br>I probably just imagined it larger than life—but the comfort it gave me was real.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Maybe the feeling of “protection” starts there:<br>in a quiet memory, long before we know what responsibility really means.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第2章：野良猫は、まだそこにおる</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>昭和50年代。まだ町には野良犬がいた。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今では、野良犬なんてまったく見かけへん。<br>いなくなったことが、まるで最初から「いなかった」かのような時代。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でも、野良猫は――まだそこにおる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>なんでなんやろ？って、ふと思った。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>犬は、狂犬病のワクチンや登録が義務づけられてて、<br>首輪がなかったら「野良や」ってすぐ分かる。<br>でも猫には、そういうルールがない。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>それに、猫は吠えへんし、近所迷惑にもなりにくい。<br>たとえ野良でも、「かわいいなあ」と思われて、そのまま見過ごされることも多い。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>いつのまにか、「誰のでもない命」が、当たり前にそこに居場所を持ってしまってるんかもしれへん。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>野良猫は、“見逃されてきた命”なんやと思う。<br>誰にも引き受けられへんかった命の続きが、そこにある。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>僕らは、見えてるのに見えへんふりをしてる命と、どう向き合ったらええんやろな――。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 2 – Stray Cats Are Still With Us</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Back in the 1970s, it wasn’t uncommon to see stray dogs in town.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These days, they’ve disappeared so completely, it’s like they were never there to begin with.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But stray cats? They’re still with us.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I found myself wondering—why is that?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Dogs are required by law to be registered and vaccinated for rabies.<br>No collar means they’re clearly seen as “strays.”<br>But cats don’t have the same system.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>They don’t bark, they don’t disturb neighbors.<br>Even if they’re strays, people often just smile and think, “Oh, how cute.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And so, these unclaimed lives are quietly allowed to stay—unnoticed, yet present.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Maybe stray cats are lives that were overlooked.<br>They’re the continuation of lives no one ever truly took in.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So I ask again:<br><strong>How are we supposed to face the lives we see—but pretend not to?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第3章：祖父が選んだ“命の判断”</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>僕の親から、昔こんな話を聞いたことがある。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>家で飼っていた犬が出産して、子犬が生まれた。<br>でも育てきれんから、祖父が石で頭を叩いて殺したんやって。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今の感覚では考えられへん話。<br>でも当時は、それが「家族を守るための選択肢」やった。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>放したら野犬になって危ない。<br>飼えない命を放置すれば、もっと不幸になる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>昔の人は、命を軽く扱ってたんやなくて、<br>「生きるために仕方なく選ぶ」という重さと向き合ってたんやろう。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 3 – The Decision My Grandfather Made</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>My parents once told me a story from the past.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Our family dog had given birth to puppies,<br>but they couldn’t raise them all—so my grandfather killed them with a stone.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It sounds unthinkable now.<br>But back then, it was considered a way to protect the family.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Letting them go meant they’d become strays.<br>Abandoning them would’ve led to more suffering.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People in those days didn’t take life lightly.<br>They were simply forced to make hard choices—out of survival, not cruelty.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1071,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->

<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第4章：“保護”という言葉のうしろ側で</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>ちなみに、「保護猫の日」は2020年に“ねこ検定実行委員会”が制定した記念日や。<br>日本では、年間およそ2万匹以上の猫が行政の施設で殺処分されてるという。<br>そのほとんどが、飼い主に捨てられたり、野良として生まれた命や。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今は、「保護犬」や「保護猫」って言葉がある。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>飼えなくなった動物たちは、保健所やセンターに持ち込まれて、<br>新しい家族を探す。誰かに“救われる”ことを待ってる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でも実際には、処分されてしまう命も多い。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>命の“終わり”は、保健所の奥のほうや、誰も見ない場所にあるんやろな。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>スーパーで買う肉と同じや。<br>誰かが血を見て、殺して、きれいにパックされたものだけが、僕らの目に届く。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>命の終わりを、自分の目で見ることがなくなった。<br>それと引きかえに、命の重みも少しずつ薄まってるんかもしれへん。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 4 – Behind the Word “Protection”</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By the way, “Cat Protection Day” was established in 2020 by the Japan Cat Certification Committee.<br>In Japan, over 20,000 cats are euthanized each year in government shelters.<br>Most of them were abandoned by owners—or born as strays.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Today, we hear words like “rescued dog” or “rescued cat.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Animals people can no longer care for are brought to shelters or centers,<br>waiting to be adopted by someone new—waiting to be saved.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But the truth is, many of them aren’t saved at all.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The end of their lives happens somewhere deep inside a shelter,<br>or in a place no one ever sees.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s like the meat we buy at the supermarket.<br>Someone else does the killing, someone else sees the blood.<br>We just get the clean, packaged version.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And maybe that’s why—we no longer see the weight of life as clearly as we used to.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第5章：最後まで見届けること</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1069,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/PXL_20250503_054027790-1-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-1069"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>命を迎えるだけやなくて、<br>その命の終わりにちゃんと立ち会い、受け入れること。<br>それが、僕らにできる“最後の責任”なんやと思う。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>もしかしたら――<br>誰かの命にちゃんと立ち会うことで、<br>僕らは“命と生きることの意味”を少しだけ実感できるんかもしれへん。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>だから今日も、もみじの寝顔を見ながら、そっと思う。<br>この命と、ちゃんと最後まで一緒におらなあかんなって。<br>前に飼っていたサクラと、同じように。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 5 – Seeing It Through to the End</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s not just about welcoming a life.<br>It’s also about being there at the end—witnessing it, accepting it.<br>That might be our final responsibility.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Maybe… it’s by standing beside someone in their last moments<br>that we truly begin to understand what life means.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s why, when I look at Momiji sleeping beside me, I remind myself:<br>I need to stay with her, all the way to the end.<br>Just like I did with Sakura before her.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🐾 第6章：サクラの涙、もみじのぬくもり</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>サクラを飼っていたときは、いつか死ぬなんて、あんまり実感できてへんかった。<br>でも、ほんまにその日が来たとき――<br>あまりのショックで、誰にも見られへんようにトイレで泣いた。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>そのとき初めて、「命って、ほんまに終わるんやな」って思った。<br>だからこそ今は、もみじの命に、ちゃんと最後まで寄り添いたいと思ってる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>サクラのときは、初めてやったから、しつけとか気にして、ちょっと厳しくしてたかもしれへん。<br>でも今は、もみじのことをめっちゃ甘やかしてる。<br>撫でて、話しかけて、一緒におる時間を大切にしてるんや。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>いつも一緒にいてくれて、ほんまにありがとう。<br>それだけで、今日という日が少しあたたかくなる気がする。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🌱 Chapter 6 – Sakura’s Tears, and the Warmth of Momiji</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I was raising Sakura, I didn’t really believe she’d die one day.<br>But when that day finally came, the shock was too much.<br>I ended up crying in the bathroom—where no one could see me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That was the first time I truly realized:<br>Life really does come to an end.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And that’s why now, I want to stay close to Momiji’s life until the very end.<br>To be there for her, all the way through.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Back then with Sakura, I worried too much about discipline.<br>I might’ve been a little too strict because it was all so new to me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But now—with Momiji—I spoil her.<br>I talk to her. I pet her.<br>And I try to treasure every moment we share.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thank you, Momiji, for being with me.<br>Just that alone makes this ordinary day feel a little warmer.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Through my days with Momiji,<br>I’m finally beginning to understand what it means to take on a life—not just love it, but truly carry it.</p>
