<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>My Son’s Choice: A Weapon to Shape His Dream</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2333,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-25T20_34_21.291Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2333"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>My son works as a graphic designer at a game company.<br>Even after work, he sits at his desk drawing; on weekends, he keeps sketching, even preparing submissions for competitions.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“The old Mac heats up too quickly,” he said with a grin.</strong><strong><br></strong> Fair enough—but still, a <strong>$3,500 MacBook Pro</strong>?!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Then I remembered: he has a dream of writing and illustrating children’s books one day.<br>That laptop isn’t just a tool. <strong>For him, it’s a weapon to shape his dream.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Where $3,500 Went: My Waste vs. His Investment</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2338,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-25T20_36_37.819Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2338"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Back in my youth, <strong>$3,500 would have gone into a car, a motorbike, or simply nights of drinking and smoking—pure consumption.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>My son, however, used $3,500 as an <strong>investment.</strong><strong><br></strong> <strong>The same money, two entirely different meanings.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Gen Z Values and Work: The Company as a “Stopover”</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2341,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-25T20_45_39.968Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2341"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Gen Z grew up as digital natives. <strong>They value efficiency, yet don’t hesitate to spend on what matters most—their individuality and their dreams.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“I don’t know if I’ll stay at this company forever,” my son once said.<br>What?! <strong>For us, just landing a job was the goal of life itself.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But his $3,500 makes sense: it’s an investment in himself.<br><strong>Maybe his approach is actually the wiser one.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The </strong><strong><em>Dankai Junior</em></strong><strong> Struggle: Living Defensively in the “Ice Age”</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2349,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-25T20_57_39.669Z-1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2349"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For us, the story was different.<br><strong>Our generation came of age during Japan’s “employment ice age.”</strong><strong><br></strong> Jobs were scarce, competition was fierce, and survival meant grabbing any position you could find—dreams were a luxury.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Many ended up in unstable work. <strong>We didn’t have the space to ask what was truly valuable.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion: A Way of Life Reflected in Money</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2354,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-25T21_01_07.032Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2354"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Today’s youth spend money on what they </strong><strong><em>truly</em></strong><strong> want.</strong><strong><br></strong> They invest in value—and in doing so, <strong>they invest in life itself.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>We, on the other hand, were too busy surviving.</strong><strong><br></strong> Maybe that’s why my son’s choices shine so brightly to me.</p>
