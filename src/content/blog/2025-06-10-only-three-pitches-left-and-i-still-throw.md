<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>A Story of Midlife, Hope, and a Manga That Helped Me Begin Again</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span class="bold-red"><strong>🕐 Estimated reading time: about 6 minutes</strong></span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>⛳ Purpose</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This blog post explores what we can still do — even when time is limited — through the lens of the manga <em>Thank You Pitch</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As a man in his 50s, I found myself deeply moved by a story about a pitcher who can only throw three balls a day. It reminded me that, even with fewer chances left in life, there's still meaning in throwing just one more.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I hope this reflection offers encouragement to those who feel like they’re running out of time or options — especially those who think, “Maybe it's too late for me.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>📝 Introduction</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Recently, I came across a manga called <em>Thank You Pitch</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Honestly, I wasn’t expecting much.<br>“Probably just another sports story about some talented young guy,” I thought.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But I was wrong.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The main character, Kiriyama Fuse, can only throw three pitches a day.<br>He hurt his elbow back in middle school, and now that's all he's got.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even so, he still takes the mound — with everything he’s got — and throws those three pitches for his team.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And somewhere along the way, I started seeing myself in him.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I can't push like I used to. My body’s slower. My mind doesn’t recover as fast.<br>I’ve started to wonder:<br><strong>How many more times will I get to go all in on something?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But even now, I realize — deep down — I still want to throw.<br>Maybe not like before, but still… I want to reach someone.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟦 Chapter 1: Why We Can Still Stand — Even If We’re No Longer Perfect</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1664,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_t3h3txt3h3txt3h3.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1664"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kiriyama Fuse isn’t a “complete” pitcher anymore.<br>He can only throw three pitches a day — the damage from his youth never fully healed.<br>He’s not as strong or precise as he used to be.<br>But still, he gets on the mound and throws with everything he’s got.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>What struck me most wasn’t what he lost — but how seriously he faces what he <em>can</em> still do.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I was younger, I felt like I had to wait until everything was ready before I did anything.<br>I was scared to act without confidence.<br>Ashamed to show up without being fully prepared.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Nobody told me that directly — but the world around me seemed to say:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Don’t mess up.”<br>“If you fail, there’s no coming back.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So I kept putting things off.<br>Waiting until I got better.<br>Until I felt more “ready.”<br>And in the meantime… I missed some chances. Probably more than I’d like to admit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But recently, I’ve started thinking:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“It doesn’t have to be perfect. Just start.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You fix things <em>after</em> you start. You learn <em>while</em> you move forward.<br>That’s way better than standing still.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kiriyama does the same.<br>He’s not in perfect shape. He has only three pitches.<br>But he still gets on that mound and gives it all he’s got — with the version of himself that exists <em>now</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We grew up in a time when society made it hard to try.<br>Make a mistake? You’d get scolded.<br>Stand out? You’d get smacked down.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But things are different now.<br>And if we wait until we’re perfect, we’ll wait forever.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s why I want to throw.<br>Even if I mess it up. Even if I fall short.<br>The act of throwing — that still means something.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Having only three pitches left…<br>That just means <em>each one matters even more</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So I stand up — even if I’m not perfect anymore.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟦 Chapter 2: This Isn’t a Do-Over — It’s Me, Throwing as I Am Now</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1666,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_uozn0duozn0duozn.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1666"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kiriyama got back on the mound because he felt like someone still needed him.<br>I think it was Captain Kohori’s words that lit that fire in him.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And I… I kinda saw myself in that moment too.<br>Though in my case, it wasn’t as noble as wanting to help someone.<br>If I’m honest — I just wanted to try something again. That’s all.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Now that I’m in my 50s, time feels faster.<br>How many years do I really have left in good health?<br>Even if I stay healthy, how many years can I move freely and live fully?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I thought about that, Kiriyama’s “only three pitches a day” hit me hard.<br>It made me wonder if maybe <em>I</em> only have “three pitches” left too —<br>Three chances to really put my heart into something.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And that’s why I didn’t want to waste them.<br>If I’m gonna throw, I want to do it properly — in my own way.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No one’s forcing me.<br>No one’s calling my name.<br>But inside, I felt it — I wanted to move again.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not to rewrite the past.<br>Not because it’s too late or too soon.<br>But just because… this is where I am.<br>And from <em>here</em>, I want to throw something out into the world.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s not about going back.<br>It’s not a do-over.<br>It’s me, now, choosing to throw — with everything I’ve got left.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And honestly, that’s enough.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟦 Chapter 3: One Pitch, One Heart — It’s Not About How Much, But What’s Inside</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1668,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/734d911fcfdc79c35b7d88d35baaf3e0.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1668"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kiriyama can only throw three pitches a day.<br>But those few throws — they change the flow of the entire game.<br>And honestly, that hit me right in the heart.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s not about <em>how much</em> you do.<br>It’s about <em>what you put into it</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I started blogging, I thought,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“No one’s going to read this anyway.”<br>The numbers were low.<br>There were barely any reactions.<br>Still… I kept writing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even if it reached just one person — that would be enough.<br>Even if I had only “three pitches” left,<br>If I could throw them with everything I’ve got, that would mean something.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These days, social media makes it feel like you have to post all the time.<br>That more content equals more value.<br>That going viral means you matter.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But maybe the truth is this:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One heartfelt message can change someone.<br>Just like Kiriyama’s fastball shifted the mood of the whole team.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>His teammates looked at him differently after those three pitches.<br>Their eyes changed.<br>Their hearts changed.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And maybe… I want to do the same.<br>With words.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I want to write blog posts that feel like <em>my pitch</em>.<br>Even if the writing is rough. Even if it’s imperfect.<br>What matters is that I <em>showed up</em>,<br>That I <em>faced the plate</em>,<br>And I threw with all my heart.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I don’t have unlimited throws left.<br>So each one counts more than ever.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Because when you know you only have three pitches left…<br>Every. Single. One.<br>Becomes powerful.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟨 Chapter 4: The Words That Got Me to Stand Again</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1669,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_b921ydb921ydb921.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1669"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I once heard something on YouTube.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“At first, nothing goes right. That’s normal.<br>But you learn. You get a little better.<br>And little by little, things begin to take shape.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I don’t remember exactly how it was said,<br>But that message stuck with me —<br>Like it was meant just for me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Out of all the noise online,<br>those words reached me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>They moved something in me.<br>Like how Captain Kohori said to Kiriyama,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Come pitch for our team.”<br>That simple sentence brought him back to the mound.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No one actually said those words <em>to</em> me.<br>But I heard them.<br>And I felt it —<br>Maybe I could try again.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Of course, the choice to move was my own.<br>But I know this:<br>Someone’s words gave me the courage to take that step.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before that, I kept telling myself:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“No one needs me.”<br>“I’ll probably fail again.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And so, I did nothing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Days passed.<br>But nothing changed — because <em>I</em> didn’t move.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s what I wanted to stop.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even if I’ve only got three pitches left,<br>Even if no one’s watching or cheering —<br>If I <em>want</em> to try again,<br>Then that’s enough.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s my mound.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟦 Chapter 5: Today, I Threw One More Pitch</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1671,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/cedeac9e3139deb8e673118f2dc4197a.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1671"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not every day feels good.<br>Sometimes I feel like I’m getting nowhere.<br>Sometimes no one responds, no one sees.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But today, I wrote a blog post.<br>Just one more quiet pitch.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And maybe — that’s enough.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If it lands softly somewhere in someone’s heart,<br>If someone reads it and thinks,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Oh… there’s someone like that out there,”<br>Then I’ve done what I needed to do.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There was a time I felt like I had nothing.<br>That I wasn’t useful. That I had no value.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But then, one line on YouTube stuck with me:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“You won’t get it right at first. But you learn.<br>Bit by bit, you move forward.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That line soaked into me.<br>Not like a shout, but like a gentle warmth.<br>And I thought:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Okay… maybe I’ll just throw one more.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not for anyone else.<br>But if this blog — if these words —<br>become <em>that</em> kind of voice for someone else someday,<br>then I’m glad I threw it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Just one pitch.<br>But one with everything I’ve got today.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟧 Conclusion: What Will You Put Into Your Last Three Pitches?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":1676,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/2afd603de1b0b7b886c298d8d3ec0e89-1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1676"/></figure>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To be honest, I started blogging because it seemed like an easy side hustle.<br>But before I knew it, it became a way to face myself.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As I kept writing, I realized something:<br>Even just one pitch — if thrown with care — might reach someone.<br>Especially someone like the old me, standing still and unsure.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You don’t have to be perfect.<br>You don’t need big results.<br>You just need to throw what <em>you</em> can — right now.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Sometimes I still wonder:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Why am I even doing this?”<br>“Where is this going?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But even with those doubts,<br>I threw one more pitch today.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And if you’re standing at the edge of something,<br>Thinking,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Maybe I want to try, but I’m not sure…”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Then this blog post — this pitch —<br>is for you.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You don’t have to catch it.<br>You don’t even have to respond.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Just know:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Someone out here threw it — quietly, but sincerely — in your direction.</p>
