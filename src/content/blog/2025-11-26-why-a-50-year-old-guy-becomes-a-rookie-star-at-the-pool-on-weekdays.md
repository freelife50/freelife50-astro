<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>You’ve witnessed the strange, parallel universe of weekday city pools<br></li>

<li>You’re in your 40s–50s and feel like a <strong>veteran at work</strong> but a <strong>youngster outside</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li>Social insurance premiums make you wonder where your money is going<br></li>

<li>You see energetic seniors and think, <strong>“So… are they healthy or not? Which is it?”</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li>You want to keep exercising even though motivation often refuses to show up<br></li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><strong><span class="fz-22px"><span class="fz-24px">🎯 Conclusion</span></span></strong></p>

<p>In a weekday pool, a man in his 50s suddenly becomes<br><strong>a highly scouted rookie.</strong></p>

<p>At work, I’m a seasoned veteran.<br>But once I step into the water, the compliments start flying:</p>

<p><strong>“You’re so young!”</strong><strong><br></strong> <strong>“You’re fast!”</strong><strong><br></strong> <strong>“You really swim a lot!”</strong></p>

<p>The gap is amusing, a little irritating, and somehow heartwarming.<br>And watching those seniors keep showing up every day makes me think:</p>

<p><strong>“Honestly… I want to be like them someday.”</strong></p>

<p>Complaints included, moving our bodies might be<br><strong>the smallest but real contribution</strong> we can make to others.</p>

<h2 id="weekday-pools-a-world-of-their-own" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ Weekday Pools: A World of Their Own</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Gemini_Generated_Image_olw9l3olw9l3olw9.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2828"/></figure>

<p>No matter the timing—lunch break, between shifts, early mornings—<br>I try to keep a ritual:<br><strong>swim at least 30 minutes every day.</strong></p>

<p>I used to be a <strong>full-red-zone health disaster</strong>:<br>liver function, blood sugar, everything off the charts.</p>

<p>Daily swimming has helped reduce that to:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Triglycerides: still high, but stable<br></li>

<li>Blood pressure: <strong>weirdly enthusiastic (too high)</strong> → meds required<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Still, that’s a massive improvement.<br>It feels like going from failing five subjects to failing one and a half.</p>

<p>Then one day, I walked into the pool during a weekday and realized:</p>

<p><strong>This is a different universe.</strong></p>

<p>Seniors in their 70s and 80s made up nearly the entire population.<br>It felt like entering a <strong>preview room of Japan’s future demographic chart.</strong></p>

<p>And suddenly, I became “the young guy.”</p>

<p>Embarrassing? Yes.<br>A little delightful? Also yes.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="the-generational-breakdown-inside-the-po" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ The Generational Breakdown Inside the Pool</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Gemini_Generated_Image_3cezto3cezto3cez.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2830"/></figure>

<p>Here’s how the ecosystem works:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>70s–80s:</strong> Absolute Monarchs<br></li>

<li><strong>60s:</strong> Established Middle Class<br></li>

<li><strong>50s:</strong> Young Talent (← me)<br></li>

<li><strong>Under 40:</strong> Rare, almost mythical creatures<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Swim one normal 25m lap and I instantly hear:</p>

<p><strong>“You’re young and fast!”</strong></p>

<p>Me? The guy who loses balance putting on socks?<br>But here, I’m a <strong>rookie sensation</strong>.</p>

<p>The pool is wild.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="the-strongest-opponent-the-wet-capybara-" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ The Strongest Opponent: The Wet Capybara Squad</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Gemini_Generated_Image_y4zupey4zupey4zu.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2831"/></figure>

<p>The real challenge isn’t swimming technique.<br>It’s the group occupying the turning point:</p>

<p><strong>The Wet Capybara Squad.</strong></p>

<p>They stand in the water chatting endlessly,<br>floating peacefully like capybaras in a hot spring.</p>

<p>Adorable? Yes.<br><strong>The ultimate obstacle? Also yes.</strong></p>

<p>Their formation is so tight even a Roman phalanx would lose.</p>

<p>I try to pass.<br>They shift half a step.</p>

<p><strong>Half a step is not enough, my friends.</strong></p>

<p>Their routine:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Talk<br></li>

<li>Swim slowly<br></li>

<li>Talk<br></li>

<li>Backstroke<br></li>

<li>Talk<br></li>

<li>Repeat forever<br></li>
</ul>

<p>This is not interval training.<br>This is <strong>Advanced Jaw-Endurance Conditioning</strong>,<br>with <strong>95% of calories spent on conversation.</strong></p>

<p>If they chatted in the jacuzzi, nobody would complain.<br>But walking there seems to require too much effort.</p>

<p>Are they energetic?<br>Are they tired?<br><strong>Impossible to tell.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="ill-be-honest-their-relaxed-vibe-sometim" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ I’ll Be Honest: Their Relaxed Vibe Sometimes Annoys Me</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Image_fx-4.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2832"/></figure>

<p>I’m squeezing workouts into a busy schedule.<br>No luxury of time.</p>

<p>Seeing them peacefully blocking my lane can be… frustrating.</p>

<p>And their conversations are extremely vivid:</p>

<p>“About my grandchild…”<br>“My hospital visit…”<br>“My pension…”</p>

<p>The pension part?<br><strong>That’s the only one I actually want to hear.</strong><strong><br></strong> My future depends on it.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="their-era-and-mine-are-completely-differ" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ Their Era and Mine Are Completely Different</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Gemini_Generated_Image_acnw65acnw65acnw.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2833"/></figure>

<p>For their generation:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Pension: small contribution, large payout<br></li>

<li>Social insurance: cheap<br></li>

<li>Economy: booming<br></li>
</ul>

<p>Today?</p>

<p>My payslip looks like it’s been attacked by invisible thieves.</p>

<p>Of course the imbalance feels unfair.</p>

<p>But one day, a weird thought hit me:</p>

<p><strong>“Maybe the warm water and their energy are partly powered by my social insurance premiums…”</strong></p>

<p>Not funny, but somehow a little funny.</p>

<p>When I heard someone say “pension” at the turning point,<br>I nearly drowned from the shock.</p>

<p>Someone give me a waterproof mic.<br>That information might save my financial life.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="stilltheir-persistence-is-really-somethi" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ Still—Their Persistence Is Really Something</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Image_fx-1-2.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2834"/></figure>

<p>I complain, but credit is due.</p>

<p>Rain, cold, heat—<br>they show up almost every day.</p>

<p>Talking or not, they move, they exercise, they continue.<br>Their <strong>consistency is incredible.</strong></p>

<p>Will I be like that in my 70s?<br>Not sure.</p>

<p>But I want to learn from that persistence.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="so-ill-keep-swimmingcomplaints-and-all" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ So I’ll Keep Swimming—Complaints and All</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Gemini_Generated_Image_5u0pq55u0pq55u0p.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2835"/></figure>

<p>Nothing else will change:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>The system<br></li>

<li>The seniors<br></li>

<li>The Wet Capybara Squad blocking the turn point<br></li>
</ul>

<p>So I must change what I can:</p>

<p><strong>30 minutes a day.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> Protect my health.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> Slightly reduce future medical costs.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> Lower the burden on younger generations—just a tiny bit.</strong></p>

<p>My daily crawl might save Japan’s finances by<br><strong>a few yen.</strong></p>

<p>And that’s enough.<br>It has to be.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="one-last-thing" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>■ One Last Thing</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Image_fx-3-1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2836"/></figure>

<p>I genuinely admire their energy.<br>And honestly, they’re fun to watch sometimes.</p>

<p>But please:</p>

<p><strong>Stop blocking the turning point.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> I am actually going to sink.</strong></p>

<p>Still, when I’m in my 70s,<br>showing up every day, still swimming…</p>

<p>Someone will probably say:</p>

<p><strong>“That young guy is still here!”</strong></p>

<p>I want to hear that.</p>

<p>Although realistically,<br>I might be the one blocking someone else’s turn.</p>

<p><strong>I’ll try my best not to.</strong></p>
