<p><strong>While Rohan Kishibe Goes to the Louvre, I Was at a Hakata Ramen Shop Yesterday.</strong></p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>✅この記事で伝えたいこと</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>この記事では、岸辺露伴『ルーヴルへ行く』の話題をきっかけに、50代の筆者が「博多ラーメン」と「健康」との向き合い方をユーモラスに綴ったエッセイです。</strong><br>フィクションと現実を重ねながら、日常にある“罪深い美味しさ”や、自分を整えることの意味を語ります。ラーメン好き、ジョジョ好き、そして健康にちょっと不安のある50代読者に共感してもらえたらうれしいです。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>This post draws a parallel between a trending JoJo spin-off and a simple bowl of ramen—blending fiction and real-life cravings, with a dash of middle-aged reflection.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">SNSに「黒い絵」がトレンド入りしていた日</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>On the last day of Golden Week, I saw "Rohan Kishibe Goes to the Louvre" trending on social media.</strong><br>“ジョジョの奇妙な冒険”のスピンオフ。<br><strong>It’s a spin-off of <em>JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure</em>.</strong><br>ルーヴル美術館の地下に眠る「この世で最も黒く、邪悪な絵」にまつわるミステリー。<br><strong>A mysterious story about the "darkest, most evil painting" hidden deep beneath the Louvre Museum.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>記憶を“本”のように読み解く漫画家・岸辺露伴。<br><strong>The protagonist, Rohan Kishibe, is a manga artist who can read memories like books.</strong><br>彼が自らの過去と向き合いながら、絵の呪いと闇に迫っていく——<br><strong>He confronts the curse of the painting while facing his own past.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":404,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/97e847ef695714369cbe70fdc1afea06-683x1024.png" alt="" class="wp-image-404"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でもその日、俺が向かったのは、<br>ルーヴルではなく、<strong>地元の博多ラーメン屋</strong>やった。<br><strong>But that day, I didn’t go to the Louvre—I went to my local Hakata ramen joint.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">露伴の「ヘブンズ・ドアー」、俺の「替え玉！」</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>While Rohan uses “Heaven’s Door,” I simply shout, “Kaedama!”</strong><br>露伴は、面白い漫画を描くためなら命をかけるタイプの変人。<br><strong>Rohan is the eccentric type who risks everything to create compelling manga.</strong><br>他人の記憶を読み、心の奥に潜む“闇”と向き合うことで作品を生み出す。<br><strong>He draws inspiration by literally diving into people’s hidden memories.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>俺も…とは言えんけど、<br><strong>I’m not quite like him, but…</strong><br><strong>「替え玉！」と叫ぶその瞬間だけは、たぶん俺も何かと向き合ってたんやと思う。</strong><br><strong>…when I yell "Kaedama!" maybe I'm facing something of my own too.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>注文したのは、いつものバリカタ。<br><strong>My go-to: extra firm noodles.</strong><br>そして1杯目が終わるやいなや、迷いなく叫んだ。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>「替え玉！」</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>…そして、もう一度。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>「替え玉！」</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":401,"width":"590px","height":"auto","sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/a654d5b879b06cba27467c42d5a4419d.png" alt="" class="wp-image-401" style="width:590px;height:auto"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>そこに<strong>白ごはん</strong>まで追加して、気づけば、目の前には“俺の黒い絵”みたいな濃厚スープが広がっていた。<br><strong>I even ordered white rice. Before I knew it, that thick tonkotsu soup was like my own “dark painting” staring back at me.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">罪と後悔の“スープの底”</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Guilt and Regret at the Bottom of the Bowl</strong><br>映画では、黒い絵に触れた者が自らの罪や後悔と向き合い、怪異に巻き込まれていく。<br><strong>In the film, those who see the black painting confront their past sins and regrets—and face strange horrors.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>俺の場合は、<br><strong>In my case…</strong><br><strong>替え玉2回＋白ごはん＋スープ完飲</strong>という行為のあと、胃の奥にじんわりと漂う罪悪感や。<br><strong>…after two extra noodle orders, rice, and drinking every last drop of soup, a quiet guilt settled in my gut.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>「そろそろ塩分も気にせななぁ…」<br><strong>“I really should watch my salt intake soon…”</strong><br>「血圧、最近ちょっと高めやったな…」<br><strong>“My blood pressure’s been kinda high lately…”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>そんなことを思いながらも、スープの底が見えるまでレンゲを止められなかった。<br><strong>Yet still, I couldn’t stop until I saw the bottom of the bowl.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>うまかった。ほんまに、うまかったんや。</strong><br><strong>It was good. Really, really good.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でも、その分だけ、自分の身体に小さな“闇”を刻んだ気もする。<br><strong>But I may have carved a little darkness into my own body.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":405,"width":"541px","height":"auto","sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/be51ce2107ec160baf37f59017e4bf63-683x1024.png" alt="" class="wp-image-405" style="width:541px;height:auto"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">写真は…ない。けど、残ってる。</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>No Photo, but It’s Burned Into My Memory</strong><br>ブログ用に写真を撮ろうと思ってたんや。<br><strong>I meant to take a photo for the blog.</strong><br>けど――気づいたら全部食べ終わってた。<br><strong>But before I realized it, I had finished everything.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>「本当に大切な時間って、記録じゃなくて記憶に残るんやな」</strong><br><strong>“Sometimes the most important moments aren’t captured—they’re remembered.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">自分を整えることが、他者の安心につながる</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Taking Care of Yourself Helps Others Feel at Ease</strong><br>50代にもなると、<br><strong>In your 50s, you start realizing—</strong><br>「誰かのために頑張る前に、まず自分をちゃんと整えなあかんな」って思うようになってきた。<br><strong>You can’t take care of others if you don’t take care of yourself first.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>身体の声を聞くこと。<br>心の声を無視せんこと。<br>スープを飲み干したあとに、ちゃんと後悔できること。<br><strong>Listen to your body. Don’t ignore your mind. And be willing to regret that last sip of soup.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":407,"width":"556px","height":"auto","sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/9eb6efd72eec784e84694945d6a2055c-683x1024.png" alt="" class="wp-image-407" style="width:556px;height:auto"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>それが、<br>家族に心配をかけずに過ごすことにつながるし、<br>職場で誰かの支えになることもできるし、<br>このブログを通じて、同じように“罪深いラーメン愛”を持つ誰かをちょっと笑顔にできるかもしれん。<br><strong>That kind of self-awareness might ease your family’s worries, help you support others at work, or even make someone out there smile—someone who also loves ramen too much.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>それって、ちょっとした“他者貢献”やと思うねん。</strong><br><strong>And maybe… that’s a small kind of contribution to the world.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">ルーヴルに行かずとも、俺たちにも“舞台”はある</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>You Don’t Need the Louvre to Face What Matters</strong><br>ルーヴル美術館の地下倉庫。<br>岸辺露伴が“黒い絵”と向き合った場所。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>そして俺は、<br>昨日の午後、ラーメン屋のカウンター席で、自分の身体と向き合ってた。<br><strong>Yesterday afternoon, I was facing my own reality—at the ramen shop counter.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>どちらも、誰かにとっては何でもない場所。<br><strong>To someone else, both places might mean nothing.</strong><br>でも、<strong>自分にとっては“何かを掘り下げる場所”になり得る。</strong><br><strong>But to us, they’re places where we dig deep into ourselves.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>それがたとえ、替え玉2回と白ごはんの向こうにある罪と後悔やとしても。<br><strong>Even if all that soul-searching is hidden behind two extra helpings of noodles and a bowl of rice.</strong></p>
