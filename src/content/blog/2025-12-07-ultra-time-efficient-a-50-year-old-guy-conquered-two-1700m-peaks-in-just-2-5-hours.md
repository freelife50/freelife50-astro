<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion</strong></h2>

<p>This hike feels <strong>way easier</strong> than Mt. Tōno-dake,<br>yet you get <strong>two</strong> 1700m peaks + jaw-dropping views in just <strong>2.5 hours</strong>.<br>Honestly… it feels like cheating.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/PXL_20251130_021334134.NIGHT_.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2844"/></figure>

<p>The route I took:</p>

<p><strong>Yunosawa Pass Parking ⇔ Mt. Okura-takamaru ⇔ Mt. Hamaiba-maru (Out-and-back)</strong></p>

<p>Starting from Yunosawa Pass (already <em>around 1700m</em>) means…<br>you’ve basically <em>won before taking a single step</em>.</p>

<p>From there?</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Mt. Fuji BOOM—front and center</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>360° panoramic ridgelines</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>Golden grass fields straight out of Hokkaido</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Round trip: <strong>2h 24m</strong></p>

<p>Peaks bagged:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Okura-takamaru (1781m)</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>Hamaiba-maru (1752m)</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
</ul>

<p>If the suffering level of climbing Tōno-dake is “10,”<br>this trail is “3.”<br>What kind of scam is this…?<br>（Don’t worry—it’s legal. 😂）</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Mt. Hamaiba-maru (1752m)</strong></h3>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/PXL_20251130_031540694.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2848"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>About 30 minutes from Okura-takamaru<br></li>

<li>Official name: <strong>破魔射場丸（Hamaiba-maru）</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li>Historically a ritual archery site (射場＝iba)<br></li>

<li>Fuji looks <strong>even bigger</strong> from here<br></li>
</ul>
