<p>■<strong>Prologue</strong></p>

<p>“Swimming laps is boring.” “I can’t keep up my pool routine.”<br>If you’ve ever thought like this, especially in your 50s, here’s a slightly ridiculous—but real—story for you.</p>

<p>This is the tale of a daydreamer uncle who turned his <em>wild imagination</em> into reality.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Underwater Instrumentality Project Begins</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-30T03_13_55.457Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2183"/></figure>

<p>I recently made a rule for myself: swim at least 30 minutes every day.<br>It’s my way of keeping my lazy self in check—a kind of ritual.</p>

<p>But let’s be honest: swimming back and forth in the pool every day gets monotonous, even soul-crushing.</p>

<p>That’s when the thought hit me:<br><strong>“What if I could listen to music underwater…?”</strong></p>

<p>This was the spark for my so-called <strong>“Underwater Instrumentality Project” (aka Underwater Walkman Plan).</strong></p>

<p>It all traced back to my 20s, when I tried scuba diving. The sensation was unforgettable:<br><em>“It feels like being in space.”</em></p>

<p>I thought: what if I could overlay that floating-in-space sensation with my favorite music?</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Enter Technology (and My Struggles)</h2>

<p>Back in my youth, that idea was pure fantasy. But now, thanks to bone-conduction earphones, it’s a reality.</p>

<p>When I discovered this, I trembled—not humanity as a whole, but me, a 50-something uncle, trembling with excitement.</p>

<p>Then came the real challenge:<br>How do I get YouTube music into these devices?<br>Download? Convert? Install? Already sounds exhausting.</p>

<p>Typical Gen-X frustration: sitting in front of my computer, muttering <em>“Forget it…”</em> after every failed attempt.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Diving into “Space”</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Gemini_Generated_Image_lope4ilope4ilope.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2202"/></figure>

<p>Finally, the big day. I strapped on the bone-conduction earphones, sealed my ears with plugs, and slipped underwater.</p>

<p>Instantly, the pool transformed into <strong>outer space.</strong><br>Water resistance wrapped around me, the floating sensation amplified.</p>

<p>Then came the music—Hawaiian melodies, deep-sea sounds—resonating directly through my bones.</p>

<p>I felt like I was inside an EVA cockpit, perfectly synchronized.<br>My <strong>synchronization rate hit 400%.</strong></p>

<p>For the first time, swimming laps felt less like torture and more like a cosmic journey.</p>

<p>Like Amuro hearing Lalah’s voice in <em>Gundam</em>, I felt guided by unseen melodies.<br>Maybe I had awakened as a Newtype?</p>

<p>It was surreal, almost mystical.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Pros &amp; Cons After Trying</h2>

<p><strong>👍 What was great</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Unbelievable cost-performance</strong>: Only ¥4,000 (~$30) for such a surreal, immersive experience.</li>

<li><strong>Stress relief</strong>: Music distracted me from the physical pain of swimming, making it genuinely fun.</li>

<li><strong>Immersion boost</strong>: With earplugs, outside noise vanished—only the music remained.</li>
</ul>

<li><strong>Durability unknown</strong>: Budget model—how long it lasts is anyone’s guess.</li>
</ul>
