<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>This post captures a quiet spring afternoon—no drama, no rush.<br>Just a bowl of negi ramen, and Momiji peacefully sniffing the breeze.<br>Sometimes, the smallest moments bring the deepest comfort.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span class="bold-red"><strong>🕒 このブログは 約1〜2分で読めます / <em>This blog takes about 1–2 minutes to read.</em></strong></span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">本文/BODY</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今日は特に何があったわけでもないけど、「なんかええ日やなぁ」って思える春の昼下がりやった。 気温もちょうどよくて、風もやさしい。こういう日は、無性にラーメンが食べたくなる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Today was one of those spring afternoons where everything just felt right. Nothing special happened, but the breeze was soft, the temperature perfect—and on days like this, I just crave ramen.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>向かったのは、いつもの「一発ラーメン」。 僕が頼むのは、いつもネギラーメン。+100円でネギ山盛り。 コスパがいいし、ネギのシャキシャキがスープと絡んで最高やねん。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I headed to my usual spot, "Ippatsu Ramen." I always go for the negi (green onion) ramen—100 yen extra for a mountain of shredded leeks. It’s a great value, and the way the crisp leeks blend into the broth is just perfect.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":332,"width":"561px","height":"auto","sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/PXL_20250503_012544543-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-332" style="width:561px;height:auto"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>一番人気は「ネギトロチャーシュー麺」らしいけど、 具が多すぎてスープがぬるくなるのがちょっと残念で、 僕はいつもあえて“シンプルだけど熱々”なネギラーメンを選ぶ。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Their most popular dish is the negitoro chashu ramen, but it comes with so many toppings that the soup cools down too fast. So I always choose the simpler, piping-hot negi ramen.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今日の一杯も、ふーふーしながらじっくり味わった。 「うん、今日もええ日やな」って思える時間。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I took my time with today’s bowl, gently blowing on each bite. It made me think, "Yep, today’s another good day."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>誰かの役に立つとか、立たへんとか、そんなこと考えずに書いてるけど、 もしかしたら、「なんかいいな」って感じてもらえる人がいるかもしれん。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I don’t write these posts thinking about whether they’ll help someone or not. But maybe, just maybe, someone out there will read this and feel a little better.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>今日のラーメンの写真を見て、「昼ごはんラーメンにしようかな」って思ってもらえたら、 それだけでちょっと、誰かの今日を明るくできた気がする。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If someone sees a photo of today’s ramen and thinks, "I could go for some ramen today," then maybe I’ve brightened their day a bit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>【今日のもみじ / Today’s Momiji】</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>もみじも気持ちよさそうに過ごしてた。 春の風がそよそよ吹く中で、 クッションの上にちょこんと座って、外のにおいをくんくん。 目を細めて、耳をピンと立てて、なんだか「春やなぁ」って顔してた。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Momiji was enjoying herself, too. With the soft spring breeze drifting in, she sat on her cushion, sniffing the air. Her eyes half-closed, ears perked up—as if she was thinking, "Yep, this is spring."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>何も特別じゃない、ただ気持ちいい日。 でも、そんな日があるからこそ、毎日をちゃんと味わえる気がする。 また、こういう日が続いたらええなぁと思った。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Nothing special. Just a calm, beautiful day. But maybe it’s these kinds of days that help us really appreciate life. I hope more days like this come along.</p>
