<p>■<strong>Introduction</strong></p>

<p>Even in your 50s, there are still challenges worth taking on.<br>One of mine: checking off <strong>“Finishing Jiro Ramen with All Toppings (Zenmashi)”</strong> from my bucket list.<br>This wasn’t about bragging — it was a day that shook both my stomach and my soul, and I’m here to share it with you, with a smile.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Bucket-List Dream</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_025310134.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2143"/></figure>

<p>At the very top of my list stood one name: <strong>Jiro Ramen</strong>.<br>But not just any Jiro. I aimed for the final boss of the Jiro world — <strong>“Large Ramen, Zenmashi (all toppings, maxed out).”</strong></p>

<p>This wasn’t about beating younger guys.<br>It was about proving something to my past self and my future self.<br>A man’s pure romance.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">My First Encounter with Jiro</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-21T22_59_17.782Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2144"/></figure>

<p>I first learned about Jiro Ramen in my 30s.<br>I went alone, thinking, “Let’s see what the fuss is about.”</p>

<p>And then the bowl appeared.</p>

<p><strong>“What the heck is THIS?!”</strong></p>

<p>A mountain of bean sprouts, thick pork slices, and shimmering back fat.<br>It didn’t look like ramen at all.<br>My first thought: <em>“This isn’t ramen… it looks like pig feed!”</em></p>

<p>But once I took a bite, I realized — this wasn’t ramen.<br><strong>Jiro is its own food genre.</strong><br>And from that day, I slowly became hooked.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Frozen at the Ticket Machine</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_021959939.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2145"/></figure>

<p>Years passed. Now in my 50s, I made up my mind:<br>“If not now, I’ll never do it.”</p>

<p>Standing at the ticket machine, my heart pounded.<br>The students in front of me all chose “Small.” Of course they did.<br>But before I knew it, my finger pressed <strong>“Large Ramen.”</strong></p>

<p>Then the words slipped out of my mouth:<br><strong>“Zenmashi, please.”</strong><br>I had cast the forbidden spell.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Is Zenmashi?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_022330983.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2147"/></figure>

<p>Jiro offers four free toppings:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Garlic</strong>: a warrior’s mark that lasts until tomorrow</li>

<li><strong>Vegetables</strong>: a fortress of bean sprouts and cabbage</li>

<li><strong>Fat (Abura)</strong>: shimmering back fat, pure energy</li>

<li><strong>Karame</strong>: extra soy sauce that defines the taste</li>
</ul>

<p>To order <em>Zenmashi</em> means to max out ALL of them.<br>The ultimate move that tests your stomach and your spirit.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Fortress Appears</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_024110182.MP_.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2148"/></figure>

<p><em>DON!</em> The bowl landed on the counter. The air shifted.</p>

<p>Sprouts forming castle walls.<br>Fat glistening like a moat.<br>Thick pork slices like towering gates.</p>

<p>This wasn’t ramen.<br>It was a <strong>ramen fortress.</strong></p>

<p>The first bite? Pure bliss.<br>The sweetness of fat, the crunch of vegetables — perfect harmony.<br>I laughed to myself:</p>

<p><em>“Fat alone could be my side dish!”</em><br><em>“I could eat rice with just this fat!”</em></p>

<p>The first ten minutes were smooth sailing.<br>I wasn’t just in Jiro world. I was in the <strong>Jiro universe.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Hitting the Wall and Entering “The Zone”</h2>

<p>Then came the wall. My stomach screamed: <em>“Stop now!”</em></p>

<p>But suddenly, something strange happened — I entered the <strong>zone</strong>.<br>Beyond the limit, my chopsticks moved on their own.<br>The noodles disappeared into the black hole of my stomach.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Flavor Hacks That Saved Me</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_023020594.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2149"/></figure>

<p>My late-game saviors were the seasonings:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>White pepper</li>

<li>Chili flakes</li>

<li>Extra soy sauce</li>
</ul>

<p>Just a sprinkle, and the soup felt new.<br>It was probably an illusion, but I thought, “I can keep going!”<br>Even as my brain trembled, I couldn’t help but laugh.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Bittersweet Finish</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250819_025118497.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2150"/></figure>

<p>I forced down the last noodles and toppings.<br>But the soup… impossible.</p>

<p>Victory and defeat blended together in one bittersweet ending.<br>But that was fine. Life doesn’t always require you to drink it all.<br>On the bike ride home, nauseous but refreshed, I felt oddly younger inside.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Next Challenge?</h2>

<p>I survived Zenmashi. The first half was heaven. The last half, pure suffering.</p>

<p>Next time… <em>Mashi-mashi</em> (even more)? No way. That’s a black hole I can’t escape.</p>

<p>But deep down, I know — someday I’ll be back. And once again, I’ll cast the spell.<br>And once again, I’ll think:</p>

<p><strong>“This isn’t ramen. This is Jiro — a genre of its own.”</strong></p>
