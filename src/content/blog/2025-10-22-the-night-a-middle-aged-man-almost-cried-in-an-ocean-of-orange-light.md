<p>🌃 <strong>What This Story Is About</strong><strong><br></strong> One autumn night, a baseball stadium in Sagamihara transformed into a glowing sea of orange light.<br>Through those lights, a 50-year-old man rediscovered warmth, memory, and a quiet spark of life.<br>In about ten minutes, you’ll experience both the event and the emotion it stirred.</p>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Event — When a Baseball Stadium Became a Sea of Light</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2588,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/PXL_20251018_084307298-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2588"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Date:</strong> Saturday, October 18, 2025<br>Usually, this field at <em>Fuchinobe Park (Thirty-Four Sagamihara Stadium)</em> echoes with the sound of baseballs.<br>But for one night only, it became a sacred sea of light.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Time: 3:30 PM – 8:00 PM (postponed to the next day if it rains)<br>Organized by: <em>Sagamihara City Green &amp; Public Foundation</em>, <em>Mizuno</em>, and <em>Tokai Sports Management JV</em><em><br></em> Supported by: <em>Ginga Arena</em> and local sponsors</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It was the city’s <strong>first-ever LED lantern release</strong> — a bold, precedent-breaking event.<br>Families and couples in Halloween costumes filled the field, wrapped in waves of orange glow.<br>For Sagamihara, it was a night to remember — a quiet but powerful statement: <em>“We can shine, too.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:table -->
<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><tbody><tr><td><strong>Item</strong></td><td><strong>Details</strong></td></tr><tr><td>Name</td><td>Halloween Lantern Festival 2025</td></tr><tr><td>Date</td><td>October 18, 2025 (Sat) 15:30–20:00</td></tr><tr><td>Venue</td><td>Fuchinobe Park, Thirty-Four Sagamihara Stadium</td></tr><tr><td>Hosts</td><td>Sagamihara City Green &amp; Public Foundation / Mizuno / Tokai Sports JV</td></tr><tr><td>Sponsors</td><td>Ginga Arena &amp; local partners</td></tr><tr><td>Program</td><td>Lantern lighting, food trucks, face painting, costumes welcome</td></tr><tr><td>Admission</td><td>¥300 for viewing / extra for lantern experience (reservation required)</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
<!-- /wp:table -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Atmosphere — “Wait, Is This Really Sagamihara?”</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2589,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/PXL_20251018_093455114-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2589"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Stepping into the stadium felt like entering another world.<br>“Is this… Disneyland?” I whispered — okay, maybe I exaggerated a bit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thousands of orange lanterns swayed above the field, each one tied to thin strings that caught the breeze.<br>They hovered gently, two to three meters above the ground, glowing like tiny floating stars.<br>From afar, it looked as if they were drifting in the night sky.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Around the field, food trucks filled the air with the smell of <em>yakisoba</em>, <em>takoyaki</em>, crepes, and hot drinks.<br>Kids lined up for face painting — pumpkins, bats, and ghosts — while parents snapped endless photos.<br>The laughter, the chatter, the aroma — all blended into something quietly magical.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And then, as the floodlights dimmed, only the lanterns and people’s smiles remained.<br>It was… breathtaking.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>A Small Disaster — The Beer Incident</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2590,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/PXL_20251018_084700238.MP_-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2590"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Confession time. I’d already had a beer before entering the venue.<br>I thought, “A little buzz under the autumn sky won’t hurt.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But as I queued for the ticket, I realized — <em>“Wait, drinking while waiting in line looks kinda bad.”</em><em><br></em> So I hid the half-full can in my bag.<br>And of course… it spilled.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By the time I sat in the stands, my bag and pants were soaked.<br>The man next to me gave me a subtle side-eye and shifted slightly away.<br>He probably thought, <em>“Drunk guy alert.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But no — I wasn’t drunk, just clumsy.<br>So I accepted my fate as the “beer-smelling middle-aged man,”<br>quietly watching the lights from the stands.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>From the Stands — A 300-Yen Luxury</strong></h2>

<!-- wp:image {"id":2591,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/PXL_20251018_092610569-1024x769.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2591"/></figure>

<p>Looking down at the glowing field, I saw families arranging lanterns,<br>kids running, couples taking selfies.<br>And I thought, <em>“This is exactly what I came for.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I didn’t need to release my own light;<br>just witnessing theirs was enough to fill my heart.<br>Three hundred yen for this kind of peace?<br>A bargain.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At 5 PM, participants started receiving their lanterns.<br>At 6:30 PM, the emcee’s voice echoed:<br>“Everyone, let’s count together! 3, 2, 1… light up!”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And then — <em>whoosh.</em><em><br></em> A wave of orange flooded the stadium.<br>Hundreds of lanterns rose gently, tethered yet alive,<br>swaying like warm ripples on a dark sea.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People gasped. Cameras clicked.<br>From above, it looked like the stars had come down to play.<br>And I thought, <em>“This is it.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The lights weren’t rising into the sky —<br>they were descending into our hearts.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong> Remembering Warmth — When a 50-Year-Old Almost Cried</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2594,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Image_fx-1024x559.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2594"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As the field glowed, something lit up inside me, too.<br>I suddenly thought of the people I can’t see anymore.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Families everywhere — parents, kids, grandparents, lovers.<br>And then there was me: alone, smelling like beer, pants still damp.<br>I chuckled… then felt a quiet ache.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But that’s the beauty of it.<br>Those lights weren’t just LEDs — they reflected human warmth.<br>I imagined my late parents smiling somewhere above,<br>watching these same orange lights.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In my 50s, this kind of calm moment hits deep.<br>Fireworks thrilled me when I was young,<br>but now it’s the stillness that moves me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“The light that reaches the soul” —<br>you don’t see it with your eyes; you <em>feel</em> it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Sitting there alone, somehow I didn’t feel lonely.<br>Under that orange glow, we were all connected.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Heart of the Festival — People, Not Performance</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2595,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/PXL_20251018_093741156.NIGHT_-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2595"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>What made this festival special wasn’t the spectacle — it was the <em>warmth</em>.<br>Local volunteers, kind staff, everyone smiling, helping, sharing.<br>Kids whispering “so pretty…”<br>Parents capturing those fleeting moments.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yeah, I was probably the only solo middle-aged guy there —<br>and the only one smelling of beer — but it didn’t matter.<br>In that light, we were all the same.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Next year, I’m sure it’ll grow — maybe even have a stage show.<br>But I hope it never loses this <em>gentle, human glow</em>.<br>That’s the true soul of this festival.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🕯️ <strong>In the End — The Light That Stays</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2598,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Image_fx-1-1024x559.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2598"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Lantern light is strange — it fades fast, yet stays forever.<br>Now in my 50s, I realize: I don’t need big fireworks anymore.<br>What I want are moments that linger quietly in the heart.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That night’s glow reminded me of gratitude —<br>for those who are gone, and those still here.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>On the walk home, I looked up at the city sky.<br>Through the haze of streetlights,<br>I swore I could still see that orange shimmer.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Next year, I’ll go again.<br>Even alone — it’s okay.<br>And this time, I’ll try <em>not</em> to spill my beer.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Because that night taught me something:<br>the light of life doesn’t fade —<br>it just changes where it shines.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>See you next year, under the same sky.</em></p>
