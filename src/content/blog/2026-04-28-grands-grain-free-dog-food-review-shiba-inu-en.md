<p>Every time I fill Momiji's bowl, the same thought crosses my mind.</p>

<p>"Is this actually good for her?"</p>

<p>I've been feeding her the same food for a while now, and honestly, I'm not entirely sure it's the right choice. The ingredient labels are confusing, and every brand seems to claim it's "premium" or "carefully selected." If you've felt the same way, keep reading.</p>

<p>I recently looked closely at <strong>GRANDS (グランズ)</strong>, a premium dog food made in France. I wanted to know if it's actually suitable for a Shiba Inu — and here's what I found.</p>

<p><strong>Bottom line:</strong> The price reflects the quality of ingredients. But it's not for everyone.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why I Started Questioning My Dog's Food</h2>

<p>My Shiba Inu's name is Momiji. She's fluffy, reddish-brown, and surprisingly affectionate and friendly — far more sociable than you'd expect from a Shiba Inu.</p>

<p>Every day I feed her, and every day I wonder if I'm making the right choice. Standing in the pet food aisle feels overwhelming. Every bag says the same things: "carefully selected," "health-conscious design." I couldn't tell the difference.</p>

<p>GRANDS caught my eye, and I decided to actually look into it properly.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Is GRANDS?</h2>

<p>Here's a quick overview.</p>

<p>GRANDS is a premium dog food manufactured in France, with the concept of "a gourmet meal for your dog every day." Production follows the quality standards of FEDIAF, the European pet food industry association.</p>

<p>It's designed for <strong>all breeds and all life stages</strong>, so it works for adult dogs like Momiji.</p>

<p>There are two varieties:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Chicken &amp; Duck</strong>: High palatability, great for picky eaters</li>

<li><strong>Fish &amp; Salmon</strong>: Lower-allergen, omega-3 rich, good for skin health</li>
</ul>

<p>Price: about <strong>$14–$17 per 600g bag</strong> (roughly $24–$27 per kg). A first-time trial price is available.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/grands_03.jpg" alt="GRANDS premium dog food in a ceramic bowl with fresh chicken and salmon" class="wp-image-4312"/></figure>

<p class="has-text-align-center"></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What's Actually in It?</h2>

<p>The biggest selling point is the ingredient composition.</p>

<p><strong>Over 70% animal protein</strong> — that's high compared to most commercial dog foods. Dogs are closer to carnivores than omnivores, so this kind of protein ratio makes sense.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Grain-free</strong>: No wheat, corn, or other grains. Grains are a common allergen trigger, and many owners prefer to avoid them</li>

<li><strong>No artificial additives</strong>: No artificial coloring, flavoring, or preservatives</li>

<li><strong>Low-glycemic design</strong>: Sweet potato and potato replace grains as carbohydrate sources</li>
</ul>

<p>Honestly, "grain-free" and "no additives" are claims you see everywhere these days. Whether GRANDS is genuinely different is something you'd only know after trying it.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/grands_04.jpg" alt="Fresh chicken, salmon, and sweet potato — grain-free ingredients used in GRANDS" class="wp-image-4308"/></figure>

<p class="has-text-align-center"></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why It Might Be a Good Fit for Shiba Inus</h2>

<p>This was the part I cared most about.</p>

<p>Shiba Inus are <strong>prone to skin problems</strong>. Atopic dermatitis and skin inflammation are common in the breed. Momiji's coat gets a bit rough during shedding season, which is something I always keep an eye on.</p>

<p>The Fish &amp; Salmon variety contains <strong>omega-3 fatty acids</strong>, which are known to help reduce inflammation and support skin and coat health. For a Shiba with skin sensitivity, the fish-based option might be the better choice over chicken.</p>

<p>The high-protein, low-carb formula also supports <strong>weight management</strong> — useful for spayed or neutered dogs who tend to gain weight easily. The kibble size is also about 1 cm, which works well for medium-sized dogs.</p>

<p>There's a <strong>first-time trial offer with free shipping</strong> available. Starting with one bag to see how your dog responds is the most practical approach.</p>
