<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>The Conclusion First</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At 50, I’m facing the fear of losing teeth and have decided to take on the challenge of dental implants.<br>This record is meant to give courage to anyone else who’s hesitating to take that first step.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Struggling to Take the First Step</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2241,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-07T06_09_08.693Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2241"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It started with my left first molar becoming loose.<br>“I want to keep my own teeth as long as possible.”<br>“If it comes out, maybe a partial denture is enough.”<br>That’s what I thought.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But then, during a checkup, the dentist said,<br>“Your second molar is also a little loose.”<br>That moment, I realized I couldn’t put this off any longer.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So I began considering removing my wisdom tooth.<br>And then it hit me:<br>“My first molar is already useless. Maybe I should just pull it out completely?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Losing the Joy of Eating</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Chewing on the left hurt, so I only chewed on the right.<br>But then my right cheek started burning as if it were scalded.<br>I could still eat, but I couldn’t <em>enjoy</em> food anymore.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I swallowed food without chewing much.<br>And meals stopped tasting delicious.<br>Eating without savoring—it’s one of the saddest feelings.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Day I Finally Made the Call</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2244,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-07T06_18_23.742Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2244"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even then, I was scared to take action.<br>So I came up with a plan:<br>“After a night shift, sober, looking at my work schedule—I’ll call.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>With trembling hands, I finally dialed.<br><strong>September 17, 2025 (Wed), 2:30 PM</strong><strong><br></strong> Appointment made: extraction of my lower right wisdom tooth.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I hung up, I felt both fear and relief at the same time.<br>I also emailed about the possibility of implants, and the dentist replied:<br>“Let’s decide together at the next visit, including financial aspects.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One step at a time—but I was moving forward.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Two Paths Ahead of Me</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Option 1: “Go Straight to Implants”</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Extract the first molar → healing period → extract wisdom tooth → implant<br>👉 A faster decision, but carries the fear of “What if it’s not what I expected?”<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Option 2: “Cautious Approach”</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>First, have the wisdom tooth extracted → then decide what to do with the first molar<br>　(remove it and go for an implant, choose a partial denture, or keep it as long as possible)<br>👉 The advantage is that I can take my time and consider all the options.<br>👉 But there’s also the fear: <em>“What if I choose a partial denture and the discomfort makes food taste less enjoyable?”</em> And if I choose to keep the tooth, the risk of it getting worse never goes away.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Either way, there’s no turning back.<br>That’s why I’m leaving this struggle behind in my diary.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The High Cost—Is It Worth Paying?</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Implants typically cost <strong>¥300,000–¥500,000 (≈ $2,000–$3,500)</strong> per tooth.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Surgery fee: ¥30,000<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Implant fixture: ¥170,000–¥250,000<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Crown (ceramic/zirconia): ¥60,000–¥150,000<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Bone graft (if needed): +¥50,000–¥200,000<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So, about <strong>¥400,000 (≈ $2,700)</strong> per tooth. Not cheap.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I thought:<br>“It’s a back tooth—appearance doesn’t matter much… but surely it won’t go over ¥1,000,000, right?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But eating is my joy. I don’t golf, I don’t travel much—food is my hobby.<br>Some buy golf clubs, others save for trips.<br>For me, it’s an investment in teeth—for the sake of eating well.<br>Not just an expense, but an investment in my <em>quality of life</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
