<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>🌍 <strong>What is "Hanazakari no Kimitachi e"?</strong><br>One day, I was casually scrolling online when I stumbled across the name. I wasn’t sure if it was a romance, but it caught my eye.<br>Turns out—it was the drama that starred Maki Horikita! I hadn’t watched it back then, but the name sounded familiar.<br>And now, surprisingly, it’s getting its <strong>first anime adaptation</strong>. That alone was enough to make me want to dig deeper.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>🌍 <strong>What’s the story about?</strong><br>It's a hit shojo manga by Hisaya Nakajo, serialized from 1996 to 2004.<br>The plot? A girl disguises herself as a boy and enters an all-boys boarding school to support her athletic idol.<br>Romance, comedy, and chaos unfold in the dorms as she tries to keep her secret—and her feelings—under wraps.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>🌍 <strong>I only remembered Maki Horikita.</strong><br>Back in 2007, the drama starred her along with Shun Oguri and Toma Ikuta—what a cast!<br>Though I didn’t watch the whole series, I vaguely remembered the image of a girl in disguise at a boys’ school.<br>It had remakes in Korea and Taiwan too—truly a pan-Asian hit.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/002ff346c43cbf8dd63c5df962e3a53f.png" alt="" class="wp-image-723"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>🌍 <strong>2025: The anime is finally coming!</strong><br>Nearly 20 years after the manga ended, it’s getting animated for the first time.<br>That’s huge—and the cast looks solid.<br>For someone who spent their 30s grinding in Final Fantasy XI (yep, that was me), it feels surreal to be learning about this now.</p>
</blockquote>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/d530ce553a330488c5100145c4c80f3d.png" alt="" class="wp-image-724"/></figure>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>🌍 <strong>Why now?</strong><br>The timing seems perfect—nostalgia, fan demand, and a new generation ready to discover it.<br>Plus, the late Hisaya Nakajo’s 30th anniversary makes this adaptation feel like a tribute.</p>
</blockquote>
