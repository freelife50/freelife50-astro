<p><strong>☀️Introduction: Fear, Pain, and Embarrassment — I Understand</strong></p>

<p>“I know I need a colon exam… but drinking all that laxative sounds awful, having a scope inserted is scary, and above all, it’s embarrassing.”</p>

<p>That was exactly how I felt as a man in my 50s. Every time my doctor recommended colorectal screening, I dreaded it. Friends told me horror stories: <em>“The laxative was torture.”</em> <em>“The exam was unbearable.”</em></p>

<p>At one point I even thought, <em>“Maybe I should just ignore it.”</em></p>

<p>But colorectal cancer is a serious and common disease in Japan:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>#1 cause of cancer death in women</strong></li>

<li><strong>#3 in men</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Thinking of my family, I knew I couldn’t keep avoiding it. That’s when I discovered <strong>CT Colonography (CTC)</strong> — also called <em>“Virtual Colonoscopy.”</em></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Chapter 1: What Is CT Colonography?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PXL_20250921_054451209.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2306"/></figure>

<p>CTC uses CT scanning to create 3D images of the colon, allowing doctors to “virtually” inspect the inside without inserting a scope.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Purpose &amp; Accuracy</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Detects colorectal cancer and polyps early</li>

<li>Evaluates strictures or adhesions</li>

<li>Examines the abdomen and surrounding organs simultaneously</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Detection Rates:</strong></p>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PXL_20250921_054457776.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2307"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PXL_20250921_054936558.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2308"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Polyps ≥6mm: <strong>80–90%</strong></li>

<li>Polyps ≥10mm: <strong>90–95% (comparable to colonoscopy)</strong></li>

<li>Polyps &lt;5mm: <strong>40–60%</strong> (lower, but low malignant risk)</li>
</ul>

<p>👉 In other words, <strong>CTC reliably detects clinically significant lesions.</strong></p>

<p>In Europe and the U.S., it’s already widely adopted as a first-line screening tool. In Japan, it’s gaining traction as a safe alternative for people hesitant about colonoscopy.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Chapter 2: My Experience — A “Painless” Process</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PXL_20250921_054946294.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2309"/></figure>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Preparation</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Colonoscopy: requires about <strong>2 liters</strong> of laxatives.</li>

<li>CTC: only <strong>150–200ml</strong>.</li>

<li>Special low-residue diet the day before.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">On the Day</h3>

<ol class="wp-block-list"><li>A soft tube is inserted about 4–5cm into the rectum.</li>

<li><strong>Carbon dioxide gas (1,500–2,500ml)</strong> is introduced to inflate the colon.</li>

<li>CT scans are taken in supine and prone positions (<strong>10 seconds each</strong>).</li>

<li>3D images and maps are generated with software.</li>
</ol>

<p>⏱️ <strong>Total exam time: 10–20 minutes.</strong><br>Afterward, the CO₂ is absorbed by the body and exhaled naturally. <strong>The bloating feeling disappeared within minutes.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Chapter 3: Techniques That Make It Easier</h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Tagging Method</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T06_39_10.972Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2310"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Small amounts of barium or iodine solution are ingested.</li>

<li>Residual stool/liquid becomes “tagged” (bright) on CT.</li>

<li>Doctors can distinguish stool from polyps.</li>

<li>Software can even digitally remove tagged stool.</li>
</ul>

<p>👉 Like <strong>adding fluorescent markers to dirt in a pool</strong> so a drone can photograph only the clean areas.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Carbon Dioxide Insufflation</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T06_44_36.887Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2311"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Absorbed <strong>130x faster</strong> than air</li>

<li>Less post-exam bloating or pain</li>

<li>Exhaled naturally through breathing</li>
</ul>

<p>👉 This makes CTC <strong>far more comfortable than conventional colonoscopy.</strong></p>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T07_08_50.319Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2314"/></figure>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T07_07_59.224Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2313"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><thead><tr><th>Aspect</th><th>CT Colonography</th><th>Colonoscopy</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Patient burden</td><td><strong>Low</strong></td><td>High</td></tr><tr><td>Laxative volume</td><td><strong>150–200ml</strong></td><td>~2L</td></tr><tr><td>Exam time</td><td><strong>10–20 min</strong></td><td>20–60 min</td></tr><tr><td>Treatment</td><td>Not possible (diagnostic only)</td><td><strong>Possible (biopsy, removal)</strong></td></tr><tr><td>Discomfort</td><td><strong>Mild</strong></td><td>Strong (sedation sometimes needed)</td></tr><tr><td>Radiation</td><td><strong>1–5 mSv (low-dose) / 4–12 mSv typical</strong></td><td>None</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>

<p>👉 Best strategy: use <strong>CTC for diagnosis</strong>, and colonoscopy if treatment is needed.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Radiation Concerns</h3>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T06_58_04.403Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2312"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Typical: <strong>4–12 mSv</strong></li>

<li>Low-dose protocol: <strong>1–5 mSv</strong></li>

<li>For comparison: <strong>Tokyo–New York round-trip flight = 2–3 mSv</strong><br>👉 About the same as a chest CT scan. <strong>Not excessive.</strong></li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Chapter 5: Who Should Consider CTC?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Google_AI_Studio_2025-09-21T07_13_11.727Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2315"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Elderly or those with reduced heart/lung function</li>

<li>People anxious about colonoscopy (<strong>fear, embarrassment</strong>)</li>

<li><strong>Positive fecal occult blood test (FOBT)</strong> results</li>

<li>Incomplete colonoscopy in the past</li>

<li>Abdominal discomfort, requiring colon + nearby organ evaluation</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Smart Strategy: Alternating Exams</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Year 1: <strong>CTC for screening</strong></li>

<li>Year 2: <strong>Colonoscopy for confirmation/treatment</strong></li>

<li>Repeat alternately</li>
</ul>

<p>👉 Balances <strong>comfort</strong> and <strong>treatment readiness</strong>.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion: One Brave Step Can Protect Your Future</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PXL_20250921_054612533.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2305"/></figure>

<p>Colorectal cancer is highly curable when caught early. Yet many avoid screening because of <strong>fear, embarrassment, or the ordeal of laxatives.</strong></p>

<p>CTC offers:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Easier preparation</strong></li>

<li><strong>Short exam time</strong></li>

<li><strong>Less discomfort and embarrassment</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>For me, experiencing CTC made me realize: <em>“I wish I had known this earlier.”</em></p>

<p>👉 The next step is simple: just tell your doctor,<br><strong>“I’d like to undergo CT Colonography.”</strong></p>

<p>That one sentence may safeguard not only your future but also the smiles of your loved ones.</p>
