<p><strong>A Quiet Morning, and Then… a Case</strong></p>

<p>Inside a dark car.<br>On the way back from the Libe City Fest, I stopped at a convenience store for a coffee, ready to unwind.<br>Right at that moment—<em>ping</em>—an email from my supervisor.<br>My gut instantly told me: <em>This isn’t going to be good.</em></p>

<p>And you know what? Gut feelings are usually right.<br>I opened it, bracing myself, and there it was:</p>

<p>“I broke the office computer monitor.”</p>

<p>The tone? Completely flat—like saying, “I had toast this morning.”</p>

<p>In short:<br>Broke it → Using a spare monitor for now → Will explain in person tomorrow.</p>

<p>But to me, “broke it” instantly translated to “destroyed it,” and in my head I saw the special move <em>Monitor Breaker!!</em> being unleashed.<br>And not just any special move—this was the kind that pulls you into another dimension.</p>

<p>Just like my “50-Something Guy Goes to a Fest (Isekai Edition)” story…<br>Here we go again.<br>Back to another world.</p>

<h2 id="1-the-silent-treatment-strategy" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. The Silent Treatment Strategy</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-15T10_49_15.204Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2074"/></figure>

<p>The next day, I arrived at work and… said nothing.<br>With these situations, it’s more satisfying to make them bring it up themselves. (Also, it’s more fun this way.)</p>

<p>But there she was—completely unfazed—leaning on her desk, smiling and chatting away.<br>And all I could think was, <em>Is this really the smile of someone who just broke a monitor yesterday?</em><em><br></em> My typing may have gotten a bit… louder.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="2-playing-the-oh-first-ive-heard-of-it-c" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Playing the “Oh, First I’ve Heard of It” Card</strong></h2>

<p>Eventually, she walked over.<br>“I broke the monitor. I told Mr. So-and-so, and he swapped in the spare one.”</p>

<p>Ah, so she thought the supervisor had already told me.<br>But I went full performance mode: wide eyes, surprised tone—<em>“Oh, this is the first I’m hearing of it!”</em></p>

<p>She must have been thinking, <em>Why hasn’t he mentioned it?</em> and finally caved.<br>Meanwhile, my inner voice said: <em>Is there any heart in that delivery?</em></p>

<p>A little “Sorry about that…”—even if it’s just acting—goes a long way.<br>Sometimes we just need that tiny bit of performance to feel consoled.<br>And in that moment, I knew:<br>Advantage, 50-something guy.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="3-the-price-tag-counterattack" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. The Price Tag Counterattack</strong></h2>

<p>Since she was being so casual about it, I casually mentioned the price of our <em>most expensive</em> monitor.<br>Her smile twitched.</p>

<p>“Oh, it wasn’t the expensive one. It was the other one,” she replied.</p>

<p>Sure, but “the other one” still isn’t cheap—it’s a leased unit.<br>Then she added, “If you need me to write an incident report, I will!”</p>

<p>…No, no, no. That’s not the point.<br>It’s not about filling out paperwork—it’s about sincerity.<br>With that attitude, even a love letter would sound like, “Here, I wrote it. Done.”</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="4-the-wall-incident--now-i-remember" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. The Wall Incident – Now I Remember</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/image-3.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2081"/><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Screenshot</figcaption></figure>

<p>Oh right—she’s done something like this before.<br>The <em>“Momiji Wallpaper Incident.”</em></p>

<p>Back then, she explained, “I tripped on a chair and hit the wall.”<br>But when I saw it, I thought, <em>There’s no way this hole is from a simple trip—it’s at kicking height.</em></p>

<p>That hole eventually got patched up—by me and Chappy (my AI sidekick).<br>We designed a cute image of my Shiba Inu, Momiji, printed it on A4 paper, laminated it for extra shine, and stuck it on the wall.</p>

<p>In the end, I forced the whole thing into a comedy bit…<br>Somehow, it turned into a “heartwarming story” that brightened the office.<br>(See related post: 🐕<a href="https://freelife50.com/%f0%9f%90%be%e3%82%82%e3%81%bf%e3%81%98%e3%81%ae%e7%99%92%e3%81%97%e3%80%81%e5%a3%81%e3%81%ae%e5%90%91%e3%81%93%e3%81%86%e3%81%8b%e3%82%89%e3%81%93%e3%82%93%e3%81%ab%e3%81%a1%e3%81%af/"> Hello from the Other Side of the Wall – Momiji’s Healing Touch</a>)</p>

<h2 id="5-detective-oji-sans-theory-and-honest-o" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Detective Oji-san’s Theory and Honest Opinion</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-15T11_31_25.994Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2082"/></figure>

<p>To be fair, you could frame this as “swift action to prevent disruption.”<br>But my detective brain leans toward:<br>“They took quick action because the evidence was obvious.”</p>

<p>If it had been <em>their</em> iPhone that broke, I bet the reaction would’ve been very different.</p>

<p>Still, work did go on without a hitch that day, and I admit I appreciated the quick fix.</p>

<p>But here’s my honest stance:<br>I’m not a fan of the “As long as the job gets done, it’s fine” mentality.</p>

<p><strong>Why? Three reasons:</strong></p>

<ol class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Relationships are the lubricant of teamwork</strong><strong><br></strong> Even if you’re skilled, you need others’ cooperation to achieve big results.<br>And if you’re a nice person, help comes naturally. (Bonus points if you hand out snacks.)<br></li>

<li><strong>Character shows in a crisis</strong><strong><br></strong> Whether people help you in trouble depends on your daily rapport.<br>Kind people get support when the printer jams at the worst moment.<br></li>

<li><strong>People shape the workplace vibe</strong><strong><br></strong> A cheerful coworker can lift everyone’s mood. (A sigh-heavy coworker is basically an air-conditioner set to “Arctic.”)<br></li>
</ol>

<p>Without good character? No thanks.<br>Work takes up about a third of our lives—might as well spend it with people you click with.<br>That’s why I believe:</p>

<p><strong>Skill + Good Personality = The Ultimate Combo</strong></p>

<p>If I had to choose only one, I’d take good personality over speed any day—<br>And if they bring snacks sometimes? Even better.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="6-grateful-for-a-workplace-where-we-can-" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>6. Grateful for a Workplace Where We Can Laugh</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-15T11_43_00.764Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2083"/></figure>

<p>Unexpected things happen—both in work and in life.<br>But behind every surprise, there’s usually someone moving to fix it.</p>

<p>That day, maybe she was just protecting herself, or maybe she truly cared about the team.<br>Either way, nobody was left stranded.</p>

<p>So the next time something happens (not that I’m calling it),<br>I’ll start by laughing, and then figure it out.</p>

<p>Because if we can laugh together—that’s already a form of contribution to others.</p>
