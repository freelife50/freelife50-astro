<h2 id="the-kid-who-dreamed-of-beam-sabers" class="wp-block-heading">🕹️ The Kid Who Dreamed of Beam Sabers</h2>

<p>I first watched Mobile Suit Gundam as an elementary school student.<br>The battles between Amuro and Char, those spectacular mobile suit dogfights — I was hooked every week, wide-eyed at the screen.<br>"Beam sabers are so cool," I'd think. That was basically my whole world for a while.</p>

<p>More than 40 years later. Never thought I'd find myself watching a new Gundam series again.</p>

<p>April 14, 2025.<br>I tuned in for Episode 6 of the new series <em>Gundam GQuuuuuuX</em> (known in Japanese as "Zieg Axis").<br>The episode title: "Assassination Plot Against Kycilia" — and even before the episode started, something stirred in me.</p>

<p>"Wait — Kycilia? Wasn't she the one Char shot in the head with a bazooka at the end of the original series?"</p>

<p>There wasn't much combat in this episode. Honestly, a little underwhelming on that front.<br>But what lodged itself in my mind afterward wasn't a battle at all — it was <strong>a red book</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="nyaans-room-and-zeon-institute-of-techno" class="wp-block-heading">📕 Nyaan's Room and "Zeon Institute of Technology"</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/5922d81a44776a6d5839d6d0eeaa05a6.png" alt="" class="wp-image-710"/></figure>

<p>It caught my eye almost by accident — a red book in the corner of the scene.<br>On its spine: <em>Zeon Institute of Technology</em>.</p>

<p>I actually said out loud: "Wait, that looks just like an exam prep guide."</p>

<p>(In Japan, university entrance exam guidebooks are infamous for their red covers — everyone calls them "akabon," the red books. Seeing the Zeon version of one was surreal.)</p>

<p>Zeon has a university?<br>Practical labs on Zaku maintenance? Lectures on psycommu theory?<br>My imagination wouldn't stop running.</p>

<p>And then a thought hit me: <strong>Could Kycilia have founded this university?</strong></p>

<p>In the original series, Kycilia Zabi was at the center of Newtype research in the Zeon military.<br>If she had survived — if that fatal moment at the end of the One Year War had gone differently — <strong>the idea of channeling military technology into academic study feels entirely plausible for her.</strong></p>

<p>That little red book made the world feel terrifyingly real.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="the-timeline-where-char-didnt-pull-the-t" class="wp-block-heading">🔁 The Timeline Where Char Didn't Pull the Trigger</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/91f3223ad4935cf8506ad6d53aeefa59.png" alt="" class="wp-image-706"/></figure>

<p>In the original Gundam, Kycilia shoots her brother Gihren to seize control of the Zabi family — and in that same moment, Char fires a bazooka into her shuttle.<br>She's gone in an instant.</p>

<p>But what if she hadn't been?</p>

<p>GQuuuuuuX seems to be set in exactly that kind of alternate timeline — a "what if" world where that moment played out differently.</p>

<p>In this version of history:<br>Zeon survived the war.<br>Newtype research continued. Enhanced human development carried on.<br><strong>And Zeon Institute of Technology exists.</strong></p>

<p>Psyco Gundams as standard military assets. Human beings weaponized without a second thought.<br>A world already completely rewritten.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="what-a-non-fighting-gundam-taught-me" class="wp-block-heading">🌀 What a Non-Fighting Gundam Taught Me</h2>

<p>Honestly, the lack of combat left me a little hollow at first.<br>But something else came through in its place — a kind of clarity.</p>

<p>Without the noise of battle, the characters' inner lives became sharper, more visible.</p>

<p>At one point, a character named Machu mutters almost to herself:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"I have to rewrite it again…"</p>
</blockquote>

<p>That line wasn't just about the story.<br><strong>It felt like it was aimed at us.</strong> At me.</p>

<p>Today's Gundam isn't asking "why do we fight?" so much as "what are we living for?"<br>And somewhere in that question, I found something shifting in myself too.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="what-in-your-life-would-you-rewrite" class="wp-block-heading">📝 What in Your Life Would You Rewrite?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/1b580ddbc9da138d7101aa16f6f54311.png" alt="" class="wp-image-708"/></figure>

<p>Even Gundam — the canonical timeline itself — is being rewritten and pushed forward.</p>

<p>Which means our lives can be restarted too, doesn't it?</p>

<p>That's what this episode said to me, quietly.</p>

<p>Is there a page in your own life — something you've long left as-is — that maybe, just maybe, is ready to be rewritten?</p>
