<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><em>I Used to Think Beam Sabers Were Everything</em></p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>I first watched the original Gundam as a kid, mesmerized by Amuro and Char’s fierce battles.<br>Beam sabers? So cool. I’d sit glued to the TV.</p>

<p>Decades passed. Then on April 14, 2025, I watched Episode 6 of <em>Gundam GQuuuuuuX</em>.<br>The title? “The Assassination of Kycilia.”</p>

<p>“Wait—Kycilia? Didn’t Char blow her away in the original?”</p>

<p>Not much action this time. A bit disappointing, honestly.</p>

<p>But what stuck with me… was that <strong>red university guidebook</strong>.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><em>The Red Book in Nyaan’s Room</em></p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/5922d81a44776a6d5839d6d0eeaa05a6.png" alt="" class="wp-image-710"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Just a glimpse of a red book on the screen—“Zeon Institute of Technology.”</p>

<p>And my imagination ran wild.<br>Zaku maintenance workshops? Psycommu lectures?</p>

<p>Could it be… Kycilia’s doing?</p>

<p>She led research into Newtypes in the original timeline.<br>If she had survived, she might’ve founded a whole institution to train youth in war science.</p>

<p>One red book suggested an entire rewritten world.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><em>What If Char Had Let Her Live?</em></p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/91f3223ad4935cf8506ad6d53aeefa59.png" alt="" class="wp-image-706"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>In the original, Kycilia was gunned down by Char right after taking out Gihren.</p>

<p>But what if he hadn’t?</p>

<p>GQuuuuuuX might be exploring that very possibility.</p>

<p>A world where Zeon endures.<br>Where Newtype experiments don’t stop.<br>Where the <em>Zeon Institute of Technology</em> becomes real.</p>

<p>This isn’t just a new story—it’s a <strong>rewriting</strong> of everything.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><em>This “Non-Fighting” Gundam Made Me Think</em></p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>I missed the battles.</p>

<p>But in that silence, I found something else: reflection.</p>

<p>When Machu muttered, <em>“We’ve got to rewrite it again,”</em><br>it wasn’t just about the story.</p>

<p>It was about us.</p>

<p>Gundam now isn’t just about war—it’s questioning the meaning behind it.</p>

<p>And maybe I’ve changed, too.</p>

<p>After all, even in Gundam… the past can be rewritten to forge a new future.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p><em>So What Part of Your Life Will You Rewrite?</em></p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/1b580ddbc9da138d7101aa16f6f54311.png" alt="" class="wp-image-708"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Even Gundam rewrites its own canon to keep moving forward.</p>

<p>So maybe… we can, too.</p>

<p>This episode felt like an invitation.</p>

<p>Is there a page in your life waiting to be rewritten?</p>

<p>If so—go ahead.</p>

<p>It’s never too late to restart.</p>
</blockquote>
