<p>■<strong>What This Article Is About</strong></p>

<p>In Japan, vending machines are everywhere — on street corners, by rivers, even in quiet neighborhoods. They don’t just sell soda; sometimes you’ll find coffee, soup, or even bananas.</p>

<p>One summer day, after swimming at the pool, I stumbled upon something I had never seen before: <strong>Sparkling Aquarius.</strong></p>

<p>I’m usually a beer guy, but this <strong>first encounter</strong> with a carbonated version of Aquarius brought me back to a feeling I thought I’d lost — <strong>youth itself, for just 150 yen.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">After the Pool, I Grabbed One Can</h2>

<p>Normally, I’d say, “Good work, time for a beer!”<br>But since I usually ride my motorbike or drive to the pool, <strong>beer would mean jail time (and social death).</strong></p>

<p>So there I was, standing in front of a vending machine, thirsty. <em>“I’ll drink beer at home anyway…”</em> I thought.<br>But that one small moment of thirst led me to something new.</p>

<p>There it was:<br><strong>“Aquarius Sparkling – 150 yen.”</strong><br>Wait, <strong>Aquarius with carbonation?!</strong><br>My heart skipped a beat.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">First Sip — Boom! Youth in a Can</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-19T11_16_30.932Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2128"/></figure>

<p>I hesitated.<br>“150 yen? I could buy a beer instead.”<br>“What if it tastes bad? I’ll regret it all day.”</p>

<p>But curiosity won, and I pressed the button.<br>One sip later… <strong>BAM! Ice-cold bubbles shot through my body.</strong><br>“Whoa, this isn’t losing to beer at all!”<br>(Of course, almost any soda feels good when you’re thirsty, but still.)</p>

<p>No alcohol buzz, but the refreshing kick was <strong>exactly like the feeling after high school club activities — pure youth in a can.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Is Sparkling Aquarius?</h2>

<p>‘Sparkling Aquarius’ is a sports soda with a refreshing grapefruit flavor.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Contains <strong>1,400mg of citric acid</strong> (sounds a lot, but actually only 1.4g — less than half a teaspoon. A classic case of <strong>number magic</strong>!)</li>

<li>Light sugar and sodium for hydration</li>

<li>Low-calorie: <strong>19 kcal per 100ml</strong></li>

<li>Can size: 350ml</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Sunset, Super Cub, and a Daydream</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250817_091312968.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2120"/></figure>

<p>…Totally a delusion.<br>But still, in that moment, it somehow felt real.<br>(Yes, this is how middle-aged men create their own “movie scenes.”)</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Childhood Flashback</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-19T11_53_57.187Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2135"/></figure>

<p>It reminded me of my childhood summer vacation.<br>Tired after hours in the city pool, my friends and I would pedal our bikes home along the riverbank, laughing:<br>“Let’s swim again tomorrow!”<br>That evening glow, that warm breeze — <strong>it felt exactly the same now, even though I was riding a motorbike instead of a bicycle.</strong></p>

<p>For a second, I almost teared up.<br><strong>150 yen for sparkling water, and I’m crying? Best value ever.</strong></p>
