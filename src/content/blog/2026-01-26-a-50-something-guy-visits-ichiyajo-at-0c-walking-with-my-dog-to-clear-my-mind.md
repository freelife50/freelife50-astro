<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Was Ichiyajo really built in one night?</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3079,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->

<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3083,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2375-768x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3083"/></figure>

<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3084,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I visited <strong>Ishigakiyama Ichiyajo Historic Park</strong>.<br>The name makes you think:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“A castle in one night? That’s impossible.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In reality, it took about 80 days to build.<br>The trees were left standing to hide the construction,<br>then cut down all at once after completion.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>From the Hojo side, it must have looked like:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“Wait… there was nothing yesterday, and now there’s a castle?!”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A classic Hideyoshi move.<br>Not brute force, but psychological impact.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Honestly, it reminded me of modern work.</strong><br>Sometimes, it’s not logic—but presentation—that moves things forward.<br>As a middle-aged manager, that realization comes with a bitter smile.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<p>Not flashy.<br>Not efficient.<br>A bit muddy, honestly.</p>

<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3086,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3087,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2352-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3087"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3088,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2387-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3088"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Dogs are allowed, but this isn’t a casual stroll—<br>it’s more like a <strong>small adventure</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There are lots of stone steps and uneven paths.<br>Around the stone walls especially, watch your footing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Important notes for dog owners</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Keep the leash short</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Carts are basically impossible</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Don’t let dogs climb stone walls</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Stop and wait in narrow spots</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Always pick up after your dog</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Medium-sized dogs like Shiba Inus will enjoy it.<br>For senior dogs or those with joint issues,<br><strong>staying near flatter areas is safer.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2363-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3094"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This needs emphasis.<br>Mountain top + sea wind = brutal cold.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Winter tips</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Windproof outerwear is essential</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Gloves help (especially for handling the leash)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Short-haired dogs may need clothes</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>A hot drink is a lifesaver</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If it’s this cold in winter,<br><strong>summer will likely be hot, humid, and buggy—especially ticks.</strong><br>Spring or autumn is probably the best season.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Summer warnings: pit vipers and ticks</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3095,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2353-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3095"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3096,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2378-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3096"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There are actual <strong>“Mamushi (pit viper) caution”</strong> signs in the park.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Tall grass, humidity, and shade—<br><strong>it feels like the kind of place snakes could appear.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And for dog owners, ticks are a serious concern.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Keep dogs out of tall grass</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Avoid paths with poor visibility</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Do a full tick check after walking</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The scenery is beautiful, but <strong>your feet need to stay realistic.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Parking and toilets (very important)</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3098,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2335-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3098"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Parking is free.<br>But weekends get crowded, especially with Yoroizuka Farm visitors.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Arriving before 10 a.m. is safest.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And this is crucial, especially for people in their 50s:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Toilets are only near the parking lot or Ninomaru.</strong><br>Once you start walking, <strong>there are none.</strong><br>Go first. Seriously.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
