<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why I decided to visit Ichiyajo</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2338.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3076"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2339.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3077"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2372.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3078"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>Lately, I’ve been watching <em>Sanada Maru</em> at night, beer in hand.<br>Seeing the Sanada clan squeezed between powerful forces, unable to move freely, I thought:</p>

<p><strong>“Wait… isn’t this kind of me right now?”</strong></p>

<p>At work too—up and down, left and right.<br>No matter where you look, there’s no clear right answer.<br>It’s not the Sengoku period, but honestly, <strong>it feels like one sometimes</strong> (laughs).</p>

<p>So I thought,<br><strong>“Let’s go breathe some Sengoku air for a bit.”</strong><br>(With that old “Ah, Kyoto” commercial playing in my head.)</p>

<p>That’s how I ended up at Ichiyajo—<br>with my dog, just to clear my head.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Was Ichiyajo really built in one night?</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2370.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3079"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2375.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3083"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2376.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3084"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>I visited <strong>Ishigakiyama Ichiyajo Historic Park</strong>.<br>The name makes you think:</p>

<p><strong>“A castle in one night? That’s impossible.”</strong></p>

<p>In reality, it took about 80 days to build.<br>The trees were left standing to hide the construction,<br>then cut down all at once after completion.</p>

<p>From the Hojo side, it must have looked like:</p>

<p><strong>“Wait… there was nothing yesterday, and now there’s a castle?!”</strong></p>

<p>A classic Hideyoshi move.<br>Not brute force, but psychological impact.</p>

<p><strong>Honestly, it reminded me of modern work.</strong><br>Sometimes, it’s not logic—but presentation—that moves things forward.<br>As a middle-aged manager, that realization comes with a bitter smile.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What I felt looking at the stone walls</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2344.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3085"/></figure>

<p>The stone walls here use <strong>Nozura-zumi</strong>—<br>natural stones stacked almost as-is.</p>

<p>They’re rough, uneven, full of gaps.<br>And yet…</p>

<p><strong>They’ve stood firm for over 400 years.</strong></p>

<p>That hit me hard.</p>

<p><strong>“Clumsy, imperfect… but still standing.”</strong><br>That’s basically life in your 50s, isn’t it?</p>

<p>Not flashy.<br>Not efficient.<br>A bit muddy, honestly.</p>

<p>But still holding everything up.<br><strong>Never thought I’d relate to a stone wall</strong> (laughs).</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Walking with a dog: honest impressions</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2337.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3086"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2352.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3087"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2387.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3088"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>Dogs are allowed, but this isn’t a casual stroll—<br>it’s more like a <strong>small adventure</strong>.</p>

<p>There are lots of stone steps and uneven paths.<br>Around the stone walls especially, watch your footing.</p>

<p><strong>Important notes for dog owners</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Keep the leash short</li>

<li>Carts are basically impossible</li>

<li>Don’t let dogs climb stone walls</li>

<li>Stop and wait in narrow spots</li>

<li>Always pick up after your dog</li>
</ul>

<p>Medium-sized dogs like Shiba Inus will enjoy it.<br>For senior dogs or those with joint issues,<br><strong>staying near flatter areas is safer.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The view that made everything connect</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2379.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3090"/></figure>

<p>From the observation deck, the view of Sagami Bay opens wide.<br>On a clear day, you can even spot Odawara Castle below.</p>

<p><strong>“So this is where Hideyoshi looked down from…”</strong></p>

<p>Suddenly, history became three-dimensional.<br>The earlier story clicked into place.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Reality check: it was freezing (felt like 0°C)</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2377.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3091"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2356.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3092"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2363.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3094"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>This needs emphasis.<br>Mountain top + sea wind = brutal cold.</p>

<p><strong>Winter tips</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Windproof outerwear is essential</li>

<li>Gloves help (especially for handling the leash)</li>

<li>Short-haired dogs may need clothes</li>

<li>A hot drink is a lifesaver</li>
</ul>

<p>If it’s this cold in winter,<br><strong>summer will likely be hot, humid, and buggy—especially ticks.</strong><br>Spring or autumn is probably the best season.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Summer warnings: pit vipers and ticks</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2353.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3095"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2378.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3096"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>There are actual <strong>“Mamushi (pit viper) caution”</strong> signs in the park.</p>

<p>Tall grass, humidity, and shade—<br><strong>it feels like the kind of place snakes could appear.</strong></p>

<p>And for dog owners, ticks are a serious concern.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Keep dogs out of tall grass</li>

<li>Avoid paths with poor visibility</li>

<li>Do a full tick check after walking</li>
</ul>

<p>The scenery is beautiful, but <strong>your feet need to stay realistic.</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Driving access: fine for me, but…</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2334.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3097"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2336.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3104"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>I expected narrow roads, and while I was fine,<br>there are tight sections through mandarin orange fields.</p>

<p>If you drive:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>A large vehicle</li>

<li>Or you’re not confident behind the wheel</li>
</ul>

<p>…it might feel stressful. Just something to keep in mind.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Parking and toilets (very important)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2335.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3098"/></figure>

<p>Parking is free.<br>But weekends get crowded, especially with Yoroizuka Farm visitors.</p>

<p><strong>Arriving before 10 a.m. is safest.</strong></p>

<p>And this is crucial, especially for people in their 50s:</p>

<p><strong>Toilets are only near the parking lot or Ninomaru.</strong><br>Once you start walking, <strong>there are none.</strong><br>Go first. Seriously.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Yoroizuka Farm: planning matters with a dog</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2388.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3099"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2389.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3100"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2395.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3102"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>Dogs are allowed <strong>only on the terrace</strong>.</p>

<p>It’s self-service, and leash hooks are limited and hard to find.<br>So honestly—</p>

<p><strong>Solo dog visits can be tricky</strong> (laughs).</p>

<p>Best options:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Go with someone</li>

<li>Visit during quiet hours</li>

<li>Bring a sling or carrier</li>
</ul>

<p>The roll cake is served on a wooden board, kamaboko-style—<br>very Odawara.<br>Local citrus flavors answer the question:</p>

<p><strong>“Why sweets in Odawara?”</strong></p>

<p>(<em>Often closed on Tuesdays—check the official site before going.</em>)</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Final thoughts</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/IMG_2391.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3101"/></figure>

<p>Stone walls.<br>Walking with my dog.<br>Heavy breathing.<br>Ending with something sweet.</p>

<p>That’s all it was—<br>and yet, <strong>my mind felt a little more balanced.</strong></p>

<p>Historic places have always watched people struggle with:</p>

<p><strong>“How to endure when there’s no right answer.”</strong></p>

<p>We’re the same.<br>Even when answers don’t come,<br>even when we’re pulled in every direction—</p>

<p><strong>If we don’t collapse, that’s already enough.</strong></p>

<p>Cold weather, uneven paths, snake warnings and all—<br><strong>it was a day worth taking.</strong></p>
