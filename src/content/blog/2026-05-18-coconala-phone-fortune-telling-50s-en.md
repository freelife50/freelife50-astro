<h2 id="osaka">That day in Osaka, when I was 20</h2>

<p>A friend invited me, and we went without thinking too much about it. I think it was somewhere in a busy part of Osaka.</p>

<p>At the time, I just thought it might make a good story.</p>

<p>I listened while my friend was told he was smart. Then I wondered what the fortune teller would say about me.</p>

<p>The answer was “trouble with women.”</p>

<p>And then: “Make more male friends.”</p>

<p>Whether that was accurate, I still do not know. My life is not over yet.</p>

<p>But from that day, fortune telling became one of those things I quietly noticed. Not only because of whether it was right or wrong, but because it showed me myself from an angle I would not normally use.</p>

<h2 id="choose">How should you choose a fortune teller?</h2>

<p>This may be the hardest part.</p>

<p>You can compare categories, fees, reviews, and availability. I would look at these points:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Number of reviews, because more reviews give you more context</li>
<li>Review content, especially whether people felt listened to</li>
<li>Price per minute, because higher rates matter if the call runs long</li>
<li>Consultation topics, such as relationships, work, family, or personal worries</li>
<li>Whether the seller is available now or works by reservation</li>
</ul>

<p>Instead of searching for “the one who will definitely be right,” I think it is healthier to search for someone who seems able to listen to your specific worry calmly.</p>

<h2 id="prepare">What to decide before calling</h2>

<p>This is more important than it looks.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Budget: how much you are willing to spend. For the first call, staying inside the coupon range is safest</li>
<li>Time: how many minutes you plan to talk</li>
<li>Main question: summarize what you want to ask in one sentence</li>
</ul>

<p>When you are worried, talking can keep going longer than you expected.</p>

<p>Deciding your time and budget first makes it much easier to avoid regret.</p>

<h2 id="caution">A few important cautions</h2>

<p>No fortune-telling service can guarantee that something will come true.</p>

<p>Japan's consumer agencies have also warned about trouble involving fortune-telling sites and apps. So I would not use phone fortune telling as a place to outsource major life decisions. I would use it as a conversation that helps organize feelings.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Use it as a place to talk about something you cannot easily say elsewhere</li>
<li>Use it as a third-person perspective when your thoughts are tangled</li>
<li>Do not use it as a substitute for medical, legal, or investment decisions</li>
<li>Set a time limit before you begin</li>
</ul>

<p>If the result is “I feel a little lighter” or “I noticed something I had not put into words,” that may be the useful part.</p>
