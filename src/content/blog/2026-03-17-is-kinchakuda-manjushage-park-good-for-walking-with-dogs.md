<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Basic Information About Kinchakuda Manjushage Park</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3291,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_2964.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3291"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Location &amp; Access</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The park is located in <strong>Koma-Hongo, Hidaka City, Saitama Prefecture</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s about a <strong>15-minute walk from Koma Station</strong> on the Seibu Ikebukuro Line.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The park sits on a flat area surrounded by the <strong>Koma River</strong>,<br>and the total area is about <strong>22 hectares</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Seasonal Highlights</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I visited in <strong>March</strong>, which is the off-season.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It was very quiet.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For a slow riverside walk, it was actually the perfect time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Spring → Cherry blossoms</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Autumn → Red spider lilies (Manjushage)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>After that → Cosmos flowers</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It seems to be a park where the scenery changes beautifully with the seasons.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Entrance Fee &amp; Parking</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I visited, <strong>admission was free</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>However, during the red spider lily season,<br>there may be an entrance fee of <strong>200–500 yen</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Parking costs <strong>500 yen per day</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Parking hours are <strong>7:00 AM – 5:00 PM</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Important Notes When Visiting with Dogs</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If you bring your dog:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>A <strong>leash is required</strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Please follow basic etiquette such as cleaning up after your dog</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There is also something else worth knowing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Red spider lilies (Manjushage) are toxic to dogs.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>While walking, I suddenly thought:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“What if Momiji starts sniffing or digging around the flowers or bulbs…?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That thought made me a little nervous.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If your dog tends to eat things off the ground,<br>you should be especially careful.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">I Only Realized Later That This Place Is Famous</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3297,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Gemini_Generated_Image_w0xifnw0xifnw0xi.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3297"/></figure>

<p>To be completely honest,</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I simply thought:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<p>“This is a big, comfortable park.”</p>

<p>But after I got home and looked it up,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I learned something interesting.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kinchakuda is a land area surrounded by the <strong>Koma River</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The shape resembles a traditional Japanese money pouch called a <strong>“kinchaku.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s where the name <strong>Kinchakuda</strong> comes from.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And part of the park is famous for its</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>red spider lily fields.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The number of flowers?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>About <strong>5 million</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I read that number, I literally said:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Wait… five million?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Because I visited outside the flowering season,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>the area was just a quiet forest.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But from <strong>mid-September to early October</strong>,<br>the entire forest floor turns bright red with spider lilies.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People say it looks like a <strong>red carpet of flowers</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After learning that, I thought:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“So that quiet forest becomes that incredible view…?”</p>
