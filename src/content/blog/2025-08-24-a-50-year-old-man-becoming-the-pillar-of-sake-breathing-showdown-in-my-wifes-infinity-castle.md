<p>■<strong>Prologue – The Movie Theater, Another Battlefield</strong></p>

<p>It had been a while since I stepped into the darkness of a cinema. My mission: to witness the Demon Slayer Corps’ final battle in the <strong>Infinity Castle</strong>.<br>The explosions, the falling warriors, the roars of the Upper Moons... Dolby Atmos shook the room so hard it felt like I was thrown into the castle myself.</p>

<p>──Or so it should have been.</p>

<p>But reality? A kid next to me showered my lap with popcorn while his parents were glued to the screen. Those kernels rained down like rubble from the collapsing Infinity Castle.</p>

<p>Inside my head, I screamed: <strong>“Dad, please do something!!”</strong></p>

<p>I couldn’t fully focus. My breathing technique shifted from <em>Total Concentration</em> to something else entirely: <strong>“Total Concentration – Breathing of Endurance.”</strong><strong><br></strong> By the time I staggered out, I was exhausted, hollow, and oddly frustrated.</p>

<p>That’s when it hit me:<br>If I can’t be a Hashira of Demon Slayer... maybe I can at least become the <strong>Pillar of Sake Breathing</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Chapter 1 – First Form: “Evening Drink Awakening” — The DX Nichirin Sword Descends</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_07_26.887Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2160"/></figure>

<p>A Hashira needs a partner. And what’s a pillar without a sword?<br>I rushed home and searched for Bandai’s <strong>DX Nichirin Sword</strong>.</p>

<p>炭治郎’s model could switch blades between <em>Water Breathing</em> and <em>Hinokami Kagura</em>. Over 50 sound effects packed inside. It was meant for kids, yet it <strong>stabbed right through my 50-year-old heart</strong>.</p>

<p>It gleamed like whiskey poured into a glass, calling to me.</p>

<p><strong>DX Nichirin Sword Facts (for 50-somethings):</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Length: about 58cm — shorter than a sake bottle, perfect for one-hand swings.<br></li>

<li>Power: 2 AAA batteries — no charging needed, ready even when tipsy.<br></li>

<li>3 modes: Day (<em>Water</em>), Night (<em>Hinokami</em>), Party (<em>Action Mode</em>).<br></li>

<li>50+ sounds: Enough to send you right back to boyhood.<br></li>
</ul>

<p>And so began the endless cycle: add to cart → remove → add again.<br>A training ground of <strong>“Total Concentration – Clicking Breathing”</strong>.<br>This was my own private Infinity Castle.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Chapter 2 – Second Form: “Stumbling Drunk Dance” — No More Excuses</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_20_19.090Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2164"/></figure>

<p>If my kid were still in grade school, I could’ve said:<br><strong>“Hey buddy, Dad bought this for you! Let’s play together!”</strong></p>

<p>But the truth? It’s me who wants it most.<br>A Saturday night, slightly drunk, swinging the sword while yelling,<br><strong>“Sake Breathing! First Form!”</strong></p>

<p>Then my wife’s thunderous voice:<br><strong>“Shut it! Enough already!”</strong></p>

<p>There’s no escape. My son’s an adult now, so the “for the kids” excuse has vanished.<br>In reality, it’s nothing more than a <strong>public execution at home</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Chapter 3 – Third Form: “The Merciless White Eyes” — The Strongest Demon Appears</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_15_41.512Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2163"/></figure>

<p>Clicking “Buy Now” under the influence... then I feel it:<br><strong>her cold stare stabbing into my back.</strong></p>

<p>That gaze is no different from <strong>Muzan’s Blood Demon Art</strong>.<br>In an instant, the alcohol fades, my heart skips a beat.</p>

<p>I have no counter-breathing.<br>All I can do is sit quietly in seiza, putting the sword away.<br>Because the <strong>strongest demon in my Infinity Castle is my wife</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Chapter 4 – Fourth Form: “Morning-After Regret” — The Drunkard’s Depression</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_23_14.214Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2165"/></figure>

<p>The next morning.<br>I open my phone, only to see: <strong>“Thank you for your order.”</strong></p>

<p>My excitement gone, replaced by the familiar <strong>hangover of shame</strong>.<br>It’s like waking up after sending weird drunk texts to the group chat.</p>

<p><strong>“Did I really... click that? For a toy sword?”</strong><strong><br></strong> The silence is deafening.<br>Cold sweat down my spine.</p>

<p>This, too, is Sake Breathing’s Fourth Form: <strong>“Morning-After Regret.”</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Chapter 5 – Fifth Form: “Wallet Breathing” — The Pain of Knowing It’s Pointless</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_54_28.058Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2166"/></figure>

<p>The final enemy always appears: <strong>the credit card statement.</strong><strong><br></strong> There it is, “Bandai” staring at me.</p>

<p><strong>“What a stupid thing to buy...”</strong><strong><br></strong> And yet... deep down, I whisper:<br><strong>“...but I wanted it.”</strong></p>

<p>This clash between reason and desire—<br>That’s the fate of a 50-year-old man.<br>The Fifth Form: <strong>“Wallet Breathing.”</strong></p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Epilogue – The Conclusion: </strong><strong><em>“I won’t buy it... but I want it!”</em></strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-24T01_59_10.463Z-1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2168"/></figure>

<p>The DX Nichirin Sword isn’t just for kids.<br>Even adults feel their hearts race at its glow and sounds.</p>

<p>But for men in their 50s, one demon always awaits:<br>the <strong>Infinity Castle of Marriage</strong>, guarded by the strongest Hashira of them all — our wives.</p>

<p>My conclusion?<br><strong>“I won’t buy it... but I want it!”</strong></p>

<p>That paradox is the very soul of <strong>Sake Breathing</strong>.<br>And I believe there are many more “Pillars” like me out there.</p>

<li><strong>Time your forms wisely:</strong> Midnight practice risks the wrath of your wife.<br></li>

<li><strong>Find comrades:</strong> Many admit, “I play with it more than my kids.” You’re not alone.<br></li>

<li><strong>Counter the strongest demon:</strong> Buy your wife’s favorite thing alongside.<br></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Secret Art: Negotiation Breathing — Strategies for Wife Boss Battles</strong></h3>
