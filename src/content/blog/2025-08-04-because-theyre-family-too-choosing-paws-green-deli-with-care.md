<p>"Probiotics," "fiber," "gut flora"—these words are everywhere in human health now. But did you know the same logic applies to dogs and cats?</p>

<p>The gut isn’t just about digestion. It's linked to the immune system, mood, and even how nutrients are absorbed.<br>Some call it the “second brain.” And yes, your dog’s poop tells you a lot. If you’re like me, you’ve become a poop sommelier: evaluating shape, scent, and consistency like a trained expert.</p>

<p>So, <strong>feeding the gut right</strong> is no longer a trendy idea—it's a smart strategy.</p>

<p>Even the best food has limits.</p>

<ol class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Is it a complete meal?</strong><strong><br></strong> → Check for “complete and balanced” labels. Many varieties are <em>supplements</em>, not stand-alone meals.<br></li>

<li><strong>Not grain-free</strong><strong><br></strong> → But they use easy-to-digest grains like rice and sweet potatoes. Balance matters more than total exclusion.<br></li>

<li><strong>It </strong><strong><em>is</em></strong><strong> expensive</strong><strong><br></strong> → Maybe not for every day—but great for weekends, special days, or during illness.<br></li>

<li><strong>Results vary</strong><strong><br></strong> → No food works for every dog. Start small and observe.<br></li>
</ol>
