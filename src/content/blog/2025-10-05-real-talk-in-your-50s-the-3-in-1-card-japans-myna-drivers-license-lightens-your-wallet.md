<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>👋 Intro: A 50-something finally catches the digital wave</strong></h2>

<p>Hi there. I’m a guy in my 50s living in Kanagawa.<br>I’ve lived through paper-heavy Showa, PC-internet Heisei, and now the AI-flavored Reiwa.</p>

<p>Honestly, I’m not a super early adopter—but I’m <strong>curious enough</strong>. I like peeking under the hood and thinking, “Huh, so that’s how it works.”<br>What I’m <strong>not</strong> fond of is half-baked systems. If we’re doing it, <strong>let me finish it on my phone</strong>.</p>

<p>“My Number card? If we’re going there, just <strong>merge it with my driver’s license and health insurance</strong> already…”<br>“Mobile pay? I’ll still use cash, but if it’s convenient, sure—why not.”</p>

<p>That’s me—<em>a touch</em> interested in the future.<br>And this time, I finally tried the thing everyone’s talking about: <strong>the MyNa Driver’s License</strong>—the 3-in-1 card that combines your <strong>driver’s license + My Number card + health insurance card</strong>.</p>

<p>“If I’ll need it eventually, better to <strong>get used to it early</strong>.”<br>Here’s my <strong>no-sugar-coating</strong> field report from a 50-something.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💳 What is the “MyNa Driver’s License”? (In 3 lines)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/20251005_1600_Futuristic-Identity-Integration_simple_compose_01k6sjntgzf31a7pvpgv0d1syk.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2430"/></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>It’s an <strong>IC card</strong> that unifies your <strong>driver’s license, My Number card, and health insurance card</strong>.<br></li>

<li><strong>Full rollout in March 2025</strong> nationwide: one card for government, healthcare, and ID.<br></li>

<li><strong>Phone-based versions</strong> are coming, so we may eventually ditch physical cards altogether.<br></li>
</ul>

<p>The “cards vanish from your wallet” era—like sci-fi—is basically on your doorstep.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🆕 The one-line verdict</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/20251005_1601_Cinematic-Card-Dilemma_simple_compose_01k6sjq4eze75sv5zvax2e1fp9.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2431"/></figure>

<p><strong>Biggest upside ☀️</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>No more <strong>multi-office paperwork hopping</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li>Government / medical / ID all in <strong>one card</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>Online renewal courses</strong> for good/standard drivers<br></li>

<li>Slimmer wallet, simpler procedures<br></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Biggest downside ⚠️</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>If you <strong>lose it</strong>, daily life can stall<br></li>

<li><strong>Centralized personal data</strong> = bigger blast radius if leaked<br></li>

<li><strong>Not every service</strong> supports it yet<br></li>

<li><strong>Expiry dates aren’t printed</strong> on the card (stored in the chip)<br></li>
</ul>

<p>👉 <strong>Bottom line:</strong> The convenience is real—but <strong>careful management is non-negotiable</strong>.<br>“Yes, it’s handy… but if I drop this one card, I’m toast, right?”<br>—Exactly. That tension <strong>is</strong> the MyNa Driver’s License.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💡The “Pros” (from experience)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_29_54.336Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2432"/></figure>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💳 One card = lighter wallet, easier life</strong></h3>

<p> Three cards down to one. My old long wallet used to bulge; the zipper protested. Now it’s <strong>slim</strong>. That little bit of <strong>physical breathing room</strong> is surprisingly uplifting.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🏠 One-stop changes for address/name</strong></h3>

<p>　This felt revolutionary.<br>Before, a move meant: city office → license center → workplace paperwork…<br>Now? <strong>City office once</strong>. Done. No more “bureaucracy relay race.”</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💻 Online renewal courses (for good/standard drivers)</strong></h3>

<p>　Do it at home. Feels like watching YouTube. No more 2-hour round trips and cramped classrooms.<br><em>(Confession: I had a violation, so it was a 2-hour course for me… and online wasn’t available this time. Please follow traffic rules—says the guy who has no moral high ground!)<br></em> <em>Note: For your first unification renewal, online might not apply; usually available from the next renewal.</em></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💰 A small fee break</strong></h3>

<p>　It’s not huge, but at our age <strong>tiny wins</strong> still feel good.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🏦 Smoother ID checks</strong></h3>

<p>　Banks, hospitals, counters—<strong>tap and done</strong>.<br>“Do you have photo ID?”<br>“Yup—<em>beep</em>.”<br>“Thanks.”<br>You’ll get hooked on that speed.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🧓 Pensions get a tad simpler</strong></h3>

<p>　My Number already links to your Basic Pension Number; more steps are moving <strong>online</strong>.<br>As pension stuff gets real for us 50-somethings, <strong>less hassle</strong> matters.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⚠️ The “Cons”</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_32_26.267Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2433"/></figure>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💥 Higher stakes if you lose it</strong></h3>

<p>　Because it’s all in one:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>You might not be able to <strong>drive</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>Healthcare check-in</strong> gets messy<br></li>

<li><strong>Identity checks</strong> stall<br>Result: parts of daily life <strong>pause</strong> until reissue (often <strong>1–2 weeks</strong>). It can affect work. <strong>Wallet care becomes a lifeline</strong>.<br></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🧩 Centralized personal info risk</strong></h3>

<p>　If data leaks, <strong>impact multiplies</strong>. Convenience demands <strong>better security hygiene</strong>.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>⏰ Expiry isn’t printed</strong></h3>

<p>　It’s stored <strong>in the IC chip</strong>, viewable via app/reader.<br>For those of us who like to <strong>see it at a glance</strong>, that’s a little unsettling at first.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🏢 Not every private service supports it</strong></h3>

<p>　“Please use your MyNa Driver’s License.”<br>“Your… what now?”<br>Yeah, that conversation still happens. <strong>Adoption takes time.</strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🚶My slightly “meh” memories with MyNa Health Insurance</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_40_18.108Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2435"/></figure>

<p>Confession time.<br>I linked My Number and my health insurance <strong>2–3 years ago</strong>. I thought, “Goodbye plastic insurance card—<strong>future unlocked!</strong>”<br>Reality? Mixed.</p>

<p>Internal medicine: smooth.<br>Pharmacies and dentists:<br>“Sorry, we’re not set up for that yet.”<br>… <em>Seriously?</em></p>

<p>This “supported here, not supported there” problem is <strong>frustrating</strong>.<br>I’m on regular meds for <strong>hypertension and gout</strong>, and pharmacies still say:<br>“Please show both your <strong>MyNa insurance</strong> and your <strong>paper medication record</strong> (お薬手帳).”</p>

<p>I want to ditch the booklet. Forget it and you pay a <strong>reissue fee</strong>.<br>“Can’t this all just live on my phone already?”<br>I know I’m not alone. Every generation probably feels this.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🏛️ The first hurdle—unification at the driving school</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_43_50.549Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2436"/></figure>

<p>I did my switch <strong>during license renewal at a driving school</strong>, not at city hall.</p>

<p>At booking/check-in they ask:<br>“Do you have your My Number card?”<br>Say yes, and you can unify <strong>on the spot</strong> at renewal.</p>

<p>Sounds easy… until the <strong>PIN screen</strong> pops up.</p>

<p><strong>💦 My small panic</strong></p>

<p>“Please insert your My Number card.”<br>I do.<br>“Enter your PIN.”<br>“…Which one? 4 digits? 6? Did I even set this?!”</p>

<p>Cold sweat. Luckily, I had it <strong>saved in my phone’s password manager</strong>. Otherwise, <strong>game over</strong>.</p>

<p>👉 If you’re doing this soon, <strong>hear me out</strong>:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>You’ll need <strong>two</strong> PINs.<br></li>

<li>Without them, <strong>reset takes time</strong>.<br></li>

<li>Worst case, <strong>you can’t finish that day</strong>.<br></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Prep your PINs beforehand.</strong> Please.</p>

<p>The rest took about <strong>30 minutes</strong>. Staff were great.<br>I walked out thinking, “Since when were government procedures this smooth?”</p>

<p><strong>Reference—two key PINs you’ll need:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Signature e-Certificate PIN</strong>: 6–16 <em>alphanumeric</em> characters<br><ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Used for One-Stop Services, MyPortal (マイナポータル), and many e-procedures<br></li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>MyNa Driver’s License PIN</strong>: <strong>4-digit number</strong><strong><br></strong><ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Needed to read license info from the IC chip; <strong>10 wrong attempts locks it</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🛡️ (Important) Loss-risk countermeasures for the 50s</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_49_36.023Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2439"/></figure>

<p>Originally, your My Number card was meant to <strong>stay at home</strong>.<br>Once unified with your license, you’ll <strong>carry it daily</strong>. “I’ll just bring it when needed”?<br>Nope. You’ll <strong>forget</strong>—guaranteed.</p>

<p>So yes, carry it—but <strong>harden your habits</strong>.</p>

<p><strong>My 3 rules</strong></p>

<ol class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Don’t forget your PINs</strong><strong><br></strong> Use <strong>memorable but strong</strong> ones, and store them in a <strong>password manager</strong>—not on random notes.<br></li>

<li><strong>Use a protective IC card case</strong><strong><br></strong> RFID-shielded, water-resistant, shock-resistant. A <strong>strap</strong> helps.<br></li>

<li><strong>Fix its slot in your wallet</strong><strong><br></strong> “Wait, which pocket?”<br>Those <strong>5 seconds of searching</strong> can be costly.<br></li>
</ol>

<p><strong>TL;DR:</strong> “Carry only when necessary” is ideal; <strong>not realistic</strong>.<br>Operate on a <strong>carry-always</strong> assumption, and <strong>minimize risk</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💊 Why I still carry the paper medication record (お薬手帳)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_52_08.522Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2440"/></figure>

<p>Even with MyNa insurance sharing prescriptions, pharmacies still ask for <strong>both</strong>:<br>“MyNa insurance <strong>and</strong> your <strong>paper medication record</strong>, please.”</p>

<p>Why? Because notes like <strong>side effects, allergies, supplements, free-text memos</strong> don’t digitize neatly yet.<br>I want to ditch the booklet—forget it and you risk a <strong>reissue fee</strong>.<br>“Can’t everything just be digital?” I know I’m not the only one.</p>

<p>Nationwide <strong>Medical DX</strong> should fix this eventually. We wait.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🚀 What I’m looking forward to—phone-based MyNa Driver’s License</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Google_AI_Studio_2025-10-05T07_53_21.618Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2441"/></figure>

<p>If your phone can carry your license credentials, you could <strong>leave the card at home</strong>.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Japan targets <strong>fiscal 2026 or later</strong> for phone-based My Number with license functions.<br></li>

<li>License data and e-certs live on your phone, authenticated via <strong>Face/Touch ID</strong>.<br></li>
</ul>

<p>That means:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Far less loss risk</strong><strong><br></strong></li>

<li><strong>No physical card</strong> to carry<br></li>

<li><strong>Convenience + security</strong> can both improve<br></li>
</ul>

<p>“Isn’t losing your phone just as bad?”<br>Fair—but phones have <strong>screen locks</strong> and <strong>remote wipe</strong>. That’s <strong>safer than a card</strong>, in my book.</p>

<p><strong>💴 Pensions, too</strong></p>

<p>My Number is already tied to pensions. Expect:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Benefit status checks</strong>, <strong>status reports</strong>, and <strong>applications</strong> handled <strong>online</strong>.<br>We’re moving from the Basic Pension Number era to <strong>My Number as the identity axis</strong>.<br>Great for efficiency—<strong>and</strong> a reminder to up our <strong>data-hygiene game</strong>.<br></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>⏳ Not live yet</strong></p>

<p>As of <strong>October 2025</strong>, this is <strong>pre-deployment</strong>. Real <strong>phone-based</strong> usage is <strong>2026+</strong>.<br>Until then, think of this as the <strong>final chapter of the plastic card era</strong>—and a bridge to the next one.<br>Our grandkids will laugh: “You carried <em>paper</em> licenses?”</p>
