<h3>🌟 Chapter 5 – Why We Still Love Dragon Quest (Lite Ver.)</h3>

<p>It’s been nearly 40 years since the first <em>Dragon Quest</em> was released,<br>yet fans still celebrate the series—and for good reason.<br>Generations have passed, but the love hasn’t faded.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4>● A Story Told Without Cynicism</h4>

<p>A hero sets out to save the world.<br>It’s a classic plot, but <em>Dragon Quest</em> never runs from it.<br>There’s no need for irony—just kindness, courage, and heart.<br>And that’s what makes it powerful.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4>● Your Own Story</h4>

<p>From the moment you choose your name,<br>you’re not just playing a game—you’re living your own journey.<br>You’re not watching someone’s tale. You’re creating it.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4>● A World That Sparks the Imagination</h4>

<p>In the early days, it was just pixels and text.<br>But even then, a villager’s line could hit you hard,<br>and castle music alone could carry a sense of gravity.</p>

<p>And of course—<strong>the music.</strong><br>The opening fanfare. The overworld theme. The battle cries.<br>Those sounds didn’t just accompany the game—<br>they pulled us into it.</p>

<p>And the most amazing part?<br><strong>That music hasn’t changed.</strong><br>Hearing it today still feels like coming home.</p>

<p>Even with 3D graphics and cinematic scenes,<br><em>Dragon Quest</em> keeps something rare—<br><strong>it doesn’t explain everything.</strong></p>

<p>The hero never speaks.<br>You’re given clues, not conclusions.<br>And that space—those quiet gaps—are where your imagination takes over.</p>

<p><strong>That’s why the world doesn’t age.<br>Because part of it lives inside you.</strong></p>
