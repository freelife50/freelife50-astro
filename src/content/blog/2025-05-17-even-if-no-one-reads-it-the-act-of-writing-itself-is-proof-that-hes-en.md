<h3 id="in-english-summary-reflection">In English (Summary &amp; Reflection)</h3>

<p>I just wanted to start something.</p>

<p>Now that I'm in my 50s, with parenting mostly behind me and work somewhat stable, I began wondering: "Is this really how I want to end things?"</p>

<p>One day, I stumbled upon a video from Ryogakucho’s Liberal Arts University on YouTube. His words stuck with me:</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>"It’s never too late to begin. Starting small can change your life."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>That pushed me toward starting a blog.</p>

<p>It wasn’t easy. I hesitated. I opened the ConoHa WING signup page several times and closed it without clicking. I kept thinking, “Is this really worth it?” But eventually, I clicked. Just three months, I told myself. That was enough to begin.</p>

<p>I don’t want tons of money. Sure, life costs money, and if a side hustle pays off, great. But that’s not the point.</p>

<p>What I don’t want is a life where I do nothing and money just shows up. It feels like a luxurious kind of prison. So even if this fails, if something in my heart shifts, if I leave behind a trace of myself—then it’s worth it.</p>

<p>These days, thinking “What should I write today?” has become a small spice in my life.</p>

<p>Maybe nobody reads it. Still, I keep checking the stats. Maybe, just maybe, someone out there will read my words.</p>

<p>So today too—</p>

<p><strong>I write one more blog post.</strong></p>

<p>It may go unread. It may not reach anyone. But writing reminds me that I’m alive.</p>

<p>And that’s reason enough to keep going.</p>
