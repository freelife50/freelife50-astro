<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🔥第1章：焼肉ポリスってほんまにおるん？</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":798,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/678656ceb7632c57ba7546634bc24996-683x1024.png" alt="" class="wp-image-798"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>調べてみたら、「焼肉ポリス」は冗談でもなんでもなく、<br><strong>実在する接客サービス</strong>の名前やった。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>焼肉ポリスとは、焼肉きんぐの店舗で、<br><strong>赤い腕章をつけたスタッフさん</strong>が、<br>テーブルを巡回してお肉の焼き加減をチェックしてくれる存在。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>たとえば、<br>「これ、もう裏返した方がええですよ」<br>「この部位は弱火ゾーンでじっくり焼くと美味しいです」<br>って感じで、プロ目線のアドバイスをくれるんやと。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>…って、冷静に考えてもやっぱりちょっとおもろいやん？笑</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でも、これが意外と喜ばれてるらしい。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>📍ポイントは、“希望者にだけお節介を焼く”ってところ。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>焼肉ポリスは、<strong>勝手に口出しせえへん。</strong><br>「自分のペースで焼きたいです」ってお客さんには、<br>ちゃんと距離を保ってくれる。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>逆に、「焼き方わからへん」とか「ベストな食べ方知りたい！」って人には、<br>ほんまに丁寧にサポートしてくれるみたい。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>焼肉きんぐの公式では、これを</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>「きんぐ流のおせっかいすぎるおもてなし」<br>って呼んどるらしい。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>焼肉を“監視する”んじゃなくて、<br><strong>焼肉を“守ってくれる”</strong>、それが焼肉ポリスの正体でした。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">📺第2章：なんで今さら話題？トレンド入りの裏側</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":803,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/967862d9db896fbe8da36ff31dc10a92.png" alt="" class="wp-image-803"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>焼肉きんぐ自体はずっとあるし、<br>焼肉ポリスも、2010年代後半には始まってた。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>でも、2025年の春。<br>SNSでもニュースでも「焼肉ポリス」が突然バズった。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>理由は…<strong>キントレ効果</strong>！</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>King &amp; Princeの永瀬廉さんが出演する番組「キントレ」で、<br>実際に焼肉ポリス体験をした様子が放送されたことで、<br>「かわいい」「一生懸命やってる」と話題に。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>その放送直後、X（旧Twitter）では<br>「#焼肉ポリス」がトレンド入り。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>さらに同時期、ビジネスメディアでも<br>「おせっかい接客」が差別化戦略として紹介された。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>テレビ × SNS × ニュースの三連コンボ！</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>こうして「焼肉ポリス」は一気に知名度を上げたわけやね。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🌍 English Afterword｜What I Found Behind "Yakiniku Police"</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I first saw the phrase "Yakiniku Police" trending online and thought,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Wait, what? Is this some kind of cosplay thing?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I had heard of <em>Yakiniku King</em> from coworkers, but honestly,<br>I just thought it was one of those all-you-can-eat places for families.<br>Nothing too fancy—just volume over quality.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But once I started digging into it,<br>I realized there was a lot more going on behind that silly name.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At <em>Yakiniku King</em>, there's actually a staff member with a red armband<br>who gives advice on how to grill your meat properly.<br>They don’t force themselves on you—they wait for your signal.<br>Helpful, respectful, and surprisingly fun.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s called "Osekkai Hospitality"—a kind of proactive care with heart.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So why did it go viral now?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Turns out, a famous idol, Ren Nagase from King &amp; Prince,<br>had a segment on TV where he played the “Yakiniku Police.”<br>Fans loved it.<br>Social media picked it up, business blogs analyzed it—<br>and just like that, the term became a trend.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Then I found out there's a <em>Yakiniku Police Notebook</em> in their app.<br>You get promoted from "Patrol Officer" all the way up to "Superintendent General"<br>just by eating there.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And yes—you can earn things like a <strong>silver tong</strong> or <strong>golden meat scissors</strong>.<br>It’s so weirdly fun that I actually started wanting to try it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People online had mixed opinions—<br>some loved the pro grilling tips, others just wanted to cook in peace.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But that’s the beauty of it:<br><em>You get the help only if you ask for it.</em><br>It’s not pushy. It’s thoughtful.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So yeah, I started off laughing at the name,<br>but in the end, I ended up respecting the whole thing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s not just food. It’s an experience.<br>A little story added to your yakiniku time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And hey—<br><strong>writing this post made me crave yakiniku like crazy.</strong><br>I might just head there tonight… with an empty stomach and an open mind.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
