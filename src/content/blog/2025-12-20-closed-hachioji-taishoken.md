<li>Those who recognize that “kind of scary, but somehow lovable” shop owner<br></li>

<li>Anyone who has a favorite place that overlaps with their own life story<br></li>
</ul>

<p><strong><span class="fz-24px">Conclusion</span></strong></p>

<p><strong>A single bowl of ramen<br>was like adding just the right oil<br>to the worn-out engine of my heart.</strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Hachioji Taishoken Has Finally Closed… 😢</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_q38mvmq38mvmq38m.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2929"/></figure>

<p>December 19, 2025.<br>Hachioji Taishoken quietly closed its doors after 23 years.</p>

<p>Just a short walk from Keio-Hachioji Station,<br>it was a place that had simply <em>always been there</em><em><br></em> since I was in my 30s.</p>

<p>On days when work wore me down,<br>or when my mood sank for no clear reason,<br>seeing that yellow sign made me think,<br>“Well… I’ll at least eat until I’m full.”</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Choosing the Limited-Time Miso Ramen — On Purpose</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/PXL_20251214_083741949.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2926"/></figure>

<p>On the final day, what I chose was<br>the limited-time <strong>miso ramen</strong>.</p>

<p>Standing in front of the ticket machine,<br>my finger stopped for just a moment.</p>

<p>The usual Chinese-style ramen.<br>The taste I’d had dozens of times.<br>I even stepped aside and let a younger guy go first,<br>just to think for a second.</p>

<p>“It’s the last time…”</p>

<p>That’s when I pressed <em>miso</em>.</p>

<p>As you get older,<br>you start wanting your choices to mean something.<br>The fear of failure,<br>and still wanting to take one small step forward.</p>

<p>Maybe that’s something you only understand at this age.<br>Or maybe not.<br>Who knows? (laughs)</p>

<p>The bowl that arrived… honestly, words weren’t necessary.</p>

<p>Through the steam,<br>it felt like I could see 23 years of history.</p>

<p>The moment I took the first sip,<br>I thought naturally,<br>“Yeah… this is a soup you finish to the last drop.”</p>

<p>Rich miso, a gentle sweetness.<br>Full-bodied, but never heavy.<br>The familiar thick noodles tasted of wheat with every bite.</p>

<p>…To be honest,<br>when I caught the smell of the guy next to me eating the regular ramen,<br>my heart wavered just a little. (laughs)</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/PXL_20251214_084600179.MP_.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2927" style="aspect-ratio:0.7509870901912071;width:398px;height:auto"/></figure>

<p><em>sigh</em><em><br></em> Thank you for the meal.</p>

<p>Result: soup fully finished.</p>

<p>From a health perspective,<br>this is probably the kind of thing I’d get scolded for.<br>Blood pressure, salt intake…<br>I chose not to see those things today.</p>

<p>From tomorrow, I’ll live like a herbivore for a week.<br>Vegetables only.<br>…Probably. (laughs)</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Tough-Looking Old Man Behind the Counter</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_k5pdxrk5pdxrk5pd.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2931"/></figure>

<p>The owner was, frankly, scary.</p>

<p>Bad manners? No mercy.<br>Young guys fiddling with their phones while eating?<br>A sharp “Eat properly.”</p>

<p>In today’s world,<br>his online reviews would definitely be a mess.</p>

<p>One look from those eyes<br>and your back straightens up,<br>like a student facing a surprise test.</p>

<p>Even the sharp <em>“BASH!”</em> sound of draining noodles<br>felt like being scolded.</p>

<p>“…Sorry. I’ll split my chopsticks properly.”<br>That’s what I’d say in my head.<br>“It’s not me, sir.” (laughs)</p>

<p>But now I realize—<br>we were probably saved<br>by that awkward honesty all along.</p>

<p>That day, the owner looked tired.<br>Still, he stood in the kitchen,<br>making each bowl carefully.</p>

<p>Scary, but never sloppy.<br>Clumsy, but sincere.</p>

<p>The words that came to mind were simple:<br>“He ran the whole race.”</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">A Bowl of Ramen That Walked Alongside My Life</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_iipepxiipepxiipe.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2932"/></figure>

<p>I’ve been eating that ramen since I was 30.</p>

<p>Through job changes.<br>Through saying goodbye to my parents.<br>Through watching my children grow into adults.</p>

<p>In happy times and painful ones,<br>that yellow sign was always there.</p>

<p>Eat quietly.<br>Walk out thinking,<br>“Alright… I’ll do my best again tomorrow.”</p>

<p>That place gave me that feeling.</p>

<p>Not being able to eat there anymore—<br>honestly, it hurts.</p>

<p>I won’t see that slightly scary old man again either.</p>

<p>I wish there had been a successor…<br>I really do.</p>

<p>But some things can’t be helped.<br>Shops have lifespans.<br>People do too.</p>

<p>“Cherish what you have while it’s here.”<br>…I say that like I’m being wise,<br>but the truth is simple.</p>

<p>I just want to slurp those noodles one more time.</p>

<p>Not very graceful, is it? (laughs)</p>

<p>Hachioji Taishoken.<br>Thank you for 23 years.</p>

<p>If we ever meet again somewhere,<br>this time I’ll be the one to say,<br>“Thank you.”</p>

<p>Your ramen added just the right oil<br>to the worn-out engine of my heart,<br>again and again.</p>

<p>Thanks to that,<br>I think I can keep moving forward—somehow.</p>

<p>…So,<br>what should I eat tomorrow?</p>

<p>See you again.</p>
