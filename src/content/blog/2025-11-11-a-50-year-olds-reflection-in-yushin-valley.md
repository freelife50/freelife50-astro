<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🏞️ Conclusion: Yushin Valley in November is Perfect for Friends!</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2730,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251108_020918309.MP_-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2730"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To start with the bottom line —<br><strong>Yushin Valley in November is one of the best hikes to enjoy with friends.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Though a 20km round trip sounds tough, it’s actually a gentle forest road — more of a <em>long stroll</em> than a mountain climb.<br>Along the way, you’ll find the <strong>mystical turquoise water known as “Yushin Blue.”</strong><strong><br></strong> It’s so beautiful that you’ll stop in your tracks.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And after the hike? A warm meal and cold drinks at the lodge.<br>That combination refreshes both body and soul — truly, <strong>an “adult field trip.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The <em>Gensou (Kurokura) Forest Road</em> often closes due to falling rocks or landslides, <strong>including for pedestrians.</strong><strong><br></strong> Always check the latest info from <strong>Kanagawa Prefecture or the Yamakita Tourism Office</strong> before departure.<br>Closures can happen suddenly!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.yamakita.net/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">山北町観光協会（公式サイト） / Yamakita Town Tourism Association (Official Site)</a></p>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🧢 A 53-Year-Old and His Annual Autumn Hike</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2720,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251108_004538941.MP_-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2720"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I’m 53.<br>Every autumn, I go hiking with my old friends from our <strong>“Hikers Club.”</strong><strong><br></strong> This year, on November 8–9, 2025, we chose Yushin Valley.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Our club started as a PTA “Dads’ Group” when my eldest son was in high school.<br>Now, more than ten years later, it’s still going strong.<br>Some of the guys are serious climbers, but I’m definitely on the <strong>“easy hiking with good food”</strong> side.<br>Honestly, my favorite part is <strong>the beer and dinner afterward.</strong><strong><br></strong> (We always end up sleeping past our train stop on the way home 🤣)</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🥂 This Year Felt Different</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before the trip, one of our members had passed away.<br>We raised a glass in silence before starting the hike.<br>That moment hit hard — <strong>life is unpredictable.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s why, as we walked, I thought,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“Just being able to walk together like this — what a gift.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Step by step, I felt gratitude deeper than usual.</p>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🚶‍♂️ Yushin Valley Hike — Quick Facts</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Start/End:</strong> Tanzawa Lake Lodge (Free parking near Kurokura Parking Lot)<br><strong>Distance:</strong> 20 km round trip<br><strong>Time Required:</strong> Around 7 hours<br><strong>Difficulty:</strong> More of a <em>long forest walk</em> than a climb<br><strong>Parking:</strong> Free (arrive early on weekends)<br>If full, check nearby paid lots around Tanzawa Lake.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Toilets:</strong> Available at Kurokura Parking Lot.<br>The one near the former Yushin Lodge is often closed — plan ahead.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Most of the route follows a quiet road through forests and tunnels, ending at the abandoned <strong>Yushin Lodge ruins</strong>.<br>As expected for a “hidden valley,” there’s <strong>no phone signal</strong> — but that’s exactly the charm.<br>A complete escape from modern noise.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>💙 “Yushin Blue” — Beauty Beyond Words</strong></h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":2722,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251108_011600537-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2722"/></figure>

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":2721,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/PXL_20251108_011533092.MP_-769x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2721"/></figure>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The water of Gensou River shifts from <strong>emerald green to turquoise blue</strong> as it reflects sunlight.<br>Photos can’t capture it.<br>The stones at the riverbed shimmer through the crystal water, changing color with the light.<br>It’s truly <strong>a natural work of art.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🌞 Best Conditions to See Yushin Blue</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Time:</strong> Around noon (11 a.m. – 1 p.m.)<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Season:</strong> Mid to late November (autumn foliage)<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Weather:</strong> Clear skies and no wind. Avoid after rain — the water clouds easily.<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We got lucky — perfect weather, vivid leaves, and glowing blue water.<br>Everyone went silent, just <strong>standing there in awe.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🔬 Why Is It So Blue?</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even science hasn’t fully explained it. Theories include:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Micro-minerals (like magnesium) scattering light<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>The <strong>Tyndall effect</strong>, where red wavelengths are absorbed and blue remains<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>White rock beneath the water enhancing reflection<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In short, it’s a <strong>chemical miracle of nature</strong> — something no human could replicate.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>🎒 What to Bring</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Essentials</strong><strong><br></strong> ・Headlamp (tunnels are pitch dark)<br>・1.5L+ of water and snacks<br>・Trekking shoes<br>・Bear bell or spray<br>・Warm layers</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Nice to Have</strong><strong><br></strong> ・Trekking poles<br>・Rainwear<br>・Portable battery<br>・Trash bag</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>⚠️ <strong>Tips &amp; Warnings</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Tunnels are long — carry backup lights.<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Watch for falling rocks near tunnel entrances.<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>No signal — plan to finish before 4 p.m.<br></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Avoid right after rain — <strong>you’ll miss the blue magic.</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
