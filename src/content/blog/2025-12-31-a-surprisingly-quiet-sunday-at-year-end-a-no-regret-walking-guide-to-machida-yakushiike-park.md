<h2 id="when-i-went-amp-how-crowded-it-felt" class="wp-block-heading">When I Went &amp; How Crowded It Felt</h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1818.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2974"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1800.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2960"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>I visited on <strong>Sunday, December 28</strong>.<br>Honestly, I expected crowds—it was year-end, after all.</p>

<p>But my impression was simple:</p>

<p><strong>“It’s… surprisingly quiet.”</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>No bumping into people</li>

<li>Benches were available</li>

<li>Plenty of breathing room</li>
</ul>

<p>Why so empty?</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Many people had already left town for the holidays</li>

<li>Others were busy with New Year preparations</li>

<li>This park feels more like a <em>local</em> park than a tourist hotspot</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Lesson learned:</strong><br><strong>“A year-end Sunday doesn’t automatically mean crowds.”</strong></p>

<h2 id="parking-get-this-wrong-and-youll-regret-" class="wp-block-heading"> Parking: Get This Wrong and You’ll Regret It</h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1809.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2961"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1805.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2959"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>Yakushiike Park has multiple parking areas, and <strong>choosing the wrong one means unnecessary walking uphill.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Which Parking Lot to Use</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Pond, Taikobashi Bridge, traditional houses</strong><br>→ <strong>North Parking Lot</strong></li>

<li><strong>Cafes, lawns, farm shop, newer facilities</strong><br>→ <strong>West Garden Parking (Welcome Gate)</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>The West Garden area is modern and stylish.<br>As a middle-aged guy who secretly loves new things… <strong>yeah, I really wanted to park there.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">My Mistake (Learn from This)</h3>

<p>The West Garden entrance is <strong>easy to miss</strong>.</p>

<p>I followed my car navigation, drove past it, passed the squirrel park,<br>and before I knew it—<strong>I was already at the North Parking Lot.</strong></p>

<p>I almost gave up and thought,<br>“Eh, whatever… this is fine.”</p>

<p>But nope. I made a stubborn U-turn. 😅</p>

<p><strong>Important tip:</strong><br><strong>Don’t rely only on your navigation system. Watch the road signs carefully.</strong><br>U-turn spots are limited around here.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">North Parking Lot Cautions</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>No right turn entry from the Machida Station side</li>

<li>Easy to overshoot if following navigation blindly</li>

<li>Some areas make U-turns difficult</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Parking Fee</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>First hour free</strong> (at the time of writing)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Perfect for short walks or scouting visits.</strong></p>

<h2 id="footwear-amp-elevation-more-tiring-than-" class="wp-block-heading">Footwear &amp; Elevation: More Tiring Than It Looks</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1798.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2962"/></figure>

<p>On the map, the park looks compact.<br>In reality, there’s a <strong>noticeable height difference</strong>.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>West Garden:</strong> higher ground</li>

<li><strong>Yakushiike pond area:</strong> lower ground</li>
</ul>

<p>Going down is easy.<br>Coming back up? <strong>You’ll feel it in your legs.</strong></p>

<p>(I did. Let’s keep that between us.)</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Slopes vs. Stairs</h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Slopes avoid steps</li>

<li>But they add <strong>about 300 meters</strong></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>If you’re with a stroller or have knee concerns, plan ahead.</strong></p>

<p>Overall impression:<br><strong>The park is thoughtfully designed with accessibility in mind.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Wooden Boardwalk Warning</h3>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1812.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2963"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1814.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2964"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>The boardwalk near the iris fields looks great, but:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Gaps between boards</li>

<li>Slippery after rain</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Avoid slippery shoes.</strong><br>(And dogs may get <em>very</em> excited—ours certainly did.)</p>

<h2 id="yakushi-hall-event-just-the-facts" class="wp-block-heading">Yakushi Hall Event (Just the Facts)</h2>

<div class="wp-block-columns"><div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1834.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2975"/></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column"><figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/IMG_1836.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2976"/></figure>
</div>
</div>

<p>Every year on <strong>January 12 around 11 a.m.</strong>,<br>the <strong>First Yakushi Goma Fire Ritual</strong> is held.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Write wishes on wooden prayer sticks</li>

<li>Burned in sacred fire</li>

<li>Blessings from Yakushi Nyorai</li>
</ul>

<p>Note:<br><strong>It’s January 12—not the 8th, as many people assume.</strong></p>

<p>I didn’t see it this time, but honestly…<br><strong>I kind of want to go next year.</strong><br>I’m that kind of guy.</p>

<p>(Event times may change—check in advance.)</p>
