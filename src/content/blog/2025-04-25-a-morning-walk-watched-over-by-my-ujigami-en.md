<p>🕒 About 2 minutes to read.</p>

<p>On my way to work one morning, I took a quiet detour through the fresh morning air and found myself stopping by Muratomi Shrine — my local <em>Ujigami</em> shrine in Sagamihara.</p>

<p>Like many people, I usually only visit shrines during New Year's. But on this particular morning, something drew me in — and what I found was a small but meaningful reset for my mind.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2>Muratomi Shrine — Simple, Yet Dignified</h2>

<p>Muratomi Shrine isn't extravagant, but it carries a calm, dignified atmosphere that quietly straightens your posture. For over 350 years since the Edo period, this Ujigami has been watching over the local community.</p>

<p>The torii gate in the morning light seemed to gently say, <em>"Do your best today."</em></p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/PXL_20250421_214449373-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-169"/></figure>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2>Take a Moment to Greet Your Ujigami</h2>

<p>Many people only visit shrines for New Year's (Hatsumode). But stopping by on an ordinary day can unexpectedly lighten your heart.</p>

<p>Ujigami — the local guardian deity — is there not just for special occasions, but also to receive daily gratitude and simple greetings. Even without a specific wish, just saying <em>"Thank you for watching over me"</em> as you clap your hands can bring a surprising sense of peace and clarity.</p>

<p>I hope you'll try it too — even once.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/PXL_20250421_214547835.NIGHT_-1-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-179"/></figure>

<h2>The Legend of the Great Pine and the White Snake</h2>

<p>Long ago, a white snake was said to live in a giant pine tree within Muratomi Shrine. In Japanese culture, white snakes are sacred messengers of the gods, and the villagers respected and cherished its presence for generations.</p>

<p>Today, we often forget to appreciate nature and the unseen forces around us. But touching stories like this reminds us that we are always being watched over — and protected.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/PXL_20250421_214527787.NIGHT_-scaled.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-172"/></figure>

<h2>Morning Walks × Mental Wellness = Visiting Ujigami</h2>

<p>I started walking for my physical health. But by visiting my local shrine along the way, I realized I was also nurturing my mental well-being.</p>

<p>In our busy daily lives, taking even a few minutes to step into a shrine can bring a sense of calm and clarity. It will surely ease your daily worries and refresh your spirit.</p>

<h2>In Conclusion</h2>

<p>Places like Muratomi Shrine — simple yet dignified — are not tourist attractions, but they quietly support our daily lives. If this article reminds you of a nearby shrine, why not stop by and greet your Ujigami?</p>

<p>You may find peace and a fresh perspective waiting for you. 😊</p>
