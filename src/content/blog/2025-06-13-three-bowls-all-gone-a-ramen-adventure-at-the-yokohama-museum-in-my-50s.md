<p>🍜 <strong>Intro</strong><br>In my last blog post, I wrote that I <em>might go someday</em> to the Yokohama Ramen Museum.<br>Well… that “someday” finally arrived.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I’m in my 50s. My blood pressure isn’t what it used to be. And let’s be honest—my stomach’s capacity isn’t either.<br>Still, standing in front of the ticket machine, something in me whispered,<br><strong>“Why not go for three?”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This is the story of how that quiet little whisper turned into me… slurping down three bowls. Yes, soup and all.</p>

<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>In my last post, I said I wanted to visit the Ramen Museum someday.<br>Well, yesterday—I finally did it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I went with the plan to try <em>mini-sized</em> bowls. You know, just a little taste of each.<br>Health in mind, blood pressure concerns, middle-aged wisdom… I swore I’d leave the soup behind.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But nah.<br>The moment I took my first sip, it slipped out of my mouth—<br><strong>“Holy crap… this is amazing.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It wasn’t just good. It was <em>brain-zapping</em>.<br>The heat of the broth shot up from my tongue to the top of my head, short-circuiting every logical thought.<br>Rich, balanced, full of umami. Too complete to resist.<br>I thought, “Mini-sized? This is a full-course punch in the face!”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By the end of that first bowl, I already knew:<br><strong>“Yeah… I’m finishing all of these.”</strong><br>That voice inside wasn’t reason—it was instinct.<br>“C’mon, you're in your 50s. You’ve got blood pressure and triglycerides to worry about.”<br>Yeah, that voice got steamrolled by three bowls of temptation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In the end, I drank every last drop.<br>I could see the clean bottom of the bowls—like trophies.<br>Sure, I felt a little gross afterward… but not a hint of regret.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Because those soups weren’t meant to be left behind.<br>They were magic. Liquid spells.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟥 <strong>Chapter 2: First Bowl — Not What I Expected, But Dang It Was Good.</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column {"width":"33.33%"} -->
<div class="wp-block-column" style="flex-basis:33.33%"><!-- wp:image {"id":1705,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>My first bowl? "Hakata Bunpuku."<br>Honestly, I didn’t research it much.<br>I figured, “It says miso… maybe some retro street-stall vibes?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But when it came out—totally different from what I imagined.<br>Sure, it looked like miso ramen… but the moment I sipped the broth, my brain went,<br><strong>“Wait… this ain’t just miso.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yes, the miso was there—but deeper, layered with something rich and soulful.<br>Like miso × tonkotsu × dashi in a triple backflip combo.<br>I don’t know the structure. All I know is, my spoon didn’t stop.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The noodles were a surprise too—thin and thick ones mixed together.<br>It was like ramen roulette: “Oh! Thin. Whoa, thick!”<br>That texture play actually made me grin while eating.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The toppings—char siu, wood ear mushrooms—weren’t trying to steal the show.<br>They were solid sidekicks.<br>Because the real star? The soup. A full-blown lead role.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>And then came the crime.</strong><br>I ended up slurping down the whole soup.<br>Not just mine… my wife’s too.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>She gave me <em>that</em> look.<br>You know—the one where it’s part disbelief, part “medical professional in silent judgment.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Like she was drafting a clinical note in her head:<br>“Patient: Male, 50s. Repeated incident of soup overconsumption. Possible chronic condition.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But I couldn’t help it.<br>Blood pressure, cholesterol—those words evaporated.<br>This miso ramen?<br>Had a power way beyond miso.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>From the first spoonful, it had me off-balance.<br>And from there, my pace was wrecked. Gloriously so.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>The second bowl? “Rishiri Ramen Miraku,” straight from Rishiri Island, Hokkaido.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even though it was a mini-sized bowl, the <strong>aroma was massive</strong>.<br>The moment it landed on the table, steam rose up and hit my nose like a tidal wave.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“Whoa... this kombu is on a rampage.”</strong><br>Instantly, my brain switched to “Japanese cuisine mode.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The first sip? Game over.<br>It hit hard but stayed smooth—<br>like being gently punched in the face by kindness.<br>A kind of <strong>umami slap</strong> I didn’t see coming.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Then it happened—<br>In my mind, a steaming bowl of white rice appeared.<br>My brain screamed,<br><strong>“Pour this broth over rice. Now.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A mental reel of kombu-chazuke played out, complete with CG steam.<br>I almost ordered rice.<br>But no—I had a three-bowl mission.<br>Rice would’ve knocked me out early.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So I resisted.<br>Which made every following sip feel even better.<br>It was like the broth <em>knew</em> I was holding back and decided to reward me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Is kombu umami some kind of emotional blackmail?”<br>Each sip gave me more flavor, more depth, more everything.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I think the noodles were curly… maybe?<br>Honestly, the broth was such a show-stealer, I forgot the noodles.<br>All I remember is my brain staging an elaborate fantasy:<br>Gently scooping rice into the bowl… letting it soak… and taking a bite with a spoon.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I didn’t actually do it.<br>But in my mind?<br><strong>It tasted perfect.</strong><br>That was my forbidden finale: a brain-powered kombu chazuke.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Bowl number three — the finisher — came from <strong>Hakata Issou</strong>.<br>By this time, my internal sodium meter was deep in the red.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A quiet voice inside me whispered,<br><strong>“Maybe… it’s time to stop?”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But then another voice—louder, reckless—yelled,<br><strong>“No way. If you skip Issou now, you’ll regret it forever.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before I knew it, my finger had already pressed the ticket machine.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And the one I chose?<br>Of course, it was the richest-looking one on the menu.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When it arrived, my nose got punched—lovingly—by that <strong>classic tonkotsu funk</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“Ugh… that smell… but it’s kinda amazing…!!”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>My brain? Totally glitched.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The foamy surface of the broth looked like a cappuccino.<br>One sip, and the rich liquid clung to my tongue.<br>It slithered down my throat, slow and silky.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“Whoa… this is addictive…”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I muttered without meaning to.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By now, I’d already slurped up two full bowls of broth.<br>My stomach was sending out SOS signals.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But my brain?<br>It had already been hacked.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Tonkotsu funk had reprogrammed my neurons:<br><strong>“Porky smell = happiness.”</strong><br><strong>“High blood pressure? Don’t care!”</strong><br><strong>“Too strong? That’s the point!”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I was completely under ramen’s spell.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And yes, I drank the broth.<br>All of it. Again.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As I stared at the bubbly surface, I actually chuckled.<br><strong>“Yup. I got destroyed.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But deep down, I thought:<br><strong>“Stinky is tasty.”</strong><br>It’s a weird concept. But somehow, totally true.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s the magic of Hakata tonkotsu.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🟪 <strong>Final Thoughts: The Irresistible Spell of Ramen</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At first, I had a plan:<br><strong>“Let’s take it easy. Mini bowls only. Leave the broth.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I was thinking about my blood pressure, my health—<br>I really <em>thought</em> I’d stay in control.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But one sip was all it took.<br>My logic vanished, like steam from the bowl.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Ramen isn’t just about flavor.<br>It’s the aroma, the color, the texture of the noodles, the vibe of the place—<br><strong>It’s an all-senses experience. A storm.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And I didn’t stand a chance.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I ended up drinking every drop from all three bowls,<br>fighting off the urge to order rice,<br>yet still watching the bottom of each bowl like it was a holy grail.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Honestly? I was grateful.<br>It was <em>that</em> good.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yeah, I felt a bit off afterward.<br>But no regrets.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Because those broths weren’t meant to be left behind.</p>
