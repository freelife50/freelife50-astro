<h2 id="i-was-satisfied-but-something-started-to" class="wp-block-heading">I Was Satisfied. But Something Started to Bug Me.</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_4ssq6m4ssq6m4ssq.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2915"/></figure>

<p>To be honest, I had no major complaints about my Pixel 7a.<br>It was fast, smooth, and worked perfectly with Google services.<br>For the first time, I thought,<br>“So this is what a truly cost-effective smartphone feels like.”</p>

<p>…And yet.</p>

<p>Every time I used Suica.<br>Every time I scanned my My Number card.<br>A small thought kept lingering in my head:</p>

<p>“Wouldn’t an iPhone feel… more reassuring?”</p>

<p>Let me be clear—<br>Pixel handles Suica and ID just fine.<br>This wasn’t about performance.</p>

<p>But the way Face ID unlocks instantly,<br>and authentication flows so smoothly afterward—<br>somehow gave me a sense of security.<br>It just <em>felt</em> good.</p>

<p>Yes, it’s completely a matter of preference. (Laughs)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<p>”<strong>Why It Took So Long to Decide</strong>”</p>

<p>That said, I couldn’t just buy one immediately.</p>

<p>In your 50s, “What if I regret it?” comes before “Let’s do it.”<br>That youthful “Eh, whatever!” courage doesn’t show up so easily anymore.</p>

<p>For me, smartphones are replaced every two years,<br>with resale value always in mind.<br>My Pixel was only about a year and a half old.</p>

<p>Wait until next May?<br>Or jump now?</p>

<p>After a lot of overthinking, I arrived at one simple conclusion:</p>

<p>“If I’m going to keep worrying, I might as well try it.”</p>

<p>So I chose the <strong>iPhone 17 (standard model, released September 2025)</strong>.</p>

<p>Why not the Pro?</p>

<p>The camera is good enough as is,<br>and more importantly—lighter is better.<br>My wrists and shoulders in their 50s don’t lie. (Laughs)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="the-biggest-fear-data-transfer" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Biggest Fear: Data Transfer</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_xp7gr3xp7gr3xp7g.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2916"/></figure>

<p>Honestly, I had resisted iPhones for years.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>The ecosystem lock-in<br></li>

<li>Less customization<br></li>

<li>Feeling like a hostage (okay, maybe exaggerating a bit)<br></li>
</ul>

<p>But my biggest fear was data transfer.</p>

<p>Especially LINE.</p>

<p>“What if all my chat history disappears?”</p>

<p>That part scared me the most.</p>

<p>In reality?<br>Using Apple’s transfer app was almost disappointingly easy.</p>

<p>I carefully backed up LINE,<br>held my breath while proceeding—<br>and everything transferred successfully.</p>

<p>…Probably. (Laughs)</p>

<p>Did I verify every single detail?<br>Not really.</p>

<p>At some point, you just say,<br>“Well, it’ll be fine,”<br>and move on.</p>

<p>And honestly, I thought:<br>“So <em>this</em> is what I was so worried about?”</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="a-long-and-winding-smartphone-journey" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>A Long and Winding Smartphone Journey</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_3jnjlh3jnjlh3jnj.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2917"/></figure>

<p>Looking back, my smartphone history is… messy.</p>

<p>First, a cheap Chinese phone under $100.<br>I don’t even remember the name—<br>or rather, I couldn’t read it.</p>

<p>The manual’s Japanese was questionable,<br>battery life was terrible, performance unstable.<br>I assumed, “This is just how smartphones are.”</p>

<p>Then came <strong>arrows</strong>.<br>Very user-friendly.<br>Too friendly.<br>It felt like a kind but overbearing supervisor.</p>

<p>Next was <strong>AQUOS</strong>.<br>Amazing battery life—<br>I thought I’d found the answer.</p>

<p>But somehow, it didn’t click.</p>

<p>Back to cheap phones.<br>“Low price is justice,” I told myself.<br>Still unhappy.</p>

<p>Back to AQUOS again.<br>Then finally… <strong>Pixel 7a</strong>.</p>

<p>For the first time, I thought:<br>“This is good.”</p>

<p>Yes—way too much wandering. (Laughs)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Turning Point: Buying a Mac</strong></h3>

<p>During all that wandering, I started blogging<br>and bought a Mac (M2) for writing.</p>

<p>Looking back,<br>that decision quietly changed everything.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="mac--pixel-vs-mac--iphone" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Mac × Pixel vs Mac × iPhone</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_p3dyuop3dyuop3dy.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2918"/></figure>

<p><strong>Living Apart vs Living Together</strong></p>

<p>You only really understand this once you try it.</p>

<p><strong>Mac × Pixel</strong> is like<br>“Great relationship, but living separately.”</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Transfer photos via Google Photos<br></li>

<li>Upload, wait, download<br></li>

<li>Copy &amp; paste doesn’t flow naturally between devices<br></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Mac × iPhone</strong> feels like<br>“Already living together.”</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>AirDrop transfers photos and videos instantly<br></li>

<li>No quality loss<br></li>

<li>Clipboard sharing<br></li>

<li>Notes, passwords, Safari—everything syncs seamlessly<br></li>
</ul>

<p>The first time I used AirDrop, I honestly thought:<br>“Wait… what just happened? Is this magic?”</p>

<p>And it’s free.</p>

<p>That’s when it clicked:</p>

<p>“Okay… this really <em>is</em> a different world.”</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="selling-the-pixel-a-trust-building-middl" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Selling the Pixel (A Trust-Building Middle-Aged Man)</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_elyejcelyejcelye.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2919"/></figure>

<p>The Pixel 7a?<br>This is the best time to sell it.</p>

<p>Next year, it’ll be three years old.<br>Value only goes down from here.</p>

<p>So, off to Mercari it goes.</p>

<p>Carefully written profile—no robotic tone.<br>Profile icon: my Shiba Inu, Momiji.</p>

<p>Yes,<br>I’m a middle-aged man desperately building trust. (Laughs)</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 id="final-thoughts" class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Final Thoughts</strong></h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Gemini_Generated_Image_xei2zixei2zixei2.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2920"/></figure>

<p>I still don’t know which choice is objectively “correct.”</p>

<p>Pure practicality?<br>Pixel might suit me better.</p>

<p>But in my 50s, I’ve realized something:</p>

<p>Less regret comes from <em>trying</em><em><br></em> than from endlessly wondering “what if.”</p>

<p>Hesitating isn’t bad.<br>But hesitating forever gets tiring—<br>and honestly, time isn’t infinite anymore.</p>

<p>If you’re struggling with a smartphone choice right now,<br>you don’t have to force a decision.</p>

<p>Try both.<br>Or step back for a while.</p>

<p>Find the tool that fits <em>your</em> pace.</p>

<p>That’s probably enough.</p>
