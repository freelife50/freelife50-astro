<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Before You Go (Access Info)</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3314,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3024-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3314"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If you’re planning to visit,<br>let me share the important details first.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Harukimichi and Shiawase Road run along the Kari River,<br>with 171 “Harumeki Sakura” trees lined on both sides.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>During March 20–22, 2026,<br>there are food trucks, small stalls, and night illuminations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You can sit on the riverbank and enjoy the view.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One important thing:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The free parking lot is at <em>Tsujishita Ground</em>.<br>Parking on the street or at nearby shops is strictly prohibited.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There’s also a loop bus from Daiyuzan Station<br>running between 10:00 and 16:00.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And me?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Well… I didn’t know about the free parking<br>and ended up paying for parking.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yeah… that hurt a little. (laughs)</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Walking Along the Riverbank</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column {"width":"66.66%"} -->
<div class="wp-block-column" style="flex-basis:66.66%"><!-- wp:image {"id":3309,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3006-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3309"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column {"width":"33.33%"} -->
<div class="wp-block-column" style="flex-basis:33.33%"><!-- wp:image {"id":3310,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3004-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3310"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The moment I saw the festival banners,<br>I could feel the atmosphere change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Pink and white flags swaying in the wind,<br>people walking underneath them.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Then I reached the riverbank—<br>rows of cherry trees stretching far into the distance.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These are called <em>Harumeki Sakura</em>,<br>a variety developed in Minamiashigara.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And they have a unique feature:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>They smell sweet.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Unlike Somei Yoshino,<br>which barely has any scent,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>these blossoms gently carry a fragrance.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As I walked, I caught it in the air.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“Ah… so this is what cherry blossoms smell like.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At over 50 years old,<br>it was my first time realizing that.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Meaning Behind the Name</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3311,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3010-768x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3311"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>While walking, I noticed a wooden sign:<br>“Harukimichi.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It turns out the name comes from Haruki Ei,<br>the founder of Fujifilm and an honorary citizen of the city.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>He lived to 101 years old,<br>and to honor him, 101 cherry trees were planted.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>101 years, 101 trees.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When I learned that,<br>it somehow touched me.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Knowing the story<br>makes the same scenery feel different.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Shrine by the River</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3312,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3014-766x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3312"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":3313,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/IMG_3013-768x1024.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3313"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Near the cherry trees,<br>there was a small shrine dedicated to a water deity.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>According to the sign,<br>the Kari River used to flood frequently.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Homes and fields were often damaged,<br>so people built embankments and prayed for safety.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even today, those traditions continue.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When you visit as a tourist,<br>it’s easy to walk past places like this.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But when you stop and read,<br>you start to see how the land has lived over time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Standing by the river, surrounded by blossoms,<br>I found myself thinking about that.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
