<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>This article explores the rising popularity of <em>BeReal</em>, a no-filter social media app embraced by Gen Z.<br>From the perspective of someone in their 50s who grew up without the internet or social media,<br>we reflect on why younger generations are choosing to share their unfiltered selves—and what that says about our digital age.</p>

<p>By examining the shift from “looking good online” to “being real together,”<br>we ask: What is social media really for?<br>In a time when many feel exhausted from chasing likes and approval,<br>perhaps there's something for older generations to learn from this return to authenticity.</p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Lately, I’ve been hearing about an app called <em>BeReal</em> that’s gaining popularity among Gen Z.<br>They say it’s a “no-filter” social media platform—and honestly, as someone in my 50s, it feels a bit puzzling.</p>

<p>Back when I was young, we didn’t even have the internet, let alone social media.<br>Now, the online world revolves around likes, filters, and perfectly curated posts.<br>So what does it mean to “not try to look good” or to “just be yourself” online?</p>

<p>In this article, I take a closer look at BeReal and Gen Z’s shift toward authenticity.<br>At the same time, I reflect on the emotional fatigue that many people experience on social media today.</p>

<p>What was social media supposed to be in the first place?<br>That’s the question I’d like to revisit—from the perspective of someone who’s seen the world both before and after it all began.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2>“Looking Good” vs. Being Real</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_v7ws0dv7ws0dv7ws.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1626"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>In the world of social media, it’s almost normal to “look better than you are.”<br>We add filters, post only the good parts, and chase likes without really knowing why.</p>

<p>But sometimes, we stop and wonder:<br>“Am I doing this for me—or for others to see?”</p>

<p>Back in my younger days, there was no internet.<br>So we didn’t grow up measuring ourselves by how many people liked our photos.<br>Now, we live in a world where your self-worth feels tied to your online image.</p>

<p>For people in my generation, it's easy to think,<br>“Getting lots of likes = doing well in life.”<br>But is that really true? Or have we just gotten used to chasing approval?</p>

<p>Gen Z seems to be saying,<br>“Let’s stop pretending. Just be real.”</p>

<p>BeReal reflects that mindset: no filters, no staging—just you, in the moment.</p>
</blockquote>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2>Why Gen Z Chooses “Unfiltered” Connections</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_5na2lp5na2lp5na2.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1627"/></figure>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>When I was young, taking a photo meant using film—and we didn’t share every little thing with others.<br>There were no likes, no filters, no need to “look good.”<br>Just being there, in the moment, was enough.</p>

<p>But now, social media feels like a competition.<br>Post the best shot.<br>Get more likes.<br>Look amazing—whether you feel that way or not.</p>

<p>Maybe that’s why some Gen Z folks are saying,<br>“I’m tired of this. I just want to be me.”</p>

<p>BeReal seems built for that.<br>It doesn’t care about being popular or perfect:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>You get only 2 minutes to post</li>

<li>No filters or editing allowed</li>

<li>No public follower counts</li>

<li>Just a small group of friends</li>
</ul>

<p>It’s not about proving anything.<br>It’s about saying,<br>“Hey, I’m here. I exist. That’s enough.”</p>

<p>And maybe that’s what a lot of us really need.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2>Why “Being Real” Matters Now</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_1u8u5h1u8u5h1u8u.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1628"/></figure>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>— Gen Z’s Honesty and Our Confusion</p>

<p>🔸 <strong>Wasn’t SNS supposed to be fun?</strong><br>Social media used to feel like a fun place—<br>a quick message to a friend,<br>a silly video to laugh at,<br>a way to stay connected.</p>

<p>But somehow, it turned into something else.<br>Now it’s all about likes, filters, followers.<br>We open an app, and instead of smiling,<br>we sigh.</p>

<p>🔸 <strong>The Comparison Trap</strong><br>It’s easy to fall into:<br>"Why is everyone’s life so shiny?"<br>"Why don't I get as many likes?"<br>"Am I... boring?"</p>

<p>We start to compare.<br>And then we start to shrink.</p>

<p>Maybe Gen Z saw this happening.<br>They watched older generations chasing approval,<br>and thought,<br>“Is this really what we want?”</p>

<p>🔸 <strong>Back to Simple, Honest Moments</strong><br>No filters.<br>No showing off.<br>Just small, real moments shared with people who get you.</p>

<p>BeReal is part of that shift—<br>a quiet protest against the pressure to perform.</p>

<p>It says:<br>"You don’t have to be amazing all the time.<br>You just have to be <em>you</em>."</p>
</blockquote>

<h2>I’m in My 50s—So Honestly, I Don’t Really Get It. But…</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_2bsbz52bsbz52bsb.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1629"/></figure>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>When I first heard about BeReal, I thought,<br>“Wait… What? You just post whatever you’re doing <em>right now</em>?”<br>Like—when the notification hits, you take a picture, no matter what you look like?</p>

<p>Waking up? Eating lunch?<br>Still in pajamas?<br>Seriously?</p>

<p>Back in my day, we didn’t even have the internet.<br>Taking a photo was something you did at weddings or vacations,<br>not just on a regular Tuesday while walking the dog.</p>

<p>📸 <strong>Cameras Make Me Stiffen Up</strong><br>Even now, when someone points a camera at me,<br>I straighten my back,<br>check the background,<br>force a smile.<br>I’m still stuck in the habit of “looking good.”</p>

<p>🌿 <strong>But Their Way of Connecting Feels… Kind of Nice</strong><br>This “BeReal” thing—it’s different.<br>There’s no pressure. No image crafting.</p>

<p>Just, “Here I am, right now.”</p>

<p>And honestly?<br>That’s kind of beautiful.</p>

<p>It’s not about perfect lighting or cool locations.<br>It’s about showing up, even when you’re tired or messy.</p>

<p>Maybe we, the 50-something crowd,<br>don’t <em>fully</em> understand it yet—<br>but we can feel something true in it.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2>So, What Is “Real,” Anyway?</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_fnz191fnz191fnz1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1630"/></figure>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Gemini_Generated_Image_2tmwxt2tmwxt2tmw.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1631"/></figure>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Through this look at BeReal and the mindset of Gen Z,<br>I’ve realized something:<br>Maybe it’s not about fully understanding it—<br>it’s about listening.</p>

<p>We’ve been trying so hard to look good online,<br>chasing likes, crafting perfect images,<br>that somewhere along the way,<br>we started losing sight of <em>ourselves</em>.</p>

<p>But maybe being “real” is simpler than we think.</p>

<p>It’s not about proving anything.<br>It’s not about comparison.<br>It’s just about saying,<br><strong>“I’m here today. This is me.”</strong></p>

<p>Even in our 50s,<br>we can let go of the pressure, the pride, the filters.<br>Maybe we could try connecting with others<br>just as we are.</p>

<p>In this age of digital noise,<br>what Gen Z is showing us might be a quiet kind of wisdom:</p>

<p>✨ <em>Being honest is enough.</em><br>✨ <em>You don’t need to shine to be seen.</em><br>✨ <em>You’re already real, just as you are.</em></p>

<p>And honestly? That’s kind of a relief.</p>
</blockquote>
