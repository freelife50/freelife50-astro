<li>You feel a bit uneasy about joining or trying something alone</li>

<li>You want to feel your heart moved by new connections again</li>

<p><strong>Prologue: A Middle-Aged Man in Another World</strong></p>



<p>A man in his 50s goes to a festival.<a href="https://libefes.com/">（https//libefes.com/</a>）<br>…And no, it’s not some deafening, youth-packed music fest.<br>It felt more like I’d been summoned into another world—an <em>isekai</em>—for middle-aged men.</p>



<p>This was the “RibeCity” Fest, a community I’ve been part of for a while.<br>Held at Makuhari Messe, the theme was all about <em>money</em>:<br>household budgeting, insurance reviews, investing, side hustles, real estate… you name it.<br>A buffet of financial knowledge packed into one event.</p>



<p>It was a three-day, two-night affair.<br>Accommodation? Nope. I went with the unthinkable choice—<strong>sleeping in my car</strong>.<br>At this age, car camping is brutal. My back… oh, my poor back… screaming in protest.</p>



<p>I hesitated for a long time before deciding.<br>“Me? A middle-aged guy at a festival? Kinda embarrassing…”<br>But with some encouragement from our community leader (“It won’t kill you—probably”),<br>I took the plunge.<br>And so, with equal parts embarrassment and anticipation, I set off for Makuhari Messe.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Day_1_Intimidated_by_the_Crowd_Rediscovering_Humanity_as_a_Volunteer"></span>Day 1: Intimidated by the Crowd &amp; Rediscovering Humanity as a Volunteer<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img decoding="async" width="769" height="1024" src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2055" srcset="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 769w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 225w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 768w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 1153w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 1538w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 376w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_030921300.jpg 1922w" sizes="(max-width: 769px) 100vw, 769px" /></figure>



<p>The first thing I saw at the venue entrance? A massive line.<br>I froze.<br>No, seriously—I <em>froze</em>.<br>For a moment I thought, “Maybe I should just go home…”<br>But the people in line looked so alive, so energized, that their enthusiasm started to rub off on me.</p>



<p>My main mission that day was a three-hour volunteer gig: <strong>venue patrol</strong>.<br>At first, I was so nervous that my eyes stayed glued to the ceiling and floor.<br>But the people I met were incredibly warm and welcoming.</p>



<p>Some had quit their jobs to live freely, others were crafting lives with no strings attached…<br>I realized, <em>I’m still clinging to my company</em>, and felt a pang of frustration—<br>but also a huge burst of inspiration.</p>



<p>While patrolling, I ran into the community leader himself.<br>“The real deal! There he is!”<br>That alone made my ticket worth it.</p>



<p>I even managed a commemorative photo with my fellow volunteers (faces hidden, of course)<br>and scored a T-shirt and plushie.<br>Didn’t explore much beyond the business card booth that day,<br>but volunteering was the real treasure.</p>



<p>Oh, and I finally got to try the legendary hot dog I’d been curious about.<br>Also ended up buying Muscle Coffee from the “Muscle Guy” vendor—<br>his sheer energy and biceps left me defenseless. Respect.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Day_2_Lost_in_Blog_Troubles"></span>Day 2: Lost in Blog Troubles<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="1024" src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2057" srcset="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg 1024w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg 300w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg 150w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg 768w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_38_41.511Z.jpg 376w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<p>By day two, the combination of car sleeping and bad weather had worn me down.<br>Still, I pushed myself to the venue. The crowd was as big as day one, but I was a little less tense.<br>…Yet, the socially awkward, 50-something me was still very much alone.</p>



<p>This day was all about visiting the official blogging booth.<br>I wrote my troubles on a card:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p>“I started blogging as a 50-something challenge, aiming at my fellow ‘Dankai Junior’ generation.<br>I wrote about running, daily reflections, and life with my Shiba Inu.<br>Then I tried Yahoo! Japan trend articles, but lost my direction.<br>After multiple rejections from Google AdSense, I gave up and switched to A8.net.<br>Now I mostly write product-focused posts and no longer know what I truly want to write.<br>I also write English versions for overseas readers… but I’m not sure if I’m on the right path.”</p>
</blockquote>



<p>The advisor’s response was a sharp but simple question:<br>“What are you good at?”</p>



<p>…Nothing.<br>Well, maybe drinking beer and zoning out (not helpful).</p>



<p>The advice:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Keep the personal blog,</li>



<li>Create a separate, specialized blog (ideally tied to your work),</li>



<li>Decide on a goal and make a “startup list,”</li>



<li>And build the English blog on a separate site.</li>
</ul>



<p>Made sense.</p>



<p>That night, I grabbed curry in the rain, soaked in a hot spring,<br>and prepared for yet another night of car camping.<br>The middle-aged youth saga continued.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Day_3_A_Miracle_Connection"></span>Day 3: A Miracle Connection<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="1024" src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2058" srcset="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg 1024w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg 300w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg 150w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg 768w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Google_AI_Studio_2025-08-12T03_43_09.746Z.jpg 376w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<p>Final day.<br>“Will I actually talk to anyone today…?” I wondered.</p>



<p>Then—it happened.<br>A man my age from Asahikawa, Hokkaido, sitting next to me at the official stage,<br>started talking to me.<br>Turns out, the whole row was full of people my age.<br>Suddenly, my social circle exploded.</p>



<p>I was given candy, got follow-backs on social media…<br>My 50-year-old heart was <em>warm</em>.</p>



<p>In the afternoon, I had a writing consultation booked.<br>Trouble is, I didn’t even know the basics of writing structure,<br>so I ended up confusing the poor advisor.</p>



<p>I could hear what she was saying, but my 50-year-old brain lagged behind.<br>Still—don’t underestimate a 50-year-old brain.<br>It’s no joke, just like Amuro’s famous “Gundam is no joke!” line.<br>This experience will come in handy… someday.</p>



<p>Later, while waiting in line for another blog consultation,<br>a woman struck up a conversation.<br><em>The goddess descends.</em><br>I kept a straight face, but inside, I was in tears of gratitude.</p>



<p>A man soon joined us, and the three of us dove deep into blog talk.<br>This—<em>this</em>—was the connection I had hoped for.</p>



<p>We then teamed up for the big networking event.<br>It felt like venturing into a massive dungeon as a party.<br>On my own, I’d never have dared. But with them, I could.<br>I talked to so many people, and finally—finally—fulfilled my goal.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Closing_Still_Got_It_at_50"></span>Closing: Still Got It at 50<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-large"><img decoding="async" width="769" height="1024" src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2056" srcset="/images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 769w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 225w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 768w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 1153w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 1538w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 376w, /images/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PXL_20250812_031234383.MP_.jpg 1922w" sizes="(max-width: 769px) 100vw, 769px" /></figure>



<p>My back was wrecked, but my heart was full.<br>From zero connections to a complete turnaround.<br>Even if you’re shy, even if you’re alone, even if you’re in your 50s—<br>you can still bloom.</p>



<p>So to you, reading this right now:<br><em>A middle-aged guy can still go for it.</em></p>



<p>Thank you, community leader.<br>Thank you to everyone I met at the fest.<br>My youth? It’s not over yet.</p>
