<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>People who find themselves muttering “That’s expensive…” at the supermarket</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Those in their 50s or 60s who feel a quiet anxiety when thinking about pensions and retirement</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>People who save money, but aren’t sure savings alone will be enough</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Conclusion</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Keep cash for money you’ll use soon.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> Invest money you won’t need for a while.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> Start preparing—slowly, calmly—so inflation doesn’t quietly steal your future.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Everything feels more expensive lately</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2997,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_2ox0oq2ox0oq2ox0.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2997"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Honestly, everything feels more expensive these days.</strong><strong><br></strong> At the supermarket. At the gas station. On the electricity bill.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I often find myself thinking,<br>“Was it really this expensive before?”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The news says, <em>“Wages are rising.”</em><em><br></em> But to be honest, <strong>prices feel like they’re rising faster than income.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I’m pretty sure I’m not the only one who feels this way.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Discount stickers—and my own quiet frugality</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2998,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_2wkj752wkj752wkj.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2998"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The other day at the supermarket,<br>I noticed an elderly person patiently waiting for discount stickers to appear.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There’s nothing wrong with that.<br>It’s smart shopping—and honestly, <strong>I grab those stickers too when I see them.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Actually…<br>when a staff member appears with the sticker machine,<br><strong>I unconsciously take half a step closer.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If our eyes meet, I even nod politely.<br>What is that reflex? Muscle memory? (laughs)</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But somehow, watching that scene made my chest feel a little tight.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>“So this is a world where older people have to be this careful, every single day.”</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That thought lingered.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Inflation hits older people harder</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":2999,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_e4p19qe4p19qe4p1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-2999"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Recently, while watching videos about personal finance,<br>something finally clicked.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The news might say <em>“Inflation is 2%,”</em><em><br></em> but <strong>older people often feel a much higher rate in real life.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Why? It’s simple:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Medical expenses</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Fresh food</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Daily necessities</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These are exactly the things that tend to rise first—and rise fast.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For people living mainly on pensions,<br>this is genuinely tough.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Pensions don’t suddenly increase just because prices go up.</strong><strong><br></strong> So prices rise, but purchasing power quietly shrinks.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No wonder people freeze in front of supermarket shelves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Japan I remember as a child</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3000,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_zaeezfzaeezfzaee.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3000"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thinking about this,<br>I suddenly remembered Japan when I was a kid.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not everyone was rich.<br>We didn’t have endless things.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But the streets felt alive.<br>Adults and kids laughed more.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Back then, Japan was seen as a strong, wealthy country.<br>And honestly, <strong>I thought that was just normal.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Japan felt unstoppable.<br>I never questioned it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Japan is a country that once stood up from the bottom</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3001,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_49g1js49g1js49g1.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3001"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But Japan also hit rock bottom after World War II.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>From burned-out cities,<br>people said, <em>“Let’s rebuild.”</em><em><br></em> And somehow, they did.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s often called a miracle,<br>but I think it was something else:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>When people share the same sense of crisis,</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> their quiet strength comes out.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Japanese people may not push forward loudly.<br>But when it truly matters,<br><strong>their persistence is remarkable.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Maybe the switch just hasn’t fully flipped yet.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Don’t wait for the country—start with your own household</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3002,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_bu8kjdbu8kjdbu8k.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3002"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Still, I don’t think we can just wait for the government to fix everything.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>We have to start by rebuilding our own household finances.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When people hear the word <em>“investing,”</em><em><br></em> many think, <em>“It’s scary. I might lose money.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I felt that way too.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But now I see it differently.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Investing isn’t about getting rich fast.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> It’s about protecting what you already have.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Keeping all your money only in cash<br>might be like <strong>putting all your eggs in one basket—Japan’s basket.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>My simple answer</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3003,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_nz23ftnz23ftnz23.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3003"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So this is how I think about it now:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Cash for money I’ll use soon</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Index investing for money I won’t need for a while</strong><strong><br></strong></li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No big bets.<br>No dramatic moves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Just one goal:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Not becoming poorer without realizing it because of inflation.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At night, lying in bed,<br>being able to think, <em>“It’ll probably be okay.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For me, <strong>that peace of mind matters more than anything.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Final thoughts</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":3004,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Gemini_Generated_Image_8880id8880id8880.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3004"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No one really knows what Japan’s future will look like.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But if more people stop drowning in anxiety<br>and <strong>focus on their own footing, living each day steadily,</strong><strong><br></strong> the atmosphere might slowly change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Today, I still stand in front of supermarket price tags.<br>And I keep moving forward anyway.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That feels just right.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After all…<br><strong>I’m in my 50s.</strong><strong><br></strong><strong> What do I know? (laughs)</strong></p>
