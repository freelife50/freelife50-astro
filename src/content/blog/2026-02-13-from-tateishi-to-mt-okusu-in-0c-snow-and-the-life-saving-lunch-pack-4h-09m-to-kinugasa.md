<p><strong>Introduction</strong></p>

<p>You know those mornings when it’s just cold.</p>

<p>The kind of day when simply stepping outside takes a bit of determination.</p>

<p>Plans don’t move just because you hesitate.<br>You stand at the door thinking,</p>

<p>“Should I go… or maybe not?”</p>

<p>That was exactly me that day.</p>

<p>Cloudy skies.<br>Light snow falling.<br>0°C.</p>

<p>The moment I reached the bus stop, I honestly thought,</p>

<p>“Am I really walking in this kind of weather?”</p>

<p>But in the end, I’m glad I did.</p>

<p>There were no dramatic views. Mt. Fuji never appeared.<br>Still, what remained with me were the river, the flowers, the birds, the wind —<br>and a Lunch Pack.</p>

<p>This article is a complete record of the day, including timestamps, distance markers, and pacing.<br>It captures what it actually feels like to walk this route from Tateishi to Kinugasa.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Route Overview &amp; Pace (Actual Log: 08:23–12:32)</h2>

<p>Before the main story,<br>here is a numerical overview of the route.</p>

<p><em>Based entirely on actual logs.</em></p>

<p><strong>Course</strong></p>

<p>Tateishi Bus Stop<br>→ Maedagawa Promenade<br>→ Mt. Okusu<br>→ Kinugasa Castle Ruins / Daizenji<br>→ Kinugasa-yama Park<br>→ Kinugasa Station<br>(Yokosuka / Miura Peninsula crossing route)</p>

<p><strong>Total Distance</strong></p>

<p>Approx. 11.5 km<br>(including detours and summit wandering)</p>

<p><strong>Total Time</strong></p>

<p>4 hours 09 minutes<br>(including breaks, lunch, and photos)</p>

<p><strong>Moving Time</strong></p>

<p>Approx. 3 hours 30 minutes</p>

<p><strong>Average Pace</strong></p>

<p>Standard to slightly fast<br>(Smooth on flat and downhill, steady on climbs)</p>

<p><strong>Cumulative Elevation Gain</strong></p>

<p>Up: approx. 480m / Down: approx. 470m<br>(Mt. Okusu 242m + Kinugasa-yama 134m + rolling terrain)</p>

<p>Time for lunch.</p>

<p>Except — I had forgotten mine.</p>

<p>“I’ll eat after descending,” I had thought.</p>

<p>Careless middle-aged decision.</p>

<p>Then a friend quietly handed me a peanut-filled Lunch Pack.</p>

<p>He didn’t have much extra either.</p>

<p>Just convenience store bread.</p>

<p>But at that moment,<br>it tasted deeply of kindness.</p>

<p>Truly, thank you.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Final Thoughts</h2>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Gemini_Generated_Image_a6m72ca6m72ca6m7.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-3191"/></figure>

<p>0°C. Light snow.</p>

<p>I hesitated.</p>

<p>But walking warmed my body.</p>

<p>The clear stream of Maedagawa.<br>The white heron.<br>Cherry blossoms and daffodils.<br>The wild boar warning sign.<br>The animals at Arinkurin.<br>The quiet weight of Daizenji.</p>

<p>And the Lunch Pack in the wind and snow.</p>

<p>Life might be similar.</p>

<p>Perfect timing rarely comes.<br>Weather, mood, physical condition — rarely aligned.</p>

<p>But perhaps only the days we move, even while hesitating,<br>remain in memory.</p>

<p>And the warmest thing that day?</p>

<p>Not the scenery.</p>

<p>It was the kindness of a friend.</p>

<p>If you’re hesitating because of something small — like cold weather —</p>

<p>You don’t have to push yourself too hard.</p>

<p>But maybe just one small step.</p>

<p>Well… that’s how it felt.</p>

<p>And yes — that Lunch Pack truly saved me.</p>
