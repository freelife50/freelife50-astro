<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why the Bill Fluctuates</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/iijmio-plan-comparison.jpg" alt="IIJmio plan options and pricing — how monthly bills shift based on data plan changes"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The reason isn't random. It comes down to one variable: <strong>which large-data plan we choose each month.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Here's our family's setup:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>5GB plan × 3 people ($6.63/person/month)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Large-data plan × 1 person (switched between 25GB and 35GB depending on the month)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:table -->
<figure class="wp-block-table"><table><thead><tr><th>Plan</th><th>Monthly cost (USD)</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>5GB plan</td><td>$6.63</td></tr><tr><td>25GB plan</td><td>$13.40</td></tr><tr><td>35GB plan</td><td>$16.11</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
<!-- /wp:table -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The base for 3 people on 5GB is about $19.89/month. The one high-usage person accounts for the remaining $13–$16, depending on the month.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When they switch up to the 35GB plan, the bill jumps by about $2.71. That's part of the "mystery gap" explained.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The other variable is <strong>voice call charges</strong>. IIJmio bills calls separately at ¥11 per 30 seconds. Months with more phone calls — doctor's offices, government offices — show up clearly in the bill.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Think of it like a utility bill — it varies a little, but it never spikes unpredictably. Once I stopped thinking "what's the monthly number?" and started thinking "what's the annual number?", the anxiety disappeared.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Quiet Features I Didn't Expect to Love</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/iijmio-data-share-feature.jpg" alt="IIJmio data sharing and rollover feature visualization"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before switching, I had a nagging worry: "What if budget SIM turns out to be inconvenient?"</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After 12 months, I can say: the inconveniences I feared never showed up. What did show up were features I hadn't expected to appreciate.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Data rollover</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Unused data carries over to the next month automatically. No more frantically watching YouTube videos at the end of the month to "use up" what's left. Leftover data just becomes a head start for next month.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Data sharing</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>All four family members share a data pool. At the start of each month, we have roughly 70GB available across the family. If one person uses more, another person's leftover naturally covers the gap.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Multi-line discount (mio discount)</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When multiple lines are on the same account, a discount kicks in. The more family members you bring over, the more each line benefits.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How We Actually Run It Month to Month</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/iijmio-plan-comparison.jpg" alt="Checking and updating IIJmio plan settings on a smartphone at month-end"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Nothing fancy. Just three habits that take about two minutes a month.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Check the plan at the end of the month</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The heaviest data user in the family looks at their usage and decides whether next month calls for 25GB or 35GB. If there's a trip or a busy stretch coming, go up. If it's a quiet, stay-at-home month, drop down. That one decision saves or costs about $4.70.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Keep everyone on data share</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>All four lines stay linked in the same data pool. Every month starts with ~70GB available. No manual management needed — it just works.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Ignore the rollover counter</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Unused data rolls over automatically. I never check how much carried over. It's just a buffer that quietly helps. That's the part I appreciate most — one less thing to think about.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Phone Bills Look Like in Your 50s</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="/images/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/iijmio-summary.jpg" alt="Thinking about household fixed costs and long-term savings strategy in your 50s"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Fixed costs are the best kind of savings because they compound without effort.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cutting $10 from your grocery budget requires discipline every single week. Switching phone plans requires effort once — and then it just keeps saving.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In my 50s, I've come to prefer that kind of quiet, structural saving over white-knuckle budgeting.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The $1,000+ we save each year can go toward NISA, iDeCo, or just a little more breathing room. A few years of that compounds into something meaningful.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before chasing high-return investments, fix your fixed costs. It's unsexy advice. But it works.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
